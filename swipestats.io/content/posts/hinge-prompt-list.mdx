## How Hinge Prompts Actually Work (So You Can Stop Guessing)

Before we dump the full list on you, let's talk mechanics. Because I've seen people waste good prompts with bad strategy, and it's painful to watch.

Here's what you get to work with on your Hinge profile:

- **3 written prompts** displayed on your profile, each with a **150-character limit**. That's roughly one tweet from 2015. Be concise or be boring.
- **1 voice prompt** (30 seconds). Your actual voice. Terrifying for some of you, but conversations that start from voice notes are 41% more likely to become dates.
- **1 video prompt** (30 seconds). Yes, you have to be on camera. No, you can't use a filter. The 50% engagement boost is worth the discomfort.
- **1 prompt poll**. Let people vote on something. It's low-effort interaction that still counts as a conversation starter.

You can change your prompts anytime. This is important because it means you can test what works and swap out the duds. Think of it as A/B testing your personality.

The real power move? People can **like** OR **comment** on your prompts. And comments are where the magic happens. A like is a head nod across the room. A comment is someone walking up and starting a conversation. Write prompts that give people something to respond to, not something to scroll past.

## The 10 Best Hinge Prompts (According to Actual Data, Not Your Gut)

Alright, enough browsing. Let's talk about which hinge prompts actually get results. Hinge published data (via Mashable) on the prompts that most frequently lead to real conversations. Not just likes. Conversations. The kind that turn into dates where you have to wear real pants.

Here are the top 10:

1. **The way to win me over is** - Works because it's basically an instruction manual for your match. You're telling them exactly how to impress you. It's generous and specific.
2. **My simple pleasures** - Reveals what makes you happy without being pretentious. "A perfect espresso and a Sunday crossword" says more about you than "I love to travel."
3. **I go crazy for** - Shows passion. Passion is attractive. Writing "perfectly crispy fries" makes you more human than "I value authenticity."
4. **Together, we could** - This is future-pacing. You're painting a picture of what a date (or life) with you looks like. It's an invitation, not a resume bullet.
5. **My most irrational fear** - Vulnerability plus humor. "Squirrels with too much confidence" is both honest and conversation-starting.
6. **We'll get along if** - Pre-qualifies your match in a playful way. It tells someone whether they should bother, which is oddly attractive.
7. **I'm looking for** - Direct and efficient. But only if you write something specific. "Someone who argues about which Miyazaki film is the best" beats "my person" every single time.
8. **This year, I really want to** - Forward-looking. Shows ambition without being a LinkedIn post. Keep it fun and achievable.
9. **Typical Sunday** - The classic. It works because Sundays are revealing. Are you a farmer's market person or a "don't talk to me until 2 PM" person? Both are valid. Both attract different people.
10. **A life goal of mine** - Shows depth without getting heavy. The trick is picking a goal that's interesting, not intimidating. "Learn to make pasta from scratch in Italy" hits different than "become a millionaire by 30."

Notice the pattern? Every single one of these best hinge prompts invites a response. They give your match something to react to, agree with, or challenge. That's not an accident. And if you're looking for funny hinge prompts specifically, almost any prompt on this list can be hilarious with the right answer (more on that below).

## Hinge Prompts That Make People Swipe Left (Stop Using These)

Time for some tough love. If your profile has any of the following, I need you to open the app and fix it before you finish reading this article. I'm serious. I'll wait.

**"I'm looking for" + anything vague.** "Someone who doesn't take themselves too seriously" is on roughly 40% of profiles. I made that number up but you know I'm right. It says absolutely nothing. What does that even mean? You want a clown? Be specific or don't bother.

**"Typical Sunday" + "brunch and Netflix."** Congratulations, you've described every human being on earth. You might as well write "I breathe oxygen and occasionally eat food." [Your Hinge profile](/blog/best-hinge-profiles-for-guys) deserves better than this.

**One-word answers to any prompt.** "Pizza." "Dogs." "Traveling." Cool. You've used 7 of your 150 characters. You have the communication skills of a stop sign. If you can't write a sentence, how are you going to hold a conversation?

**The Office references.** "Looking for the Pam to my Jim" was cute in 2018. It's 2026. The show ended over a decade ago. Find a new personality trait. I'm begging you.

**"I'm fluent in sarcasm."** No you're not. You're fluent in being too lazy to write something original. This is the Nickelback of prompt answers. Everyone pretends to hate it but somehow it keeps showing up.

**"Just ask!"** Ask what? You've given me nothing to work with. This is the prompt equivalent of showing up to a potluck empty-handed and telling people to guess what you would have brought.

## How to Pick Your 3 Hinge Prompts (Without Overthinking It)

You've seen the full list. You've seen what works and what makes people want to throw their phone into the ocean. Now let's build your actual prompt lineup.

**The formula is simple: humor + vulnerability + specificity.**

Pick one prompt that makes someone laugh. Pick one that shows depth. Pick one that's so specific it could only be about you. If all three do at least two of those things, you're golden.

**Pull from different categories.** Three prompts from "About Me" makes you look self-obsessed. Mix it up. One from "Let's Chat About," one from "My Type," one from "Storytime." Variety shows range.

**Write answers that create openings.** Your prompt answer should make someone think "oh, I have to respond to that." If your answer is a closed statement with nowhere to go, you've wasted the slot. "I go crazy for a good debate about whether a hot dog is a sandwich" gives someone a reason to message you. "I go crazy for food" does not.

**Use every character wisely.** You've got 150. That's not a lot. Don't waste them on filler words or generic statements. Every word should earn its spot. Trim the fat. Then trim it again.

**Test and rotate.** Hinge lets you change your prompts anytime. If a prompt isn't generating likes or comments after a week, swap it out. Your profile is a living document, not a tattoo. Unless you're the type to get impulsive tattoos, in which case, that's probably good prompt material.

## Good Hinge Prompt Answers: What Separates the Matches from the Misses

Reading about [good hinge prompt answers](/blog/tinder-prompt-answers) is one thing. Seeing the difference is another. Let me break this down so it sticks.

**Bad answer to "I go crazy for":** "good vibes"

**Good answer to "I go crazy for":** "someone who can beat me at Mario Kart and isn't smug about it (impossible)"

See what happened there? The bad answer could apply to literally every person alive. The good answer is specific, playful, and gives someone a reason to message you ("I will absolutely destroy you at Rainbow Road"). That's the whole game.

**Bad answer to "The way to win me over is":** "be yourself"

**Good answer:** "show up with a controversial take on the best Pixar movie and be willing to die on that hill"

One is a bumper sticker. The other is a conversation waiting to happen.

Your prompts need to do three things: reveal something real about you, be specific enough that a stranger can picture it, and leave a door open for someone to walk through with a comment. If your answer does all three in under 150 characters, you've cracked the code.

## FAQ

### How many prompts does Hinge have?

Hinge has **over 105 prompts** spread across 10 categories. They update the list periodically, so new ones pop up without warning. Think of it as a menu that keeps expanding while you still can't decide what to order.

### How many prompts can you have on your Hinge profile?

You get **3 written prompts**, plus **1 voice prompt** (30 seconds), **1 video prompt** (30 seconds), and **1 prompt poll**. That's 6 total touchpoints. Most people only use the 3 written ones and leave free engagement on the table like leaving money in the street.

### What is the character limit for Hinge prompts?

**150 characters** for written prompts. That's shorter than this answer. Voice prompts and video prompts get 30 seconds each. Brevity is your friend. If you can't say it in 150 characters, you're overcomplicating it.

### Can you change your Hinge prompts?

Yes. Anytime you want. There's no cooldown, no penalty, no limit. If something isn't working, swap it out. If you matched with someone amazing and want to refresh your profile, go for it. Flexibility is one of Hinge's [better features](/blog/hinge-x-vs-hinge-plus).

### What are voice prompts on Hinge?

30-second audio clips where you answer a prompt with your actual voice. They're underused and underrated. Conversations that start from voice notes are **41% more likely to lead to a date**. Your voice conveys tone, humor, and warmth that text simply can't. If you sound like a normal human being (low bar, I know), use one.

### Why did my Hinge match disappear after I liked their prompt?

That's a whole other problem. They either unmatched you, deleted their account, or got [shadowbanned](/blog/hinge-shadowban). If your [matches keep disappearing](/blog/hinge-match-disappeared), it's not your prompts. It's the app being the app.
