## The 5 Buttons Your Thumbs Already Know (But Your Brain Doesn't)

Let's start with the obvious stuff. The row of colorful circles at the bottom of your screen. You've been mashing them like a toddler with an iPad for months, so you might as well know what they do.

**Red X (Nope):** Swipe left. You're not interested. This person will never know you rejected them, which is honestly the kindest thing Tinder does. Free for everyone, because rejection should always be accessible.

**Green Heart (Like):** Swipe right. You want to match with this person. [Free users get limited likes per day](/blog/when-do-tinder-likes-reset) because Tinder knows you're too broke to afford the premium versions. Premium users get unlimited likes, which just means unlimited opportunities to be ignored.

**Blue Star (Super Like):** The digital equivalent of running across a crowded bar to tap someone on the shoulder and say "YOU. I CHOOSE YOU." like they're a Pokemon. Free users get one per week. Does it work? Super Likes do increase your match rate significantly. Does it make you look a little desperate? Almost always. Use wisely.

**Yellow Arrow (Rewind):** The "oh shit" button. Accidentally swiped left on someone hot? Rewind lets you undo it. This one's locked behind Tinder Plus or higher, because regretting your decisions should cost money.

**Purple Lightning Bolt (Boost):** Puts your profile at the top of the stack for 30 minutes. It's like cutting the line at a club, except the bouncer is an algorithm, and the club is full of people judging you silently on their phones. Premium feature. Worth using during peak evening hours when the most people are swiping.

That's it. Five buttons. If you needed this explained, I have concerns about how you're navigating the rest of your life.

## Tinder Signs Next to Names: The Cryptic Badges That Keep You Guessing

This is where every Tinder badge meaning starts to blur together. Confusing enough that you're Googling "tinder icons meaning" at 11pm on a Tuesday instead of actually talking to your matches.

### Blue Checkmark: "I Am a Real Human Person"

The blue checkmark means they've passed Tinder's Face Check verification. As of 2026, this is mandatory for all new users. You take a video selfie, Tinder's AI compares it to your photos, and if you're not a catfish, you get the badge.

Since rollout, Match Group reports a **60% drop in suspicious profiles** and **40% fewer reports**. That's genuinely impressive. It doesn't guarantee they'll look like their photos after three margaritas at 1am (filters and angles are still legal), but at least you know an actual person is behind the screen.

If someone doesn't have the checkmark, they probably created their account before it was mandatory. Or they're a bot. Your call.

### Gold Heart: "Someone Likes You But We Blurred Their Face Like a Crime Show"

This is Tinder's masterclass in psychological manipulation. That gold heart notification tells you someone liked your profile. Exciting, right? Except their photo is blurred into a pixelated smudge that could be literally anyone. Your soulmate. Your ex. Your landlord.

Want to see who it is? Pay for [Tinder Gold](/blog/is-tinder-gold-worth-it). It's digital extortion with a heart-shaped logo. And it works. Millions of people pay up every month because curiosity is a hell of a drug.

### Gold Diamond: Top Picks

The gold diamond badge appears on profiles Tinder's algorithm has selected as your "Top Picks" for the day. Think of it as Tinder playing matchmaker, except the matchmaker is a machine learning model trained on the swiping habits of millions of equally confused people.

Free users can see their Top Picks but can only like one per day. [Gold and Platinum subscribers](/blog/tinder-plus-vs-tinder-gold) get more. Because of course they do.

### Green Dot: "This Person Is Also Sad and Online Right Now"

The green dot means someone was active on Tinder within the last 24 hours. It's only visible to Gold and Platinum users, because knowing someone's online status is apparently a luxury feature.

Here's the thing. If someone is on Tinder, they're probably active. That's how phones work. You don't need a green dot to tell you that the person who literally has a dating app installed on their phone might be using it. But sure, pay $29.99 a month for that earth-shattering intel.

### Blue Star Badge: "Someone Super Liked You"

When you see a blue star border around a profile, someone spent their precious Super Like on you. This is the notification version of someone passing you a note in class that says "I like you. Do you like me? Circle yes or no."

It's flattering. It also means this person either really likes you or Super Likes literally everyone, which kind of defeats the purpose. Either way, give them extra consideration. They're trying.

### Red Dot: "Something Happened, Probably"

The generic red notification dot. Could be a new match. Could be a new message. Could be Tinder reminding you that you exist and should please open the app again. It's the least useful indicator on the platform, which is saying something when your competition is a blurred gold heart.

## Tinder Signs in Your Messages (Because Reading the Room Is Hard Enough IRL)

Your matches are just the beginning of the confusion. The chat screen has its own collection of tiny icons designed to make you question everything.

### Blue Camera Icon: Video Chat

Both you and your match need to enable the video chat feature for this to work. It's Tinder's attempt to bring face-to-face interaction back to a platform built entirely on avoiding face-to-face interaction. Perfect for confirming your match isn't a catfish, or for realizing that you have absolutely nothing to talk about in real time.

### Double Blue Checkmarks: Read Receipts

Like WhatsApp's blue ticks, but you have to pay for them. These tell you whether your match has read your message. Which means you'll now know with certainty that they saw your "Hey, how's your week going?" and chose silence. Congratulations. That information cost you money.

This is a premium add-on, sold in packs. Because Tinder figured out they could monetize your anxiety. Brilliant business model. Terrible for your mental health.

### Blue Shield: Report or Unmatch

The safety icon. Tap it to report someone for bad behavior or to unmatch them entirely. If you're wondering why your [match disappeared](/blog/tinder-match-disappeared), someone might have used this button on you. Take that information however you'd like.

### Dice Icon: "I Can't Think of an Opener"

The random opener generator. Tinder gives you a pre-written conversation starter because it knows you were just going to type "hey" anyway. It's like those conversation cards you buy for awkward dinner parties, except the dinner party is your love life and everyone's on their phone.

Honestly? It's not the worst feature. Using a slightly lame generated opener is still better than the conversational equivalent of a limp handshake. But if you want to actually stand out, maybe put in the effort to write something yourself.

### Heart Reaction on Messages

You can react to specific messages with a heart. It's the "I acknowledge what you said but don't have a real response" button. Functionally identical to the thumbs-up reaction on iMessage that passive-aggressive people use to end conversations. Use sparingly or your match will think you've forgotten how words work.

## The 2026 Tinder Signs You Haven't Seen Yet

Tinder dropped a ton of new features at the Sparks 2026 keynote on March 12th. Some of these are rolling out right now. Others are coming soon. All of them add new icons to an already cluttered interface.

### Astrology Mode: Sun, Moon, and Rising Signs

Tinder now lets you add your sun, moon, and rising signs to your profile. Small zodiac icons appear next to your name. During testing, women sent roughly **20% more likes** to profiles with astrology signs visible. So whether you believe Mercury retrograde is responsible for your ex texting you, the data says astrology info gets more engagement.

I personally think horoscopes are nonsense. But I also know that "what's your sign" has been a pickup line since before Tinder existed, so who am I to argue with tradition? If adding "Scorpio sun, Pisces moon" to your profile gets you more matches, embrace the cosmic clown show.

### Music Mode Redesign: Spotify Integration

Tinder revamped its Spotify integration. You can now add an "anthem" and top artists to your profile, and a small music note icon indicates you've opted in. About **1 in 10 users under 22** adopted this feature during initial rollout.

If your anthem is anything by The Chainsmokers, I need you to understand that this is actively hurting your chances with anyone who has ears.

### Face Check Badge: Mandatory Verification

I mentioned this above. But this is the biggest change Tinder has made in years. The blue checkmark is now required for all new accounts. The single best safety feature they've shipped. You'll see this badge everywhere now. If someone doesn't have it, they're grandfathered in from a pre-verification era. Proceed with appropriate suspicion.

### Chemistry: AI-Curated Daily Matches

A new purple flask icon appears on profiles Tinder's AI has specially selected for you based on conversation patterns, not just swipe behavior. It's like the algorithm watched you fumble through enough bad conversations to finally understand your type. Early days on this one. Could be useful. Could be another gimmick to make you open the app more. Probably both.

### College Mode: Tinder U Badges

If you're in college, Tinder U badges now show your graduation year, major, and Greek life affiliation. A small graduation cap icon appears on qualifying profiles.

This is either incredibly useful for finding people in your campus orbit, or it's going to make the "so what's your major" conversation even more redundant than it already is. For everyone over 25 reading this: this feature is not for you. Let it go.

### Camera Roll Scan and Photo Insights

Tinder can now scan your camera roll and suggest which photos would perform best on your profile. A small AI sparkle icon appears when using this feature. It's like having a brutally honest friend who tells you that the fish pic isn't working, except the friend is a neural network and it's seen millions of profile photos.

### Tinder Connect: Third-Party Badges

New integration badges are appearing on profiles. A Duolingo badge means they're learning a language (or they opened Duolingo once six months ago and forgot about it, like the rest of us). A Beli badge means they have restaurant preferences linked. These are small, optional icons that add context without requiring you to actually read someone's bio.

## The Premium Decoder Ring (AKA Why Half Your Icons Are Greyed Out)

Here's the part nobody else wants to explain clearly. You've noticed that some icons on your screen are greyed out, faded, or have a little lock on them. That's not a bug. That's a business model.

Tinder has four tiers. Here's what each one unlocks.

| Feature | Free | Plus | Gold | Platinum |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Basic Swipes | Limited daily | Unlimited | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Super Likes | 1/week | 5/week | 5/week | 5/week |
| Rewind (Yellow Arrow) | No | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Boost (Purple Lightning) | No | 1/month | 1/month | 1/month |
| Passport (Location Change) | No | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| See Who Liked You (Gold Heart) | Blurred | Blurred | Yes | Yes |
| Top Picks (Gold Diamond) | 1/day | 1/day | More | More |

Every greyed-out icon is Tinder saying "I see you looking at this feature. Wouldn't it be nice if you could actually use it? That'll be $9.99 to $39.99 a month, depending on how desperate we think you are."

And look, paying for Tinder is worth it if you're a guy who wants results. Our data from **7,000+ profiles** and **294 million swipes** shows that premium users get significantly more visibility. The [average match rate for men is around 2.5%](/blog/tinder-statistics). Every edge helps.

But no premium tier can fix a bad profile. That's like putting a spoiler on a broken-down car. If your photos look like Bigfoot sightings and your bio is "just ask," fix that first. Then throw money at the problem.

## What Happened to Tinder Insights? (RIP, You Beautiful Nerd Tool)

If you're here because you Googled "tinder insights" hoping to find that data visualization tool everyone used to talk about, I've got bad news.

**tinderinsights.com is dead.** The domain is parked by MarkMonitor (a domain holding company). The SSL certificate expired in 2022. Nobody's home. The lights are off. The building has been condemned.

### What It Was

Tinder Insights was a free third-party tool where you could upload your Tinder data export and get charts and graphs about your swiping behavior. Match rates, message patterns, peak activity times. It was genuinely useful for understanding your dating patterns. People loved it.

Then it just... stopped working. No announcement. No farewell post. It just quietly died, like a match conversation where both people stopped responding.

### Why People Still Search for It

Because it was good. And because nobody told anyone it was gone. If you search "tinder insights" right now, you'll find old Reddit threads, outdated blog posts, and broken links. The internet has a long memory and a short attention span.

## FAQ: The Questions You're Too Embarrassed to Ask Out Loud

### What does the gold heart mean on Tinder?

It means someone liked your profile. But Tinder blurs their photo so you can't see who it is unless you pay for Tinder Gold ($29.99/month). It's the dating app equivalent of someone slipping a note under your door that says "someone in this building has a crush on you." Helpful and infuriating in equal measure.

### What does the green dot mean on Tinder?

The green dot means that person was active on Tinder within the last 24 hours. It's only visible to Gold and Platinum subscribers. If you can see the green dot, you're paying at least $29.99/month for the privilege of knowing someone else is also swiping at 2am. Welcome to the club.

### Can you add your zodiac sign on Tinder?

Yes. As of the Tinder Sparks 2026 update, you can add your sun, moon, and rising signs. Small zodiac icons appear on your profile. During testing, profiles with astrology signs saw about a **20% increase in likes from women**. Science can't explain it. Astrology Twitter will try.

### Is Tinder Insights still working?

No. The site (tinderinsights.com) has been dead since around 2022. The domain is parked, and the SSL certificate expired. If you want similar (and honestly better) functionality, upload your data to SwipeStats. We'll give you charts, comparisons, and the cold hard truth about your swiping habits.

### What do the greyed-out icons on Tinder mean?

They're premium features you haven't paid for yet. The rewind arrow, the boost lightning bolt, the ability to see who liked you. Tinder shows them to you in a faded, locked state as a constant reminder that you're on the free tier. It's a sales tactic. A very effective one, based on Tinder's revenue numbers.

### What does the blue star mean on Tinder?

Two things. If you see a blue star on the button bar, that's the Super Like button. If you see a blue star border around someone's profile in your stack, that means they Super Liked you. Either way, someone is making a bold move. Respect the energy, even if you swipe left.

### What's the difference between the blue checkmark and the gold diamond?

The blue checkmark means verified identity (Face Check). The gold diamond means Tinder selected them as one of your Top Picks for the day. One is about trust. The other is the algorithm playing favorites. Both are useful. Neither guarantees they'll be good at conversation.
