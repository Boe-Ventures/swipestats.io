## How Much Does Tinder Plus Actually Cost in 2026?

Let's get the Tinder Plus price question out of the way, because Google brought you here and I respect your time (sort of).

The honest answer? Nobody knows. Not even Tinder. The company treats pricing like a state secret, adjusting it based on your age, location, device, and probably your astrological sign. Two people sitting next to each other on the same couch can open Tinder and see completely different prices. It's like Uber surge pricing, except instead of getting to the airport, you're paying for the privilege of unlimited right-swipes on people who will never message you back.

Here's what we've been able to piece together from multiple sources in early 2026:

| Plan Duration | Tinder Plus Price (approx.) |
|---|---|
| 1 week | ~$12.99 |
| 1 month | ~$17.99 - $24.99 |
| 6 months | ~$9 - $16.66/mo |
| 12 months | ~$6 - $9/mo |

DatingScout reported Plus at **$17.99/month** for a single month as of February 2026. Android Authority had it at **$24.99/month** in late 2025. The discrepancy isn't an error. It's Tinder being Tinder.

The key takeaway: if your friend says they're paying $9/month and you're paying $25, you're not getting ripped off. Well, you are. But so is everyone else. Just differently.

If you're under 30, you'll likely see lower prices. If you're over 30, Tinder assumes you're more desperate and charges accordingly. (More on that particular dumpster fire in section 3.)

## The Full Tinder Subscription Lineup: Plus vs Gold vs Platinum vs "I Have Too Much Money"

Tinder now has four subscription tiers, because apparently three ways to separate you from your money wasn't enough. Let me walk you through all of them so you can make an informed decision about which level of financial regret suits you best.

### Tinder Plus Features

Plus is the entry-level premium tier. Think of it as the Honda Civic of Tinder subscriptions. Gets the job done, nothing fancy.

- **Unlimited Likes** so you can swipe right on everyone without Tinder putting you in time-out every few hours
- **Passport** to change your location and swipe anywhere in the world (great for travelers, questionable for people pretending to live in Miami)
- **Rewind** your last swipe, for when your thumb betrays you and swipes left on someone actually attractive
- **1 Free Boost per month** that puts you at the top of the stack for 30 minutes. This is actually the most valuable feature and I'm not exaggerating.
- **5 Super Likes** to tell someone "I'm REALLY interested" (read: "I'm REALLY desperate")
- **Hide your age and distance** because sometimes mystery is all you've got
- **No ads** so you can swipe in peace without seeing promotions for local singles in your area (wait, wrong app)

### Tinder Gold (Everything in Plus, Plus More)

Gold is where things get interesting. Or at least, where Tinder starts dangling the features that actually matter.

- **See Who Likes You** unlocks that blurry grid of faces that Tinder has been teasing you with since day one. Now you can see exactly who swiped right on you before you decide. It's like peeking at your Christmas presents early, except the presents are strangers from the internet.
- **Top Picks** gives you a daily curated list of profiles Tinder thinks you'll like. Think of it as Tinder playing matchmaker, with about the same success rate as your aunt at Thanksgiving.
- **Tinder Gold price: ~$39.99/month**

If you want the deep dive on this one, check out our [Tinder Gold review](/blog/is-tinder-gold-worth-it).

### Tinder Platinum (Everything in Gold, Plus Desperation Features)

Platinum is Gold with two extra features that Tinder charges another ten bucks a month for. Bold strategy.

- **Priority Likes** means your right-swipes get shown to people sooner. Tinder claims this increases your match potential by **25%**. That's their number, not ours.
- **Message Before Match** lets you attach a note to your Super Like. In theory, this helps you stand out. In practice, most people either ignore the note or read it and still swipe left.
- **Tinder Platinum price: ~$49.99/month**

### Tinder Select (For People Who Hate Money)

And then there's Select. The $499/month invite-only tier that less than 1% of Tinder users have access to. For that price you get:

- **DM anyone twice per week** without matching first. Just cold-approach someone's inbox like a LinkedIn recruiter.
- An **exclusive badge** so everyone knows you spent five hundred dollars a month on a dating app.
- You get shown to "the most sought-after profiles." Whatever that means.

If you're considering Select, I have two pieces of advice. First, go outside. Second, keep going outside. That's $6,000 a year. You could literally fly to another continent and meet people in person for that money.

## That Time Tinder Got Sued for $60.5 Million (The Age Tax)

Remember how I mentioned Tinder charges older users more? Turns out that's not just shady. It's potentially illegal. At least in California.

Here's the story. For years, Tinder charged users over 30 significantly more for Plus and Gold subscriptions than users under 30. Same features, same app, different price tag based purely on age. A group of California users noticed, got angry, and filed a class action lawsuit under the Unruh Civil Rights Act.

The result? A **$60.5 million settlement** affecting roughly **268,000 California users**. The court gave preliminary approval on **January 13, 2026**, with a payment deadline of **August 18, 2026**.

Tinder, naturally, denies all allegations. Of course they do. The company that charged 35-year-olds double what 25-year-olds paid says there was nothing discriminatory about it. (Sure, Jan.)

The kicker? Age-based pricing is STILL happening outside California. If you're 32 and sitting in Texas right now wondering why your Tinder subscription costs more than your younger coworker's, that's why. California got a payout. You get the privilege of paying more.

## Does Paying for Tinder Actually Get You More Matches? (We Have Data)

This is the section you actually came for. Forget the feature lists and pricing tables. Does throwing money at Tinder make your phone buzz more?

YouTube creator Zackary Smigel ran a controlled experiment that now has over **1 million views**. He used [SwipeStats.io](/upload) to track his data across different tiers. The results:

<Video url="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=02Ss76rFInw" />

- **Free Tinder:** 3 matches per month
- **Tinder Gold:** 4.6 matches per month
- **Tinder Platinum:** 14.6 matches per month

Before you run to the App Store with your credit card out, here's what Smigel actually discovered: the free monthly Boost drove the majority of his Platinum matches. Not the Priority Likes. Not the Message Before Match. The Boost. That single 30-minute window where Tinder actually shows your profile to people.

Then there's Rey Arty, another creator who spent $120 across three dating apps for one week each. His Tinder Platinum results after spending $20+ for the week? **3 matches. Two ghosted him. One gave dry replies.** He rated the experience **1 out of 10**. He also sent 10 Super Likes during the test. Matches from Super Likes? Zero. A big fat goose egg.

I'm Paw Markus, and I've been analyzing dating app data at SwipeStats for years now. Here's what our dataset of **7,000+ real Tinder profiles** with **294 million total swipes** and **3.14 million matches** tells us: the average male match rate is about **1.69%**. That's 1-2 matches per 100 right-swipes. The average right-swipe rate for men is **53%**, meaning dudes swipe right on more than half of all profiles they see. (And they wonder why their match rates are low. Check out our full [Tinder statistics](/blog/tinder-statistics) for more depressing numbers.)

The bottom line? Premium amplifies an existing good profile. It does not resurrect a dead one. If your photos make people cringe, no amount of Priority Likes will help. You're just paying to get rejected faster.

Let's do some quick math. If Gold costs $40/month and gets you 5 matches, that's **$8 per match**. If two of those five actually respond to your opener, that's $20 per conversation. If one leads to a date, that's $40 per date. Before you've even bought the coffee. Suddenly that "free" dating app isn't looking so free.

## Is Tinder Plus Worth It? (The Honest Verdict)

I could give you a wishy-washy "it depends" answer. But you didn't come to a blog called SwipeStats for lukewarm takes. So here's my honest breakdown.

### Tinder Plus is worth it if:

- **You travel frequently.** Passport is genuinely useful. Being able to start swiping in a new city before you land is a real advantage, especially if you travel for work. It's the one feature free users can't replicate at all.
- **You're a heavy swiper who keeps hitting the daily cap.** Free users get about 100 likes per day. If you blow through those in 20 minutes (we've all been there), unlimited likes removes that ceiling.
- **You want to hide your age or distance.** Maybe you're 38 and matching with 25-year-olds. Maybe you live 45 miles from the nearest city. Privacy controls are underrated.

### Skip Tinder Plus if:

- **You swipe less than 30 minutes a day.** You're probably not even hitting the free like cap. Paying for unlimited likes you won't use is like paying for a gym membership and going twice a year. (Also, hi. I see you.)
- **Your photos are bad.** I keep saying this because nobody listens. Paying for Tinder with bad photos is like putting premium gas in a car with no engine. The fuel isn't the problem. Fix your profile first. Work on your [bio and photos](/blog/best-tinder-bios) or at minimum ask a brutally honest friend.
- **You want a serious relationship.** Tinder can work for relationships, but it's built for volume. If you want something deeper, [Hinge](/blog/hinge-statistics) is designed for that. Tinder is the McDonald's drive-through of dating. Sometimes you want a sit-down restaurant.

### The "Just Buy Individual Boosts" Alternative

Here's a money-saving move most people overlook. Individual Boosts cost about **$6.99 each**. If the main value of Plus is the monthly Boost (and based on the data, it is), buying 1-2 Boosts per month costs $7-$14. That's cheaper than even the cheapest Plus plan.

You miss out on unlimited likes and Passport, sure. But if you're a casual user who just wants more eyeballs on their profile, individual Boosts are the move.

### The Bigger Picture: Tinder Is Losing Paid Users

Here's something Match Group probably doesn't want me telling you. Tinder payers **fell 8% year-over-year** to 8.77 million in Q4 2025. Revenue per payer went UP 5% to $17.63. Translation: fewer people are paying, and the ones who do are spending more. Only about **15%** of Tinder users pay for any subscription. The other 85% are either happy with free or decided the juice wasn't worth the squeeze.

That's not a ringing endorsement of the product.

## How to Get Tinder Plus Cheaper (Without Selling a Kidney)

If you've decided Tinder Plus is right for your situation, at least don't pay full price like a sucker.

### The Subscribe-Cancel-Wait Hack

This one comes directly from Zackary Smigel's testing. Subscribe to Plus, then cancel immediately. Wait a few days. Tinder will panic and offer you **up to 50% off** to come back. They'd rather have you at half price than not at all. It's the dating app equivalent of threatening to cancel your cable subscription. Works more often than you'd think.

### Go Annual

The 12-month plan drops the Tinder Plus cost to roughly **$6-9/month**. That's a serious discount over the monthly rate. The catch? You're committing to a year of Tinder. If that sentence made your stomach drop, maybe reconsider.

### Be Under 30

Not exactly a hack you can control (unless you have a time machine, in which case you have bigger problems to solve). But if you're under 30, your prices are likely lower across the board. Thank the [algorithm](/blog/tinder-algorithm) and the age-discrimination lawsuit above.

### Wait for Promotions

Tinder regularly offers first-month discounts based on your activity patterns. If you've been active on the free tier for a while, you might see a discounted offer pop up. Patience, grasshopper.

### What About a Tinder Plus Free Trial?

Search volume for "tinder plus free trial" has jumped **86% year-over-year**. People really want to test-drive before buying. The reality? Free trials exist, but they're inconsistent. Tinder targets them based on your activity, account age, and whatever mood their algorithm is in that day. You can't reliably get one, so don't base your decision on the hope of a freebie.

## FAQ

### How much is Tinder Plus per month?

The Tinder Plus price ranges from about **$17.99 to $24.99 per month** for a single-month plan in the US. The exact amount depends on your age, location, device, and what Tinder thinks you're willing to pay. Multi-month plans bring the cost down significantly, with annual plans running as low as $6-9/month.

### Does Tinder Plus have a free trial?

Occasionally, but don't count on it. Tinder targets free trial offers based on your in-app activity and account history. There's no reliable way to trigger one. If you see the offer, grab it. If not, refer to the discount hacks above.

### Why is my Tinder Plus price different from my friend's?

Dynamic pricing. Tinder adjusts subscription costs based on age, location, device (iOS vs Android), and account history. If you're over 30, you almost certainly pay more. This was literally the basis of a **$60.5 million lawsuit**. Your friend isn't lying. You're just getting charged more.

### Is Tinder Gold worth the extra money over Plus?

The big upgrade is "See Who Likes You," which shows you everyone who already swiped right on you. If you actually get likes (big if), this saves time and removes the guessing game. For a full breakdown, read our [Gold deep-dive](/blog/is-tinder-gold-worth-it). The short version: if you're getting likes and don't want to waste time swiping to find them, Gold pays for itself. If your likes tab is emptier than a gym on January 15th, save your money.

### Can you use Tinder effectively for free?

Yes. Free Tinder gives you 100 likes per day and access to the core matching and messaging features. You'll see ads and won't get the premium bells and whistles. But plenty of people find matches, have conversations, and go on dates without spending a cent. The 85% of Tinder users who don't pay aren't all sitting alone.
