## How Many People Use Happn? (170 Million... Sort Of)

Happn loves to throw around that **170-180 million registered users** number. You will see it in every press release, every acquisition announcement, every "look how relevant we still are" puff piece. And technically, it is not wrong. That many people have, at some point in their lives, downloaded happn and created an account.

The operative phrase there is "at some point in their lives."

Because the monthly active user count is **6.5 million**. Do the math. That is a **96% drop-off** from registered to active. Ninety-six percent of people who signed up for happn are not using happn. They signed up, swiped twice, realized nobody in their suburb had the app, and went back to [Tinder](/blog/tinder-statistics) or Bumble like everybody else.

Here is what the engagement actually looks like:

| Metric | Number |
|---|---|
| Total registered users | 170-180 million |
| Monthly active users | 6.5 million |
| Daily active users | 2 million |
| New signups per month | 1.5 million |
| Messages per day | 4.9 million |

For perspective, Tinder has about **60 million** monthly active users. Bumble sits around **30 million**. Happn's 6.5 million puts it in the "I'm just happy to be here" tier of dating apps. It is available in **75+ countries**, which sounds global until you realize that its user base is concentrated in maybe five of them.

The 1.5 million new signups per month sounds encouraging. But if 96% of your historical users already ghosted, you are basically running on a treadmill. People keep coming in the front door and immediately walking out the back.

## Happn Revenue Statistics: All $25 Million of It

Let's talk money. Happn's annual revenue for 2024 was approximately **$25 million**. In 2023, they reported about **37 million euros** ($40 million USD) with **5 million euros** in EBITDA. Q1 2024 alone pulled in $8 million, but the full-year number came in lower than the previous year.

To put $25 million in dating app terms:

| App | Annual Revenue (2024) |
|---|---|
| Tinder | $1.94 billion |
| Bumble | $866 million |
| Happn | ~$25 million |

Tinder makes roughly **78 times** what happn makes. Bumble makes **35 times** more. Happn is not playing in the same ballpark. Happn is not even in the same zip code as the ballpark. Happn is the kid with a lemonade stand down the street from the ballpark.

The revenue breakdown tells an interesting story too. About **40% of happn's revenue comes from Brazil alone**. When one country accounts for nearly half your income, you do not have a global dating app. You have a Brazilian dating app with international ambitions.

Happn has **3 million paying subscribers**, which translates to an average revenue per user of about **$23 per year**. US pricing runs $24.99/month, $89.99 for six months, or $119.99 for a year. Not cheap. But also not relevant if you are American, because the odds of matching with anyone in your area are roughly the same as finding a parking spot in Manhattan on a Friday night.

The company did manage to triple its profits since 2022 through strategic repositioning in India and Turkey. So they are making money. Just not Tinder money. Not Bumble money. Not even [Match.com money](/blog/match-com-statistics).

## Happn User Demographics: Who Is Still Swiping on This Thing?

### The Gender Split (Spoiler: It Is Bad)

Happn's user base is **66% male and 34% female**, according to a dataset of 1,200 profiles analyzed by ROAST. That is a nearly 2:1 ratio of men to women, which makes every other [dating app gender ratio](/blog/dating-app-statistics-gender) look balanced by comparison.

For context, Tinder's split is roughly 75/25 (also terrible), but happn's smaller user base makes the imbalance feel even more pronounced. When your entire active user pool is 6.5 million and two-thirds of them are dudes, the math gets bleak fast.

### Age Distribution (Younger Than You Think)

The **average happn user is 26 years old**, which makes sense given the app's design. You need to actually go outside and walk past people for happn to work. People over 40 tend to drive everywhere.

| Age Group | Percentage |
|---|---|
| 18-24 | ~28% |
| 25-34 | 42% |
| 35-44 | ~18% |
| 45+ | ~12% |

The largest group is **25-34 year olds at 42%**, which tracks with urban professionals who commute through dense city centers. Interestingly, **18% of happn users have a post-graduate degree**, suggesting the app skews slightly more educated than the average swipe fest.

A 2025 Guardian report claimed that over half of happn users are now 35-54, which contradicts the other data. Either the user base aged up rapidly post-acquisition, or someone is playing fast and loose with their survey samples. I am inclined to believe the younger skew based on the app's core mechanic. You have to physically cross paths with people. That favors city dwellers who walk, not suburbanites who drive from their garage to a parking lot.

## Happn Match Rates: The Gender Gap That Makes Tinder Look Fair

This is where things get properly depressing for the guys. I have seen bad match rate data across every [dating app](/blog/dating-app-statistics) I have analyzed, and happn still managed to surprise me.

| Metric | Men | Women |
|---|---|---|
| Match rate | ~2.5% | ~50% |
| Average daily matches | 0.6 | ~5 |
| Like rate (how often they like profiles) | 1 in 3 | 1 in 16 |
| % getting less than 1 match/day | 52% | ~10% |

**Men's match rate on happn is about 2.5%.** That is 1 match for every 40 likes you send. You could swipe right on your entire morning commute and still go home empty-handed. **Fifty-two percent of men** get less than one match per day. Let me say that differently. The majority of men on happn do not get a single match in a 24-hour period. Not one.

Women, meanwhile, enjoy a **50% match rate**. One in every two likes turns into a match. Five matches per day on average. Different planet.

The selectivity gap explains part of this. Men like **1 out of every 3 profiles** they see, essentially swiping right on a third of everyone. Women like **1 out of every 16**. Men are casting a wide net. Women are fishing with a spear. The results track accordingly.

Now, **85% of happn users say they are looking for a relationship** (only 3% say hookups). Whether that 85% number reflects reality or just what people tell surveys to feel better about themselves is a separate conversation. But it does differentiate happn from [Tinder's hookup reputation](/blog/tinder-review).

## What Happened to Happn? (The French Romance That Got Sold to Beijing)

The happn story is genuinely fascinating if you like watching a good idea struggle against market reality. Let me walk you through it.

**2014:** Didier Rappaport (who co-founded Dailymotion) launches happn in Paris. The pitch is simple and kind of romantic. Instead of swiping through strangers, you match with people you have actually crossed paths with in real life. The Sliding Doors approach to dating. The app tracks your GPS and shows you profiles of people who came within 250 meters of you.

**2015-2019:** Rapid growth. Happn hits **75 million users** by 2019. At its peak, it was the **number two dating app worldwide** (excluding China). Not bad for a French startup competing against the Silicon Valley machine.

**2021:** Rappaport leaves the company in July amid sexual harassment allegations. Karima Ben Abdelmalek takes over as CEO. The app is still growing but losing ground to the giants.

**2022-2024:** Ben Abdelmalek repositions the app toward India and Turkey, tripling profits. The "Daily Matches" feature launches in August 2025. The web version gets discontinued (app-only from 2025). Revenue stabilizes but does not grow into Tinder territory.

**September 2025:** Hello Group, a Beijing-based social discovery company (listed on Nasdaq as MOMO), acquires happn. The deal positions happn for expansion into Asia and Africa. The French love story now has Chinese investors steering the ship.

Total funding over its lifetime was **$22-37 million** across 5 rounds. For a company that was once the world's second most popular dating app, that is surprisingly lean. Tinder's parent company Match Group has a market cap north of $8 billion.

Academic researchers have also flagged **privacy vulnerabilities** in happn's location system. Forensic analysis showed that bad actors can exploit the GPS data to track users' daily routines and movements. When your entire product is "we know where you have been," that is a particularly uncomfortable security concern.

## Is Happn Still Worth Downloading in 2026?

I will be straight with you. This depends entirely on where you live.

**Happn works in exactly one scenario:** you live in a dense urban area with high foot traffic. Paris. Sao Paulo. Mumbai. Delhi. Maybe Manhattan on a good day. In these cities, the crossed-paths feature is not a gimmick. It is actually a smarter way to meet people than algorithmic swiping, because you already share geography and probably a commute.

**Happn does not work in literally every other scenario.** Suburbs. Small towns. Rural areas. Any place where people drive instead of walk. If you are not physically crossing paths with other happn users throughout your day, the app has nothing to show you. It is a dating app powered by foot traffic, and if there is no foot traffic, there are no dates.

A few other things to consider:

- **24-hour boosts** (compared to Tinder's 30 minutes) are genuinely more useful for the app's proximity model.
- The **privacy concerns** are real. An app that logs every location where you crossed paths with someone is either innovative or creepy, depending on your perspective. I lean toward "both."
- **YouTube reviewers** in the US rated it mixed-to-negative, mostly because the user pool outside major cities is paper-thin.
- The app is now **app-only** (web version discontinued in 2025), so you cannot casually browse on your laptop like some kind of desktop-dwelling hermit.

If you are in one of happn's strong markets, it is worth a shot alongside your main dating apps. If you are not, stick with the [best dating apps for your situation](/blog/best-dating-apps) and save your storage space.

## Happn Statistics FAQ

### How many users does happn have?

Happn claims **170-180 million registered users**, but the monthly active number is only **6.5 million**. That 96% gap between signups and actual usage tells you everything about retention.

### Is happn free?

Freemium model. You can use it for free with limited features, but premium subscriptions start at **$24.99/month** in the US. Six months runs $89.99 and a year costs $119.99.

### Which countries use happn the most?

**Brazil** (40M+ users, 40% of revenue), **India** (~39M users), **France** (17.2% of users), **USA** (13.1%), and the UK round out the top five. It is heavily concentrated in a handful of markets.

### How does happn work?

Happn uses your phone's GPS to detect when two users pass within **250 meters** of each other. When that happens, both profiles appear in each other's feed. You like, they like back, you match. It is proximity-based matchmaking instead of algorithm-based swiping.

### Is happn better than Tinder?

Completely different concept. Happn is proximity-based, Tinder is algorithm-based. Tinder has **10x more active users** and works everywhere. Happn works brilliantly in dense cities and barely functions elsewhere. If you are in Paris or Sao Paulo, try happn. If you are anywhere else, Tinder or [Hinge](/blog/hinge-review) are safer bets.

### What is happn's match rate?

Men average a **2.5% match rate** (1 match per 40 likes). Women average **50%** (1 match per 2 likes). The gender gap is alive and well on happn.

### Who bought happn?

**Hello Group**, a Beijing-based social discovery company listed on Nasdaq (ticker: MOMO), acquired happn in **September 2025**. The deal aims to expand happn into Asian and African markets.
