## What a Hinge Subscription Actually Gets You (And What It Doesn't)

Let's start with what you're working with. Hinge has three tiers, and they're structured exactly the way a company that says it's "designed to be deleted" but also desperately wants your recurring revenue would structure them.

**Free Hinge** is the diet soda of dating apps. You get:

- 8 likes per day (yes, eight, like it's 2003 and you're rationing texts on a Nokia 3310)
- Basic filters: age, location, gender, ethnicity, religion
- The ability to send roses (if you ever get one)
- A profile that sits in the pile with everyone else

**Hinge+** is where Match Group starts reaching for your wallet:

- Unlimited likes (the single best feature, full stop)
- See everyone who's already liked you (instead of the blurred-out tease)
- Sort your incoming likes by Last Active and Nearby
- Advanced filters: height, politics, drinking, smoking, drugs, children
- Dealbreakers that actually filter people out

**HingeX** is the "I have disposable income and questionable priorities" tier:

- Everything in Hinge+
- Skip the Line (an always-on boost that puts you near the top of people's feeds)
- Priority Likes (your likes sit at the top of someone's queue for 7 days)
- Enhanced Recommendations (the algorithm shows you "better" profiles first)

Here's what absolutely zero Hinge subscription plans include: fixing your photos, writing your [Hinge prompts](/blog/best-hinge-prompts), or injecting personality into your profile. That's still on you, champ.

| Feature | Free | Hinge+ | HingeX |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily Likes | 8 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| See Who Likes You | No | Yes | Yes |
| Advanced Filters | No | Yes | Yes |
| Dealbreakers | No | Yes | Yes |
| Skip the Line | No | No | Yes |
| Priority Likes | No | No | Yes |
| Enhanced Recommendations | No | No | Yes |
| Monthly Price | $0 | ~$29.99 | ~$49.99 |

## Hinge Free vs Paid: Living on 8 Likes a Day

Let me paint you a picture of what [8 likes a day](/blog/how-many-likes-a-day-on-hinge) actually feels like.

You open Hinge. You see someone cute. Like. Someone interesting. Like. A profile that made you laugh. Like. Someone with a dog. Like. Someone who looks like they'd tolerate your existence. Like. And just like that, you're already more than halfway through your daily supply. You haven't even finished your first cup of coffee.

Here's the math that'll make you cry. The average male right-swipe rate from our SwipeStats data (based on **7,000+ profiles**) is about **53%**. That means for every 8 profiles you see, you want to like roughly 4 of them. So your 8 daily likes get you through about 15 profiles before Hinge puts up the velvet rope and says "come back tomorrow, peasant."

That's not a dating strategy. That's Hinge drip-feeding you human connection like a dealer giving out free samples.

Unlimited likes is, without question, the single most valuable feature in any Hinge subscription plan. It's not close. Everything else is nice to have. Unlimited likes actually changes how you use the app.

The "see who likes you" feature is useful, sure. It saves you time and lets you prioritize people who've already shown interest. But it doesn't generate new likes. It just reveals the ones that already exist. If nobody's liking your profile, this feature is a window into an empty room.

Advanced filters sound great on paper. Height, politics, drinking habits, children preferences. But let's be honest. Most people's actual filter criteria boil down to "attractive and breathing." The filters are really only valuable if you have specific dealbreakers, and if you do, they're genuinely worth it.

## Is a Hinge Subscription Worth It? Here's What the Data Says

Alright, let's get into the part you actually came for. Does paying Hinge make a measurable difference? I'm going to use real numbers instead of the marketing fluff Hinge wants you to believe.

A real user ran an A/B test across all three tiers, sending 500 likes on each. Here's what happened:

| Tier | Likes Sent | Matches | Match Rate |
|---|---|---|---|
| HingeX | 500 | 12 | 2.4% |
| Free | 500 | 10 | 2.0% |
| Hinge+ | 500 | 8 | 1.6% |

Read that again. **Free outperformed Hinge+.** HingeX did the best, but we're talking about 2 extra matches per 500 likes. That's the kind of improvement that makes statisticians shrug and say "within noise."

The subscription is a multiplier, not a miracle. If your profile converts at 2%, paying means you can send 200 likes a day instead of 8, which means more total matches purely through volume. That's the actual value proposition. Not some magical boost to your attractiveness.

But here's the thing I need you to understand (and I'm writing this in a dating blog, which is a weird place to deliver life advice, but here we are): if your profile sucks, a Hinge subscription means your bad profile gets shown to MORE people. Congratulations, you've scaled your failure. You're now bad at Hinge at an industrial level.

**1.9 million people** pay for Hinge subscriptions according to Match Group's Q4 2025 earnings. That's a lot of hopeful credit cards. But the dirty secret is that most matches still ghost. The funnel leaks at conversation, not visibility. So before you throw money at the problem, make sure the problem is actually visibility and not the fact that your opening message is "hey" followed by silence.

## Hinge+ vs HingeX: The $20 Question

The gap between [Hinge+ and HingeX](/blog/hinge-x-vs-hinge-plus) comes down to one real question: is Skip the Line worth an extra $20 a month?

Skip the Line is an always-on boost. Instead of paying for individual boosts that last 30 minutes, HingeX keeps you perpetually near the top of people's feeds. In theory, this means more eyes on your profile, more incoming likes, and more chances for matches.

Priority Likes are the other differentiator. When you like someone on HingeX, your like sits at the top of their queue for 7 days instead of getting buried under a pile of other desperate souls. It's basically cutting the line at a club, except the club is full of people staring at their phones.

Enhanced Recommendations is the feature Hinge is least transparent about. They say it shows you "better" profiles first. Which also means they're deliberately showing free users worse profiles first. They won't say it that way, but that's exactly what it means. Cool. Love that for us.

But the short version: if you're already paying for Hinge+ and your [profile is optimized](/blog/best-hinge-profiles-for-guys), HingeX might squeeze out a few extra matches. If your profile needs work, that $20 is better spent on better photos.

## How to Get Your Hinge Subscription Cheaper (Yes, Really)

The first rule of Hinge subscription discounts: never pay monthly if you can avoid it. The 6-month plan for Hinge+ works out to about $15/month, which is nearly half the monthly price. That's not a coupon. That's basic math that Hinge hopes you're too impatient to do.

**Promo codes exist.** Sites like SimplyCodes occasionally have Hinge discount codes, and Hinge runs seasonal promotions (cuffing season is prime time, because nothing says romance like a corporate marketing initiative tied to your loneliness).

**The cancel-retention trick.** Start the cancellation process in the app. Don't actually cancel. Just start the flow. Hinge sometimes throws a discount at you to keep you subscribed, like an ex who suddenly texts you the moment you start moving on. It doesn't always work, but when it does, you can shave 20-30% off your renewal.

**The sprint strategy.** This is what I actually recommend. Subscribe for 1-2 months. Go hard. Optimize your [prompts and photos](/blog/best-hinge-prompts-for-guys), send likes aggressively, respond to every match, and actually try to convert matches into dates. Then cancel. Dating apps are not meant to be permanent fixtures in your life, no matter how much their revenue model depends on it.

The app is literally called "designed to be deleted." Take them at their word for once.

## How to Cancel Your Hinge Subscription (Before It Eats Your Wallet)

This is the part where I save you money, and you should honestly bookmark this section because you will forget where to find it when you need it.

**If you subscribed through iOS:**
1. Open Settings on your iPhone
2. Tap your Apple ID at the top
3. Tap Subscriptions
4. Find Hinge and tap it
5. Tap Cancel Subscription

**If you subscribed through Android:**
1. Open the Google Play Store
2. Tap your profile icon
3. Tap Payments & subscriptions, then Subscriptions
4. Find Hinge and tap it
5. Tap Cancel subscription

**If you subscribed through Stripe (web):**
1. Open Hinge's Account Settings
2. Find Subscription
3. Tap Cancel

Here's the thing that catches people every single time, and I cannot stress this enough: **deleting the Hinge app does NOT cancel your subscription.** Your credit card will keep bleeding money to Match Group while you're blissfully unaware, thinking you've moved on with your life. Hinge (and every other subscription app) is absolutely counting on you making this mistake.

Cancel at least 24 hours before your renewal date. You'll keep all your premium features until the paid period ends, so there's no reason to wait until the last second and risk forgetting.

## FAQ

### How much is a Hinge subscription?

Hinge+ runs about **$29.99/month** or as low as **$15/month** on the 6-month plan. HingeX is about **$49.99/month** or **$25/month** on the 6-month plan. But thanks to dynamic pricing, your mileage (and your bill) may vary.

### Is a Hinge subscription worth it?

Depends entirely on your profile quality. If your photos are solid and your prompts are engaging, unlimited likes will get you more matches through sheer volume. If your profile looks like it was assembled during a hostage situation, you're just paying to show more people how bad you are at this. Fix the profile first, then consider paying.

### Can you cancel Hinge early?

Yes. You can cancel anytime, and you'll keep your premium features until the end of your current billing period. No refunds on unused time though. Match Group didn't become a billion-dollar company by giving money back.

### Does Hinge use dynamic pricing?

Yes. Hinge shows different prices to different users based on factors like age and location. Two people standing next to each other can open the app and see completely different hinge subscription plans at completely different prices. It's legal, it's common, and it's annoying.

### What happens when Hinge likes reset?

Free users get their [8 likes reset](/blog/when-do-hinge-likes-reset) every 24 hours. The exact reset time varies, but it's generally tied to when you used your first like the previous day. Paid subscribers don't need to worry about this because unlimited means unlimited.
