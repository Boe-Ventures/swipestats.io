## Figure Out Who's Charging You (Before You Start Rage-Clicking)

This is where most people screw up. They open the Tinder app looking for a cancel button, can't find one, and spiral into a Reddit post at 2am titled "TINDER IS A SCAM."

You need to cancel through whoever is handling your billing. Not Tinder. The billing platform. And here's a neat trick to figure out which one: check your order number.

- **Starts with MK** = Apple App Store
- **Starts with GPA** = Google Play
- **Starts with TNDR** = Tinder direct billing (credit card through their website)

Don't remember your order number? Check your email for payment receipts from Apple, Google, or Tinder. One of those three has been quietly siphoning money from your account, and it's time to find out which one.

Wrong platform = can't find the cancel button = more angry Reddit posts = still getting charged. Let's avoid that cycle.

## How to Cancel Tinder Gold on iPhone (The Apple Tax Exit Strategy)

If you subscribed through the App Store, Apple is your landlord. Here's how to break the lease:

1. Open **Settings** on your iPhone.
2. Tap **your name** at the top (the one with your Apple ID).
3. Tap **Subscriptions**.
4. Find **Tinder** in the list and tap it.
5. Tap **Cancel Subscription**.
6. Confirm when prompted.

That's it. You'll see an expiration date showing when your Gold access runs out. Until then, swipe away with your remaining premium privileges like a king surveying his crumbling kingdom.

If Tinder doesn't show up in your subscriptions, you either subscribed through Google Play or Tinder.com. Go back to the previous section and figure out who's actually billing you.

## How to Cancel Tinder Gold on Android (The Google Play Escape Route)

Google makes this slightly more convoluted because Google loves burying settings deeper than the Mariana Trench.

1. Open the **Google Play Store** app.
2. Tap your **Profile icon** in the top right.
3. Tap **Payments & subscriptions**.
4. Tap **Subscriptions**.
5. Find **Tinder** and tap it.
6. Tap **Cancel subscription**.
7. Follow the prompts and confirm.

Google will send you a confirmation email. Keep it. Screenshot it. Frame it on your wall if you have to. Because if anything goes wrong later, that email is your proof that you did what you were supposed to do.

## How to Cancel Tinder Gold on Tinder.com (For the Direct Billing Crowd)

If you subscribed through Tinder's website with a credit card, you're in a special club. And by "special," I mean Tinder made it extra annoying for you.

1. Log in at **tinder.com**.
2. Click your **profile icon**.
3. Go to **Account Settings**.
4. Click **Manage Payment Account**.
5. Click **Cancel Subscription**.

This is the only way to cancel if you subscribed directly. The app won't show a cancel option. Tinder won't redirect you. You're on your own, kid. Like a Home Alone sequel nobody asked for, except instead of fighting burglars, you're fighting a subscription management interface designed by people who clearly don't want you to leave.

## What Happens After You Hit Cancel (Spoiler: You Don't Lose Everything Immediately)

Deep breath. You clicked cancel. The world didn't end. If you're reading this section before actually canceling, good for you. Planning ahead like a functional adult. If you already canceled and you're here in a panic, welcome. Here's what actually happens:

**What stays:**
- Your Gold features keep working until the end of your billing period. You paid for it. It's yours.
- Your profile stays visible to other users.
- Your matches stay. Your conversations stay. Nothing disappears.

**What you lose when the period ends:**
- See Who Likes You (back to the blurred-out tease)
- Unlimited likes (hello again, daily cap of roughly 100)
- Your monthly [Boost](/blog/tinder-boost) (back to being invisible)
- [Super Likes](/blog/super-like-on-tinder) (back to the free-user ration)
- Passport (stuck swiping in your own city like a peasant)
- Top Picks (the algorithm stops playing matchmaker for you)

And let me say this one more time because I know at least three of you need to hear it:

**Deleting the app does NOT cancel your subscription.**

Deleting the app removes the icon from your phone. That's it. The billing continues. Tinder keeps charging you. Your bank account keeps bleeding. You keep wondering why there's a mysterious $34.99 charge every month. It's not mysterious. It's Tinder. You played yourself.

## How to Get a Tinder Gold Refund (Good Luck, But Here's the Play)

Trying to get money back from Tinder is like trying to get a cat to apologize. Technically possible. Practically? You'd have better luck convincing Thanos to return the Infinity Stones politely.

**Apple:** Request a refund within 48 hours at [reportaproblem.apple.com](https://reportaproblem.apple.com). Apple reviews these at their discretion. Be polite. Be specific about why. Don't write a novel.

**Google Play:** Request through the Play Store. Google reviews it on their end. Similar deal. Be clear, be quick.

**Direct billing through Tinder:** You've got a 14-day window per their terms of service. After that, you're out of luck and out of money.

**The state loophole:** If you live in Arizona, California, Connecticut, Illinois, Iowa, Minnesota, New York, North Carolina, Ohio, or Wisconsin, you might have cooling-off rights that let you cancel without penalty within 3 business days. Check your state's consumer protection laws. This is the closest thing to a cheat code you'll find.

## Can't Cancel? Here's What's Probably Going Wrong

If you've followed every step above and something still isn't working, you're probably making one of these mistakes. I once spent 20 minutes trying to cancel through the app before realizing I'd subscribed through the website. Don't @ me.

- **Subscription not showing up:** You're logged into the wrong Apple ID or Google account. I know. It feels impossible to have the wrong one. But you do. Check your email address on the account and compare it to the one getting receipts.
- **Cancel button is grayed out:** Don't stare at it hoping it'll change. Contact Apple or Google support directly. They deal with this all day.
- **Still getting charged after canceling:** Check if you accidentally have multiple Tinder subscriptions. Or a second Tinder account you forgot about (no judgment, we've all been there). Also double-check you didn't subscribe through a different platform than the one you tried to cancel on.
- **You deleted the app instead of canceling:** Congratulations, you played yourself. Again. Go to Settings on your iPhone or Google Play on your Android and cancel the actual subscription. The app being gone from your phone means absolutely nothing to your billing cycle.

If all else fails, call your bank. Dispute the charge. It's the nuclear option, and Tinder might ban your account, but if you're canceling anyway, who cares? You can always start fresh on [a free dating app](/blog/free-dating-apps-without-payment) that doesn't treat your wallet like an all-you-can-eat buffet.

## FAQ

### Can you cancel Tinder Gold anytime?

Yes. No contracts. No cancellation fees. No angry phone call with a retention specialist trying to guilt you into staying (looking at you, cable companies). Cancel whenever you want. Your Gold features just keep running until the end of your current billing period.

### If I cancel Tinder Gold, will I be refunded?

Probably not. Tinder's official policy is essentially "lol no." Your best shot is going through Apple or Google within 48 hours of a charge. After that, you're mostly writing angry emails into the void.

### Does deleting the Tinder app cancel my subscription?

No. No. A thousand times no. This is the number one mistake people make, and it's more persistent than a cockroach after a nuclear apocalypse. Deleting the app removes it from your phone. Your subscription lives on your Apple ID, Google account, or Tinder.com billing. Like the Hotel California, you can check out any time you like, but you can never leave (unless you cancel through the actual billing platform).

### Can I downgrade from Gold to Plus instead of canceling entirely?

Yes, but Tinder doesn't give you a nice little "downgrade" button because of course they don't. Cancel Gold first. Let it expire. Then subscribe to Plus at the lower tier. It's clunky, but it works.

### What's the Tinder support phone number?

**214-853-4309**. Use it as a last resort when the app and website have failed you. And honestly, if you've reached the point of calling Tinder's phone support, you deserve a medal for patience.
