<TLDR>

### Cancel Tinder Gold

- Apple order number starts with **MK**: cancel in iPhone Settings → your name → Subscriptions.
- Google Play order starts with **GPA**: cancel in Play Store → Payments & subscriptions → Subscriptions.
- Tinder direct order starts with **TNDR**: cancel in Manage Payment Account on Tinder.com or the relevant Android billing screen.
- Deleting Tinder or the account does not cancel renewal.
- Access normally continues until the end of the paid billing period.

</TLDR>

## First, Find the Billing Provider

The cancellation path depends on who processes the payment. Check the receipt or order number:

- **MK** indicates an Apple purchase;
- **GPA** indicates Google Play;
- **TNDR** indicates Tinder.com or direct card billing.

If a different merchant appears on the receipt, it may be a third-party purchase. That merchant controls cancellation and refund requests.

## Cancel Tinder Gold on iPhone

For an Apple-billed subscription:

1. Open iPhone **Settings**.
2. Tap your name.
3. Tap **Subscriptions**.
4. Select Tinder.
5. Tap **Cancel Subscription**.

If Tinder is absent, verify that you are signed in with the Apple ID used to purchase it and check the receipt for another billing provider.

## Cancel Through Google Play

For an order beginning with GPA:

1. Open Google Play.
2. Tap the profile icon.
3. Open **Payments & subscriptions** → **Subscriptions**.
4. Select Tinder.
5. Tap **Cancel subscription** and confirm.

Changing from Android to iPhone does not move the billing relationship. Tinder says subscriptions do not transfer between platforms, so cancel through the original platform.

## Cancel Direct Android Billing

If the Android purchase used a card or PayPal directly rather than Google Play:

1. Open Tinder on the Android device.
2. Tap the profile icon.
3. Choose **Manage Payment Account**.
4. Select the active subscription.
5. Tap **Cancel Subscription**.

## Cancel a Tinder.com Purchase

For a TNDR order:

1. Sign in at Tinder.com.
2. Tap the profile icon.
3. Open **Manage Payment Account**.
4. Select the subscription.
5. Tap **Cancel Subscription**.

Apple Pay and web payment methods are available only in some markets. Follow the direct-billing instructions when the receipt identifies Tinder rather than an app store.

## What Happens After Cancellation?

Cancellation stops future renewal. It does not normally remove Gold immediately. Tinder says the subscription remains available for the days already paid.

The profile, matches, and messages remain unless you separately delete the account or unmatch people. When the paid term ends, the account returns to the available free experience.

## Canceling Is Not a Refund

Stopping renewal does not automatically reverse an earlier charge. Refund rules depend on the billing provider, location, and timing:

- Apple handles Apple purchase refunds;
- Google handles eligible Play refunds;
- Tinder reviews eligible direct-payment requests;
- third-party merchants handle their own purchases.

Use the official receipt and support channel. Avoid paying a third-party “cancellation” service or sharing login and verification codes.

## If You Cannot Find the Subscription

Check:

- every Apple ID or Google account used on the device;
- the order-number prefix and merchant name;
- whether a direct card or PayPal purchase was used;
- the subscription's current status and renewal date;
- whether the charge belongs to a different Tinder account.

An active subscription can be associated with only one Tinder account at a time. If access is missing but billing is active, use Tinder's Restore Purchase flow or contact official support with the receipt.

## FAQ

### Does deleting Tinder cancel Gold?

No. Tinder explicitly says uninstalling the app or deleting the account does not cancel the subscription.

### Do I lose Gold immediately?

Normally no. Paid features remain through the current billing period.

### Will cancellation delete matches?

No. Subscription cancellation and account deletion are separate actions.

### Can I downgrade instead?

Depending on the platform, you may need to cancel, wait for expiration, and subscribe again at the lower tier.
