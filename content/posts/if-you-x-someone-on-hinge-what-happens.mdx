<TLDR>

### The Short Answer

- The X button skips the current profile in Discover.
- You can undo only the most recent Skip when the undo arrow is available.
- Hinge support cannot manually restore a skipped profile.
- Use Remove or Report when you do not want to encounter a profile again or there is a safety issue.
- Unmatch is for an existing match and permanently removes the conversation.

</TLDR>

## What the X Button Does

In Hinge Discover, tapping the X means **Skip**. The feed moves to another recommendation and records that you were not interested in the skipped profile.

Hinge's help page does not say that a Skip sends the other person a rejection notification. It is a feed action, not a message.

Do not confuse the X button with HingeX, the paid subscription tier. They share a letter but are unrelated features.

## Can You Undo an Accidental X?

Yes, sometimes. Tap the undo arrow immediately after the Skip. Hinge says only the most recent Skip can be restored.

If the app shows **Undo unavailable**, that profile cannot be brought back through the control. Hinge support says it cannot manually restore skipped profiles from Discover, Standouts, or Likes You.

## Can the Profile Appear Again?

Hinge does not promise that every skipped profile is permanently excluded, nor does it publish a guaranteed reappearance schedule. Recommendations and account availability can change.

If simply skipping is not sufficient, use the three-dot menu and select **Remove**. If the account or content violates the rules or creates a safety concern, choose **Report** and provide accurate information.

## X vs. Remove vs. Report vs. Unmatch

| Action | Use it when | Documented result |
| --- | --- | --- |
| X / Skip | You are not interested in a Discover profile | Moves to the next recommendation |
| Undo | The most recent Skip was accidental | Restores only that last skipped profile when available |
| Remove | You do not want to see a profile again | Removes it through the profile menu |
| Report | There is harmful, deceptive, or prohibited behavior | Sends a safety report for review |
| Unmatch | You want to end an existing match | Permanently removes the match and conversation |

Use the least severe action that fits the situation, but do not hesitate to report a genuine safety problem.

## If They Already Liked You

Discover and Likes You are separate surfaces. The safest way to handle an incoming Like is to review it in Likes You and choose the available skip or match action there.

Avoid relying on old claims that an X in one feed must delete every interaction everywhere. Hinge's current documentation describes how each surface works but does not promise that broad side effect.

## Does Skipping Affect Recommendations?

Hinge says skipping indicates that you were not interested. That is a preference signal, but the company does not publish an exact penalty, score change, or formula for one Skip.

Skip profiles that are not a fit. Trying to manipulate the recommendation system by accepting or rejecting people strategically is less useful than keeping your actual preferences and Dealbreakers accurate.

## FAQ

### Will someone know I X'd them?

Hinge does not document a notification to the skipped person.

### Can Hinge support bring the profile back?

No. Its Discover guide says support cannot manually restore skipped profiles.

### Is the X permanent?

The immediate feed action is a Skip. Use Remove when the goal is not to encounter the profile again.

### Does X-ing someone use a Like?

No. A Skip is different from sending an outgoing Like. Hinge does not describe the X as consuming a Like.
