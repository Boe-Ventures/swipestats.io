<TLDR>

### The Short Answer

Hinge says it does not offer a search option for specific people. You can adjust Discover preferences, but you cannot enter a name, phone number, email, or social handle to retrieve someone's profile.

</TLDR>

## Can You Search a Name on Hinge?

No. Hinge's current Discover documentation explicitly says there is no person-search feature.

Discover shows recommendations based on preferences and mutual fit. Changing age, distance, location, or other filters may change the feed, but it does not create a reliable lookup tool and should not be used to monitor a particular person.

## What Hinge Preferences Can Do

All users can adjust core filters such as gender, distance, age range, ethnicity, relationship type, and religion. Subscribers have additional preferences.

A Dealbreaker makes a selected preference strict. Without it, Hinge may prioritize the preference while showing people outside it.

These controls are for shaping whom you may date, not identifying a known individual. Hinge does not expose a complete directory or guarantee every eligible profile will appear.

## If You Want to Avoid Someone You Know

Use **Block List** rather than trying to find their profile first:

1. Open the profile area.
2. Open Settings.
3. Select **Block List**.
4. Sync contacts or add a phone number or email manually.
5. Choose the contact to block.

Hinge says blocked contacts are not notified. The feature works only when the contact information matches the information that person used for Hinge, so it is a best-effort control rather than a guarantee.

Disconnecting contact access does not automatically clear contacts already placed on the block list.

## If You Encounter the Profile

From Discover, open the three-dot menu and choose:

- **Remove** when you do not want to see the profile again;
- **Report** when there is harmful, deceptive, or prohibited conduct.

Do not submit a false report merely because you know or dislike the person. Hinge prohibits misuse of reporting tools.

## Avoid Third-Party “Hinge Search” Sites

A service claiming it can find anyone from a phone number, photo, or social account may be:

- collecting personal information;
- selling low-quality public-record guesses;
- asking for Hinge credentials;
- scraping profiles without consent;
- or facilitating harassment.

Do not provide a dating-app password, SMS code, private photo, or payment information. Hinge support does not offer a secret lookup endpoint to third parties.

## If This Is About a Partner

Discovery is not a reliable fidelity investigation. A profile screenshot may be old, impersonated, paused, or missing context; failing to find a profile proves nothing.

Ask directly about relationship agreements and what each person considers a breach. If there is a safety concern, prioritize personal support and evidence you already lawfully possess rather than impersonation, account intrusion, or location tracking.

## FAQ

### Can I find someone by phone number?

No person search is available. You can use a number in Block List to try to avoid the account.

### Can I search by name or Instagram handle?

No. Hinge does not offer a specific-person search.

### Can I change my location to find someone?

Changing location changes the discovery area but does not guarantee a particular profile appears.

### Will a blocked contact know?

Hinge says Block List contacts are not notified.
