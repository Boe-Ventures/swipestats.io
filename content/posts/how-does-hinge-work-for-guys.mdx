<TLDR>

Hinge does not document a separate product for men. You build a profile from photos, prompts, and details; review people in Discover; Like or comment on a specific profile item; and match when the other person accepts.

Improve the part you control: clear photos, specific prompt answers, selective Likes, relevant comments, and respectful invitations.

</TLDR>

## The Basic Hinge Flow

1. Create the profile and choose preferences.
2. Review profiles in Discover.
3. Like a photo or prompt answer, optionally adding a comment.
4. The other person can accept the Like and match.
5. Either person can continue the chat.

Hinge's official description emphasizes that each match begins from a Like or comment on a specific profile element. Use that context rather than sending a generic greeting after the match.

## Free and Paid Use

Hinge says the regular version is free. Its plan comparison includes matching and messaging on the free tier. Hinge+ adds benefits such as more Likes, seeing incoming Likes together, and advanced preferences. HingeX adds higher-tier recommendation and visibility features.

Prices and packages vary. Start free long enough to understand the local pool and your profile before buying more volume.

## Build a Profile That Gives People Something to Choose

### Photos

Use current, clear images that make it easy to recognize you. A practical set might include:

- an unobstructed first photo with your face visible;
- a full-body photo;
- an activity you actually do;
- one social photo where your identity is obvious;
- a setting or interest that can start a conversation.

Avoid a first image dominated by sunglasses, a large group, or an old crop of another person.

### Prompts

Specific answers outperform empty self-praise because they provide material for a response.

Weak: “I love travel and food.”

Stronger: “I plan one trip a year around a train route and one around a bakery. Copenhagen currently leads.”

The stronger answer communicates behavior and gives somebody several openings.

## Send Better Likes

Do not Like every profile. Choose people whose stated goals and life fit yours, then comment on something you genuinely want to discuss:

- “You ranked the neighborhood cinemas. What put Rialto at number one?”
- “That bread looks professional. What was the hardest part to learn?”
- “You chose the same impossible debate prompt I did. Defend your answer.”

A comment does not guarantee a match. It simply helps the recipient understand why you chose them.

## After Matching

Return to the exact item that created the match, ask one shaped question, and share something too. When the exchange is reciprocal, suggest a simple public date:

> I am enjoying this. Want to continue over coffee Saturday afternoon?

Do not move immediately to sexual comments, demand a reply, or interpret a delayed response as a character verdict.

## About “Men's Match Rates”

SwipeStats' published Tinder dataset cannot be used as a Hinge match-rate benchmark. It comes from voluntarily uploaded Tinder exports, and Tinder and Hinge expose different actions and data.

Your results depend on location, preferences, profile, activity, and the people using the app around you. Any website promising one universal male Hinge match rate is hiding those differences.
