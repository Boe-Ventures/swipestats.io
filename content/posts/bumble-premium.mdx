## What Is Bumble Premium? (And Why Does It Cost More Than Your Grocery Budget?)

A Bumble Premium subscription is the second-highest tier in Bumble's increasingly confusing hierarchy. The lineup goes: Free, [Boost](/blog/bumble-boost), Premium, and the recently added Premium+. Four tiers. Because apparently three ways to take your money wasn't enough.

Here's the thing. Bumble used to have two paid tiers. Simple. Then in 2024 they bolted on Premium+ and reshuffled which features lived where. The biggest casualty? Beeline (seeing who liked you) got yanked out of Boost and moved up to Premium. If you were a Boost subscriber who liked that feature, surprise. Pay more now.

That's the dating app playbook. Give you something. Let you get used to it. Take it away. Charge you double to get it back. It's like your ex returning your hoodie and then asking for rent.

If you've used [Tinder Gold](/blog/is-tinder-gold-worth-it) or [Hinge+](/blog/is-hinge-plus-worth-it), you know what Premium is trying to be. It's the tier where you stop guessing who likes you and start seeing the list directly. On Tinder, that costs $30/month. On Hinge, $35/month. On Bumble? $55-60/month. Because Bumble apparently thinks its users have hedge fund money.

Let me ruin your day with a fun fact. The price you see on your screen right now might not be the price your roommate sees. Bumble uses dynamic pricing, which is a fancy way of saying they charge you whatever they think you'll pay.

Your age, your city, your device (iOS users consistently pay 15-30% more, thanks Apple), and possibly your usage patterns all factor in. It's algorithmic price discrimination wearing a yellow hoodie.

## Every Bumble Premium Feature (Ranked by Whether They Actually Matter)

Not all features are created equal. Some will change how you use the app. Most won't. Let me save you the trouble of figuring out which is which.

### Beeline: See Who Liked You (A-Tier)

This is it. The feature. The whole reason Bumble Premium exists.

Beeline shows you a list of every person who already swiped right on your profile. No guessing. No swiping through 300 profiles hoping to stumble into a mutual match. Just a queue of people who are already interested in you.

For guys on Bumble, this is genuinely useful. You still can't message first (Bumble's women-message-first rule doesn't care about your subscription tier). But knowing who likes you means you can swipe right on them specifically, create the match, and then wait for them to open the conversation. It removes the "am I swiping into the void?" feeling that makes [free Bumble such a ghost town](/blog/bumble-no-matches).

The catch? Your Beeline might have 50 people in it. Or 3. If your profile photos look like a police lineup and your bio says "just ask," Beeline will show you an empty room and charge you $60 for the privilege.

### Incognito Mode (B-Tier)

Incognito makes your profile invisible to everyone except people you swipe right on. It's stealth mode for people who don't want their coworkers, exes, or that weird guy from high school finding them on Bumble.

The trade-off is obvious. If nobody can see you, nobody can like you first. Your Beeline goes dark. You're trading visibility for privacy. That's a fine deal for people in specific situations (recently separated, public figures, people whose boss would definitely make it weird). For everyone else, it's paying $60/month to hide from the very people you're trying to meet.

### Travel Mode (C-Tier)

Swipe in another city before you get there. Useful if you're planning a trip and want to line up dates in advance. Borderline useless if you're not a frequent traveler.

I'll be honest, I've used this feature exactly twice. Once before a trip to Berlin. Once when I was bored and wanted to see what the dating pool looked like in Tokyo. The Berlin one led to an actual date. The Tokyo one led to me realizing I don't speak Japanese. So, mixed results.

### Advanced Filters (C-Tier, and Here's Why)

On paper, advanced filters sound incredible. Filter by height, exercise habits, education level, drinking preferences. Finally, a way to narrow down your matches to exactly what you want.

In practice, there's a fatal design flaw. When you filter by a trait (say, "has a graduate degree"), Bumble removes everyone who didn't fill in that field. Not everyone who doesn't have a degree. Everyone who just didn't bother to answer. And on Bumble, a LOT of people leave fields blank.

So you're not filtering out people who don't match your preferences. You're filtering out lazy profile-fillers. Which, let's be honest, includes half the population of any dating app. Your curated pool of height-verified, gym-going grad students? It's about 15% of what it could be. Congrats, you played yourself.

### Unlimited Swipes, Backtrack, Rematch, Extensions (Inherited From Boost)

All of these come with [Bumble Boost](/blog/bumble-boost) already. If you're upgrading from free to Premium, they're nice additions. If you're upgrading from Boost to Premium, they're not new. You're paying the premium for Beeline, Incognito, Travel Mode, and Advanced Filters. Full stop.

## Bumble Premium vs Boost: Which Tier Deserves Your Money?

Let's cut through the marketing fog. Here's what you actually gain by going from Boost ($17/month) to Premium ($55-60/month):

| Backtrack | Yes | Yes |
| Unlimited Extensions | Yes | Yes |
| Rematch | Yes | Yes |
| 5 SuperSwipes/week | Yes | Yes |
| 1 Spotlight/week | Yes | Yes |
| **Beeline** | No | **Yes** |

| **Travel Mode** | No | **Yes** |
| **Advanced Filters** | Basic | **Full** |

You're paying roughly $40 more per month for Beeline, Incognito, Travel Mode, and Advanced Filters. Beeline is the only one that consistently delivers value. The others are situational at best.

My take? If you live in a big city where your Beeline has actual humans in it, Premium is worth the jump. If you're in a small town where your dating pool is shallower than a rain puddle, Boost is fine. Your Beeline will have three people in it, and you probably already know all of them.

## Bumble Premium vs Premium Plus: The $20 Question

Premium+ costs about $80/month and adds a few extras on top of regular Premium:

- **Priority Likes**: Your swipes get shown to other users sooner. You skip the line.
- **2 Compliments per week**: Text-only messages you can send before matching. Like a Super Like with words.
- **2 Spotlights per week**: Double the visibility bursts compared to Premium's one.
- **10 SuperSwipes per week**: More than the 5 you get with Premium/Boost.

Sounds great on paper. Here's the problem with Priority Likes. Bumble pushes your profile to the front of the stack, where it competes directly with the most attractive profiles in your area. The top of the stack is where the hottest people live. If you're a solid 6 getting catapulted into 9-territory visibility, you're not getting more matches. You're getting more left-swipes from people who just saw three models before your face popped up.

YouTuber data backs this up. One creator found that Priority Likes didn't meaningfully increase his match rate compared to regular Premium. The theory is sound. The execution punishes average profiles.

Compliments (text messages before matching) are decent but limited to 2 per week. That's not going to reshape your dating life. It's a nice gesture feature, not a game-changer.

**Verdict**: Premium+ is for people in dense cities who already match well and want to squeeze out a few more percentage points. For most people? Regular Premium (or honestly, just Boost) is plenty.

## Is Bumble Premium Worth It? (Let's Ask the People Who Actually Paid)

This is where we stop theorizing and start looking at what happened when real people swiped their credit cards. Spoiler: it's not pretty.

### YouTuber Rey Arty: Premium Plus for 1 Week

Rey paid for a full week of Premium Plus. The deluxe package. Priority Likes, extra Spotlights, the works. His results?

**4 matches. All ghosted.** He used both Spotlights at peak times (Sunday and Tuesday at 8 PM, the supposedly optimal windows). Zero results from the Spotlights specifically. His rating? **2 out of 10.** Two. The same score you'd give a gas station sushi restaurant. That's what $40 bought him.

### YouTuber Nick Daniels: Premium for 1 Week

Nick spent a full week testing Bumble Premium. His total haul?

**1 match. ONE.** He also discovered (the hard way) that the Undo feature doesn't work retroactively. All those people he accidentally left-swiped before buying Premium? Gone forever. He literally said he "had more success as a free account." That's not a review. That's a cry for help.

### YouTuber Zackary Smigel: The Lifetime Subscriber

Zackary went all in. Bought the lifetime subscription for $250. Committed. Devoted. In it for the long haul. Here's what that devotion got him:

- **58,184 total swipes** (the man was DEDICATED)
- **373 people swiped right on him** (that's 0.64% of the population)
- **~26 total matches** (a 0.04% conversion from swipe to match)
- **~90% of his premium-sourced likes came from the weekly Spotlight**, not from any other premium feature

Read that last bullet again. The $250 lifetime subscription's value came almost entirely from the one feature that's already included in the $17/month Boost tier. Ninety percent. He could have gotten 90% of the results for a third of the price. Math: undefeated.

### The Business Side Tells the Same Story

Bumble's own financials paint a bleak picture. Revenue dropped **9.9% in 2025 to $965.7 million**. Paying subscribers fell **11.5% to roughly 3.6 million**. They laid off **30% of their staff** and slashed **80% of their performance marketing budget**.

Only **4.6% of Bumble's 50 million users** actually pay for a subscription. When 95 out of 100 people look at the pricing and say "nah," that's not a user problem. That's a product problem.

### The Brutal Truth

Premium multiplies your existing match rate. It does not create attraction where none exists. If your profile gets likes, Beeline shows you those likes faster and lets you act on them. If your profile doesn't get likes, Beeline shows you an empty queue. A very expensive empty queue.

Three guys paid real money. One got 4 matches that ghosted. One got 1 match. One got 26 matches over a lifetime subscription after swiping 58,000 times. I've seen better ROI from scratch-off lottery tickets.

### Who Premium IS Worth It For

Let's be fair. Premium isn't a scam. It's just not for everyone. You'll get value if:

- You live in a city with 100,000+ people where the dating pool is actually deep.
- You already get matches on free Bumble and want to see your Beeline instead of guessing.
- You're an active dater who uses the app daily and values time over money.
- Your [profile photos](/blog/best-dating-profile-photos) are solid and your bio isn't a war crime.

### Who Should SKIP It

- You live in a small town. Your Beeline will have tumbleweeds in it.
- You're getting zero matches on free Bumble. That's a profile problem, not a subscription problem.
- You swipe once a week when you're bored on the toilet. Premium rewards daily users, not tourists.
- You haven't read a single guide on [how to fix your dating profile](/blog/bumble-bio). Fix that first. Seriously.

## How to Get Bumble Premium for Less (Without Being Sketchy About It)

Nobody should pay full sticker price if they don't have to. Here are legit ways to save:

**Start with the 1-week plan.** Yes, the per-month equivalent is absurd (~$120/month). But you're not buying a month. You're buying a test. One week tells you exactly how many people are in your Beeline in your area. If the answer is "enough to justify the price," upgrade. If it's "three bots and a guy named Steve," cancel.

**Check web pricing vs iOS pricing.** Apple takes a 30% cut of in-app purchases, and Bumble passes that cost directly to you. Open Bumble on your computer, log in at bumble.com, and check the prices there. Web pricing is consistently 15-30% cheaper. Same product. Less Apple tax.

**Watch for seasonal discounts.** Bumble runs promos around Valentine's Day, New Year's, and other "you're probably feeling lonely" holidays. Not guaranteed, but worth waiting for if you're not in a rush.

**Do the lifetime math.** $270 for lifetime vs $55-60/month means you break even in about 5 months. If you know you'll use Bumble for longer than that, lifetime is the better financial move. If you're not sure, don't commit to a lifetime relationship with a dating app before you're ready. The irony would be too much.

## How to Cancel Bumble Premium (Before They Auto-Renew Your Soul)

Bumble will charge you again unless you cancel at least 24 hours before your renewal date. They don't warn you. They don't send a friendly reminder email. They just take the money like you agreed to it. Which, technically, you did. Buried in paragraph 47 of the terms you never read.

### On iPhone

1. Open **Settings** on your phone.
2. Tap your **Apple ID** at the top.
3. Tap **Subscriptions**.
4. Find **Bumble** and tap it.
5. Tap **Cancel Subscription**.

### On Android

1. Open the **Google Play Store**.
2. Tap your **profile icon** in the top right.
3. Tap **Payments & subscriptions** then **Subscriptions**.
4. Find **Bumble** and tap **Cancel**.

### On the Web

1. Go to **bumble.com** and log in.
2. Click your **profile icon** then **Settings**.
3. Scroll to **Manage Subscription** and click **Unsubscribe**.

No pro-rated refunds, by the way. If you cancel on day 2 of a monthly plan, you still get access until the end of the period, but you don't get 28 days of money back. Exceptions: California residents get a 3-business-day cooling-off period, and EU/UK users get 14 days under consumer protection laws. Everyone else? Should've read the fine print. Or, you know, this article.

## FAQ

### Is Bumble Premium worth it for guys?

Probably not, unless you tick very specific boxes. The women-message-first mechanic means even if you see every single person in your Beeline, they still have to open the conversation. Premium doesn't fix that bottleneck. You can match with 20 people from your Beeline and still sit there in silence like a phone on Do Not Disturb. If you're in a [major city](/blog/bumble-statistics), already have decent photos, and want to save time by seeing who likes you, it can be worth it. Everyone else? Try Boost first or just fix your profile.

### Can people see if you have Bumble Premium?

No. There's no badge, no crown, no golden border. Your financial desperation is between you and your credit card statement. Nobody on the other end will ever know you paid $60 to see them in your Beeline. Your secret shame is safe.

### What's the difference between Bumble Premium and Premium Plus?

Premium gives you Beeline, Incognito Mode, Travel Mode, and Advanced Filters on top of everything in Boost. Premium+ adds Priority Likes (your profile shown sooner), 2 Compliments per week, 2 Spotlights per week, and 10 SuperSwipes per week. The gap between them is mostly about visibility. Premium lets you see who likes you. Premium+ tries to make more people like you. Whether that second part works is... debatable.

### Why is Bumble Premium so expensive?

Because Bumble knows you're lonely and willing to pay. But also because their revenue dropped nearly 10% last year, they cut 30% of staff, and they slashed their marketing budget by 80%. Fewer users are paying, so Bumble needs to squeeze more from the ones who do. Expect prices to stay high or climb higher. You're not paying for features. You're subsidizing a company in financial triage.

### Is there a Bumble Premium free trial?

Bumble occasionally offers limited free trials, usually to new users or lapsed subscribers they're trying to win back. There's no reliable way to trigger one on demand. Your best bet is to create your account, then wait a few days before paying for anything. Bumble tends to dangle trials in front of people who seem interested but haven't opened their wallet yet. Playing hard to get works on dating apps AND the apps themselves, apparently.

### How does Bumble Premium compare to Tinder Gold?

[Tinder Gold](/blog/is-tinder-gold-worth-it) costs roughly $30/month and gives you the same headline feature: seeing who liked you. Bumble Premium costs nearly double for the equivalent. Both apps offer travel/passport features and advanced filtering at the premium tier. The main difference is Bumble's women-message-first mechanic, which Premium doesn't override. On pure value, Tinder Gold offers more for less. On app preference? That's between you and [which app you actually prefer](/blog/tinder-vs-bumble).
