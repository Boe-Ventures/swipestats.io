## Can You Use Hinge for Friends? (The Short, Painful Answer)

Let me save you three months of confused matches and uncomfortable "so I'm actually just looking for friends" conversations. Hinge is a dating app. That's it. No friend mode. No platonic toggle hiding in the settings somewhere. No secret menu you can unlock by swiping in a specific pattern like the Konami code.

Hinge's entire brand is "designed to be deleted" because you found a relationship. Not because you found a drinking buddy.

But people try it anyway. Reddit threads on this topic read like a civil war. One camp swears they met their closest friend through Hinge. The other camp calls it a waste of time and, frankly, kind of a dick move. Both sides have a point.

Here's the core problem. **70% of Hinge users are looking for commitment**. That means when you match with someone and drop the "just looking for friends" bomb, you're disappointing someone who thought they had a shot at romance. It's the dating app equivalent of inviting someone to dinner and then telling them you already ate.

If you're curious whether Hinge is even worth your time for its intended purpose, check out our [Hinge review](/blog/hinge-review) first.

## Why You're Googling This (And Why It Doesn't Make You Pathetic)

Let's address the elephant in the room. You're searching "hinge for friends" because making friends as an adult is brutally, absurdly hard. And nobody prepared you for it.

The numbers are genuinely depressing. **36% of all Americans** report feeling lonely. Among young adults, that number jumps to **61%**. Men have it the worst. **1 in 6 men** report having zero close friendships. That's up 5x since 1990. Zero. Not "we've drifted apart." Zero.

The US Surgeon General declared loneliness a public health crisis in 2023 because social isolation carries the same health risk as smoking 15 cigarettes a day. Let that sink in. Your lack of a group chat is literally killing you.

"How to make friends as an adult" was Google's number one "how to" search in 2022. You're not weird. You're statistically normal. Which, honestly, might be worse.

So yeah. You already have Hinge on your phone. The temptation to dual-purpose it makes complete sense. You're already swiping. Why not swipe for friends too? Because it doesn't work. That's why.

## What Actually Happens When You Try (Spoiler: It's Rough)

I appreciate the optimism. Really. But let me walk you through what making friends on Hinge actually looks like in practice.

One writer from Time Out spent three months on four different dating apps trying to make platonic connections. **50+ text conversations**. Dozens of hours invested. The grand total of friends made from Hinge? One. A single friend. That's a worse conversion rate than a cold email campaign run by your nephew who just took a marketing course.

The expectations mismatch is brutal. You match with someone. They're excited. They've crafted a witty opener referencing your prompt about pasta. Then you hit them with the "so I'm actually not looking to date anyone." The silence that follows is deafening. Or worse, they get angry. And honestly? Fair enough.

The gender dynamics make it even messier. Women who list "looking for friends" on dating apps get skepticism from every direction. Half the people think it's code for "I want attention but no commitment." Men doing it get side-eyes from everyone, because a guy on a [dating app](/blog/best-dating-apps) saying he wants friends reads like a guy who struck out and is rebranding.

Here's the thing that actually works, though. Friendships CAN emerge from failed dates. That's the most common path. You go on a date, the chemistry isn't there romantically, but you genuinely enjoy talking to each other. That's organic. That's real. Trying to force platonic connections through a romance-optimized algorithm? That's fighting the current.

And then there's the trope everyone's thinking about. "She said she uses Hinge just for friends." Sure, Jan.

## Hinge Used to Be a Friends App (Sort Of)

Here's a fun piece of history that makes this whole situation more ironic. When Hinge launched in 2012, it was built on a "friends of friends" model using Facebook's social graph. You could only see people within two or three degrees of your social circle.

This made the whole experience feel less like cold-swiping strangers and more like being set up by mutual friends at a house party. It was warmer. More personal. The kind of environment where platonic connections could actually happen naturally.

Then Facebook's 2018 API restrictions killed the feature. Hinge pivoted to a fully algorithmic model, and the social layer evaporated. The "friends" part of Hinge died five years before you thought to search for it.

Now [the Hinge algorithm](/blog/hinge-algorithm) decides who you see based on your preferences, behavior, and whatever black-box magic it runs behind the scenes. The social connective tissue that once made Hinge unique is gone. RIP.

## Actual Apps for Making Friends (That Won't Waste Everyone's Time)

Alright. You can't use Hinge for friends. What can you actually use? I've put these through their paces so your touch-starved ass doesn't have to.

### Bumble BFF

The obvious answer. Bumble launched BFF mode years ago, but it was always the neglected middle child of the Bumble family. Then in September 2025, they gave it a major overhaul. New Groups feature. Events. An in-app calendar built on the Geneva platform.

Is it perfect? God, no. Articles with titles like "Bumble BFF Sucks For Making Friends, But It's the Only Option" tell you everything you need to know about the state of friendship apps. But **15% of Bumble users** were on BFF mode by end of 2021 (up from 10% the prior year), so at least the user base is growing.

The dirty secret? Bumble BFF generated roughly **$1 million** against Bumble's **$1 billion+ total revenue** in 2023. Friendship isn't profitable. Which means the feature will always be an afterthought. But it's still your best swipe-based option. For a deeper look at [Bumble overall](/blog/bumble-review), we've got you covered.

### Meetup

Event-based. **60 million members**. Free to join. The beauty of Meetup is that you're not sitting across from a stranger trying to manufacture a connection from nothing. You're at a hiking group or a board game night or a coding workshop, and friendship happens as a side effect of doing something together.

Less awkward than one-on-one friend-swiping. More effective than hoping your coworkers want to hang out after 5 PM. If you're comparing your options across multiple platforms, our [dating app statistics](/blog/dating-app-statistics) page breaks down user bases in detail.

### The New Wave (2025-2026)

The friendship app market is booming. **$16 million in US consumer spending** with **4.3 million downloads** in 2025. Here's what's worth knowing about:

- **Timeleft**: Curated dinners with strangers. You show up, sit down, and make conversation with people who are equally desperate for human connection. It's like a dinner party where nobody knows each other and everyone pretends that's fine.
- **Patook**: Strictly platonic. Has a flirt detection algorithm that boots anyone trying to use it for dating. Finally, an app that treats "just friends" like it means something.
- **Friending**: Launched March 2026. Limits chat features to push face-to-face meetups. The idea is that texting strangers for weeks doesn't create friendships. Actually seeing them does. Revolutionary concept.

### The 2026 Trend: Friction-Maxxing

The hot move right now is rejecting frictionless digital connection entirely. Hobbies. Classes. Community events. Running clubs. Pottery workshops. Places where you can't hide behind a screen and where showing up consistently is the whole point.

It takes roughly 200 hours of shared time to build a close friendship. No app can speed-run that for you. They can only get you in the same room with someone. The rest is on you.

## FAQ

### Is Hinge for dating or friends?

Dating. Full stop. Hinge markets itself as "designed to be deleted" because you found a romantic partner, not because you completed your friend roster. There's no friend mode, no platonic setting, and no indication they plan to build one. If you want the full rundown on what [Hinge actually offers](/blog/is-hinge-a-good-dating-app), we've written about that too.

### Can you use Hinge for friends?

Technically, yes. You can also use a screwdriver as a hammer. Doesn't mean you should. Most Hinge users are looking for romance, so putting "just here for friends" in your [Hinge bio](/blog/hinge-bio) will confuse, disappoint, or annoy the majority of people you match with. It's not illegal. It's just inconsiderate.

### What's the best dating app for friends?

Bumble BFF is your best bet if you want the swipe-based experience. Meetup is better if you'd rather bond over shared activities than shared awkwardness. For the full breakdown of how [Hinge stacks up against Bumble](/blog/hinge-vs-bumble) on features, we've compared them side by side.

### Is it OK to use dating apps just for friendship?

Be upfront about it. That's the bare minimum. Put it in your profile. Mention it early. Don't let someone invest emotional energy thinking they're on a date when you're conducting a friend audition. Transparency won't fix the fundamental mismatch, but at least nobody feels tricked.

### Why is making friends as an adult so hard?

Because the structures that used to create friendships (school, college, shared dorms, organized sports) disappear after your twenties. You're left with work and... that's basically it. The loneliness epidemic isn't a personal failure. It's a structural one. Modern life optimized for productivity, not connection. And here we are, Googling "can you use Hinge to make friends" at 11 PM on a Tuesday.

### Did Hinge ever have a friends feature?

Sort of. Hinge's original 2012 model used Facebook's social graph to show you friends of friends, which made the experience feel more personal and social. Facebook's 2018 API changes killed that feature. Now it's a standard algorithmic dating app. The irony is not lost on anyone.
