## What a Tinder Subscription Actually Costs in 2026 (Spoiler: It Depends on How Old You Are)

Let's start with the numbers. Here's the current tinder subscription pricing as of March 2026:

| Tier | Weekly | Monthly | 6-Month (per mo.) |
|------|--------|---------|--------------------|
| Plus | $12.99 | $24.99 | $16.66 |
| Gold | $18.99 | $39.99 | $23.33 |
| Platinum | $24.99 | $49.99 | $29.99 |
| Select | N/A | $499.00 | N/A |

Those weekly plans are a newer addition. And they're a trap. Paying $12.99 per week for Plus works out to roughly $52/month. That's more than Platinum's monthly rate. Tinder is betting you're bad at math. (Based on the average profile I see, they're probably right.)

Now here's the fun part. Those prices above? They might not be YOUR prices.

Tinder uses **dynamic pricing**. If you're under 28, you get lower tinder subscription prices. If you're 28 or older, congratulations on your wisdom and your bigger bill. Location matters too. And the length of your subscription changes the per-month cost.

This isn't a secret. It's so not-a-secret that it became a **$60.5 million lawsuit**. A class action filed in 2015 accused Tinder of age-based pricing discrimination. Preliminary approval came in January 2026, with a final hearing scheduled for May 2026. About **268,000 eligible California residents** might get a piece of that. So if you've been a Tinder subscriber in California and felt like the app was taxing you for getting older, your suspicion was legally correct.

Oh, and one more thing. Legacy subscribers (people who've been paying since the earlier pricing structures) sometimes get better features than new subscribers at the same tier. Tinder doesn't advertise this. Because of course they don't.

### The Free Tier: Tinder's Loss Leader

Before we talk paid tiers, let's acknowledge what free Tinder gives you: **100 right swipes per day**, a parade of ads, and none of the premium features. It's the dating app equivalent of a test drive where the salesman sits in the passenger seat sighing loudly every time you touch something.

For casual swipers who log in a few times a week, free is honestly fine. But if you're serious about this (and the fact that you're reading a 3,000-word breakdown of tinder subscription costs suggests you might be), you're going to hit walls fast. Walls designed by a billion-dollar company whose entire business model is making "free" feel just frustrating enough that you reach for your credit card.

## Tinder Plus: The "I Just Hate Ads and Swipe Limits" Tier ($24.99/mo)

Here's what Plus gets you:

- **Unlimited likes** (no more daily cap)
- **Unlimited rewinds** (for when your thumb has opinions your brain doesn't share)
- **Passport** (change your location, swipe anywhere)
- **Ad-free experience** (finally)
- **Incognito mode** (hide from people you know, which is half the reason some of you are here)

That's it. No boosts. No Super Likes. No "See Who Likes You." Just the basics without the artificial restrictions that Tinder built specifically to annoy you into paying.

**Who this is for:** Heavy swipers hitting that 100/day cap. If you're the type who burns through your daily likes before your morning coffee gets cold, Plus removes the leash. Also great if the ads make you want to throw your phone into traffic.

**Who should skip it:** Casual users who swipe 20-30 times a day. You're not hitting the cap, the ads aren't killing you, and you'd be paying $25/month for features you'll barely touch. That's a gym membership for your thumb that you'll use twice.

**The honest verdict:** Plus is the sensible Honda Civic of Tinder subscriptions. It does what it needs to do. No flash, no nonsense. If your only complaints about Tinder are "too many ads" and "not enough swipes," this is your tier and you should stop reading here.

But you won't stop reading, will you? Because deep down you want to know if spending more money will fix your love life. (Spoiler from someone who's tested every tier: it won't. But it might make the process less annoying.)

## Tinder Gold: The One Most People Actually Want ($39.99/mo)

Gold includes everything in Plus, and adds:

- **See Who Likes You** (this is the big one)
- **5 Super Likes per week** (they used to be free, one per day for everyone, because Tinder giveth and Tinder taketh away)
- **1 Boost per month** (30 minutes at the top of the stack)
- **Daily Top Picks** (curated profiles Tinder thinks you'll like)

Let me be blunt about the "See Who Likes You" feature. It turns Tinder from a slot machine into a buffet. Instead of mindlessly swiping and hoping someone on the other end did the same, you can see exactly who's already interested. You can scroll through your admirers (all three of them, but still) and decide from there.

This single feature is why Gold is consistently described as the most popular tinder subscription tier. And it's why the jump from Plus to Gold is worth the extra $15 for most people, while the jump from Gold to Platinum rarely is.

The Super Likes are a nice bonus. They do genuinely increase your match rate. The Boost is fine. One per month won't change your life, but it's something. The Top Picks are algorithmic suggestions that range from "oh, interesting" to "Tinder, do you even know me?"

**Who this is for:** Anyone who's tired of the swiping lottery and wants to see their cards before playing. If you're a guy dealing with the typical [male match rate](/blog/tinder-statistics) of 1-2%, knowing who already likes you saves an absurd amount of time.

**The honest verdict:** Gold is the answer to "is Tinder Plus worth it... or should I just go one tier higher?" For most serious users, this is the ceiling. Everything above this is diminishing returns wearing a fancy hat.

## Tinder Platinum: Throwing Money at the Algorithm ($49.99/mo)

Platinum includes everything in Gold, plus:

- **Message Before Matching** (requires using a Super Like, 140 character max)
- **Prioritized Likes** (your swipes get seen sooner)
- **See Likes Sent in the last 7 days** (review your recent activity)

The headliner here is Message Before Matching. Tinder claims it produces a **25% higher match rate**. Sounds great on paper. In practice, you need to burn a Super Like to send one, and you get 140 characters to work with. That's shorter than a tweet. You're writing a pickup line with the word count of a fortune cookie.

Here's some real data though. YouTube creator Zackary Smigel (in a video with over 1M views) tracked his results across tiers. Free tier: **3 matches per month**. Gold: **4.6 matches per month**. Platinum: **14.6 matches per month**. That's a massive jump. He even used SwipeStats to analyze his data, which, okay, shameless plug, but the numbers are the numbers.

But (and this is a big but, no pun intended) that kind of jump doesn't happen in a vacuum. The tinder platinum subscription works best when your profile is already strong enough that a prioritized like actually converts. If your photos look like you took them during a hostage situation, getting seen faster just means getting rejected faster.

**Who this is for:** Users in competitive urban markets (NYC, LA, London) who already have a dialed-in profile and want to squeeze out extra matches. If you've already optimized your photos, your bio is tight, and Gold isn't moving the needle enough, Platinum might push you over the edge.

**Who should skip it:** Everyone else. If your profile needs work, the extra $10/month over Gold is a waste. That money would be better spent on a dating photographer or literally anything that improves what people see when they find you.

## Tinder Select: The $500/Month Cold-Call Feature

And now for the absurd one.

**$499 per month.** Invite-only. Here's what you get for the price of a reasonable car payment:

- **DM up to 2 people per week** without matching first
- **SELECT Mode badge** on your profile
- **Profile shown unblurred** in the Likes You grid

The requirements to even qualify: verified photo, bio with 15+ characters, 5 listed interests, and 4+ photos. So you need to put in bare minimum effort AND pay $500/month. Groundbreaking.

Let me put this in perspective. YouTuber Penguinz0 made a video about Select that racked up **1.86 million views**. His take? You're "paying $500 a month to cold call like a telemarketer." And honestly, even telemarketers get more than 2 calls per week.

For context, after Match Group acquired The League in 2022, that app's top tier runs about **$1,000 per week**. So Select is actually the budget option in the "insane money for dating apps" category. Which is like saying you got a good deal on a gold-plated toilet. Technically true. Still stupid.

**Who this is for:** People with more money than sense who want a status symbol on a dating app. If you're the type who buys bottle service at clubs not for the alcohol but for the table, Select is your energy.

**The honest verdict:** This is a vanity product. Not a dating tool. The 2 DMs per week rate means you're paying $250 per unsolicited message to a stranger. I've seen better ROI on lottery tickets. I've seen better ROI on scratch-offs bought at a gas station in the middle of nowhere. If you're reading this and seriously considering Select, close this tab, open your banking app, and donate that $499 to literally any cause. You'll feel better about yourself, and your match rate will be exactly the same.

## Which Tinder Subscription Is Actually Worth It? (The Honest Verdict)

Alright, let me save you the mental gymnastics. Here's the breakdown by user type:

- **Casual swipers** (a few times a week): Stay free. You're not using the app enough to justify paying for it.
- **Heavy swipers** (daily, hitting the cap): **Plus**. Remove the limits, ditch the ads, keep it simple.
- **Serious daters** who want to see their likes: **Gold**. This is the recommendation for most people reading this post. The "See Who Likes You" feature alone justifies the tinder subscription cost.
- **Competitive markets** with an already-strong profile: **Maybe Platinum**. Emphasis on "maybe" and "already-strong."
- **Nobody reasonable**: Select.

### The Cancel-and-Resubscribe Trick

Here's something Tinder doesn't put on a billboard. If you cancel your subscription and wait a few days, Tinder often sends you a discounted offer to come back. Sometimes 40-50% off. They'd rather have you paying something than nothing. It's the dating app equivalent of threatening to cancel your cable. Works about as often, too.

### The Alternative Nobody Considers

Instead of upgrading from Gold to Platinum ($10/month more), buy **5 individual Boosts for about $35**. You get more targeted visibility spikes than Platinum's single monthly Boost, and you keep the Gold features. More bang for your buck. ## FAQ

### How do I cancel my Tinder subscription?

Go to your phone's subscription settings (not the Tinder app itself). On iPhone, it's Settings > Apple ID > Subscriptions. On Android, it's Google Play Store > Subscriptions. Tinder makes this deliberately non-obvious. We wrote a full guide on [how to cancel Tinder Gold](/blog/how-to-cancel-tinder-gold) if you need the step-by-step.

### Does Tinder charge more based on age?

Yes. Users 28 and older consistently see higher prices. This practice led to a **$60.5 million class action settlement** with approximately 268,000 eligible California residents. The final hearing is scheduled for May 2026.

### Can I get a refund on my Tinder subscription?

Generally, no. Tinder's refund policy is about as generous as their free tier. Apple and Google sometimes grant refunds if you contact them directly, but don't count on it. Cancel before your renewal date or accept the loss.

### Is Tinder Plus worth it in 2026?

Depends entirely on how much you swipe. If you're hitting the 100-like daily cap regularly, Plus removes that ceiling and kills the ads. If you swipe casually, you're paying for features you won't use. Check out the full Tinder Plus price breakdown for the details.

### What is Tinder Gold?

Tinder Gold is the mid-tier subscription ($39.99/mo) that includes everything in Plus, plus the "See Who Likes You" feature, 5 weekly Super Likes, 1 monthly Boost, and daily Top Picks. It's the most popular paid tier for a reason. We go deeper in the [Plus vs Gold comparison](/blog/tinder-plus-vs-tinder-gold).

### What's the difference between Tinder Gold and Platinum?

Gold lets you see who likes you. Platinum adds the ability to message before matching (via Super Like), prioritized likes, and a 7-day sent-likes history. The real question is whether that's worth an extra $10/month. For most people, no. Read the full [Tinder Platinum breakdown](/blog/tinder-platinum) if you're on the fence.
