<TLDR>

No text proves somebody's private feelings. The useful question is whether the interaction is repeatedly reciprocal:

- she initiates sometimes;
- she answers and adds detail;
- she asks about you;
- she remembers prior conversations;
- she responds positively to flirting;
- she helps make or reschedule plans.

When the pattern is warm, ask her out. A clear answer is better than amateur forensics.

</TLDR>

## Stronger Signals Are Behavioral

### She contributes to the conversation

She does not merely answer; she introduces topics, tells stories, sends things that reminded her of you, or asks follow-up questions. That shows investment in the interaction, although it still does not prove romantic interest.

### She initiates sometimes

Repeated initiation is more informative than one fast reply. It means the conversation is not being sustained entirely by your prompts.

### She makes meeting easier

The clearest practical signal is cooperation with a plan:

> Thursday is difficult, but I can do Saturday afternoon.

An alternative is not guaranteed romance, but it is meaningful evidence that she wants to spend time together.

### Flirting is returned

She may compliment you, tease back, name attraction, or respond warmly when you express interest. Increase intensity gradually and ask before sexual content.

## Weak Signals People Overinterpret

### Response time

Fast replies can reflect availability; slow replies can reflect work, sleep, caregiving, notification habits, or low interest. One interval proves very little.

### Message length

Some people send paragraphs to everyone. Others communicate warmly in short bursts. Compare the ongoing interaction, not an internet rule about word count.

### Emojis and punctuation

A heart, exclamation mark, or “haha” depends on the person's normal style and context. It is not a diagnostic test.

### “Good morning” texts

They can show attention, habit, friendship, or romance. Notice whether the broader relationship also moves toward closeness and plans.

## Mixed Signals

Mixed behavior often means the evidence is mixed. Do not convert uncertainty into a flattering explanation or a harsh accusation. Ask once:

> I like talking with you and would like to take you on a date. Are you free Saturday afternoon?

Possible outcomes:

- **Yes:** make the plan.
- **No:** accept it.
- **Cannot, but proposes another time:** continue planning.
- **Repeated vagueness with no alternative:** step back.
- **No response:** do not build a campaign around getting one.

## What SwipeStats Can and Cannot Tell You

If you upload an export containing only your outgoing messages, SwipeStats can identify where **you sent more messages** or where your messaging continued over more days. It cannot prove that she replied, liked you, ghosted you, or wanted a date.

Even a complete two-sided conversation would support interpretation, not mind reading. Tone models can miss sarcasm, personal style, and offline context.

## Avoid These “Tests”

- making her jealous;
- deliberately withdrawing to provoke pursuit;
- asking friends to message her;
- sending sexual content to measure her reaction;
- monitoring read receipts or activity indicators obsessively;
- treating a boundary as a challenge.
