## Is Bumble or Hinge Better? (The Numbers Already Answered This)

Let me save you 2,000 words of deliberation: in 2026, one of these apps grew revenue by 26% for the fourth consecutive year while the other cut its marketing budget by 80% and laid off a third of its entire workforce.

If you're trying to decide between hinge or bumble, the financial data reads like a boxing scorecard after a knockout. Hinge is celebrating. Bumble is on life support wondering what went wrong. And before you say "revenue doesn't equal user experience," stick around. Because the wedding data, match rates, and free-tier comparison all tell the same story.

I've been running SwipeStats and analyzing dating app data from **7,000+ profiles and 294 million swipes** for years now. I've watched Bumble's women-first model go from revolutionary to exhausting. I've watched Hinge's "designed to be deleted" tagline go from annoying marketing speak to something that actually reflects reality. And I've read enough user complaints about both apps to fill a therapy waiting room.

So let's get into it. If you want the [full feature-by-feature breakdown](/blog/hinge-vs-bumble), we already wrote that. This is different. This is the data picking a winner.

## The Numbers Tell the Whole Story (Or: One App Is Circling the Drain)

Let's start with the money, because money doesn't lie (unlike your Tinder bio that says you're 6'1").

| Metric | Hinge (2025) | Bumble (2025) |
|---|---|---|
| **Revenue** | $691M (+26% YoY) | $966M (-10% YoY) |
| **Paying Subscribers** | 1.9M (+17% YoY) | 3.6M (-16% YoY) |
| **User Base** | 32M registered, 22M DAU | 50M+ MAU |
| **Market Share** | ~18% (growing) | ~25% (shrinking) |
| **Workforce Changes** | Hiring | Laid off 240 employees (30%) |
| **Growth Trajectory** | 4th year of 25%+ growth | Q4 revenue down 14% |

Read that table again. Bumble still makes more money and has more users. But every single trend line points in the wrong direction. Revenue down. Paying users down. A 30% workforce reduction in June 2025. They cut performance marketing by 80%, which is corporate speak for "we're bleeding cash and hoping nobody notices."

Hinge, meanwhile, has been growing at 25%+ for four straight years. Their paying subscribers jumped 17% to 1.9 million. That's not a fluke. That's a pattern that says people are voting with their wallets.

Bumble has the bigger number today. Hinge has the momentum. And if you've ever watched a sports team blow a lead in the fourth quarter, you know which metric matters more.

## Who's Actually Getting Married Off These Apps? (Spoiler: Not Bumble)

Here's where things get brutal for Bumble fans. The Knot's 2025 Real Weddings Study found that **36% of app-facilitated weddings came from Hinge**. Bumble? **20%.** Even Tinder beat Bumble at 25%, and Tinder is the app people use when they explicitly don't want a relationship.

Think about that. The app with the smaller user base is producing nearly twice as many marriages. That's not a coincidence. That's a product that actually works for what it says it does.

A 2024 Stanford study backs this up: **77% of Hinge users say they're seeking serious relationships**, compared to 55% on Bumble. When more than three-quarters of an app's users want something real, the conversations and the effort level just hit differently.

The downstream numbers confirm it. Hinge conversations are **40% more likely to result in a planned date** than Bumble conversations. About **90% of Hinge matches convert to a first date**, vs roughly 80% on Bumble. And **72% of Hinge first dates lead to a second date**. That's a conversion funnel most startups would sell a kidney for.

Bumble built its brand on being the "relationship app for women." Hinge is quietly building the relationship app for everybody.

## Hinge or Bumble for Guys? (The Brutal Math)

Alright fellas, let's talk about your favorite subject: why your phone is drier than a lecture on municipal zoning laws.

The average match rate for men on Bumble is about **3%**. Three. Percent. That means for every 100 swipes, you get maybe 3 matches. And from our analysis of 7,000+ profiles and 294M swipes, the **median male match rate across all dating apps is just 2.04%**. The top 10% of men get 67% of all matches. If those numbers don't make you want to flip your phone into a river, you're not paying attention.

But here's where it gets worse on Bumble specifically. You match with someone and then you wait. And wait. And stare at your phone like it owes you money. Because she has to message first within 24 hours or the match disappears forever. It's like winning a raffle ticket and then being told you have to stand in a specific spot at a specific time or you lose the prize. Fun.

On Hinge, you get **8 likes per day**. Sounds pathetic compared to Bumble's ~100 swipes. But those 8 likes let you send a comment on a specific photo or prompt. You're not throwing your resume into a pile of 500 other identical resumes. You're walking up to someone and saying something specific. That's a fundamentally different game.

Eight targeted likes with comment ability vs 100 anonymous swipes with a 3% match rate and a 24-hour prayer window. I'm no mathematician (I'm a guy who stares at [Hinge statistics](/blog/hinge-statistics) all day), but that math doesn't favor Bumble.

## Hinge or Bumble for Women? (It's Closer Than You Think)

This is where I have to give Bumble some credit. The "women message first" mechanic is genuinely useful if you hate opening your inbox to a wall of "hey" and "ur hot" messages. Bumble set out to give women control over the conversation, and it does that. Full stop.

But here's the thing nobody at Bumble wants to talk about: the 24-hour message-first pressure is exhausting. I've talked to dozens of women who say the same thing. "I match with someone, forget to message, and it expires." "I don't know what to say first." "I feel guilty letting matches expire but I'm tired of always initiating." It's a feature that sounded revolutionary in 2014 and now feels like a chore.

On Hinge, men send comments on specific photos and prompts. Which means women receive thoughtful (or at least semi-thoughtful) openers instead of the conversational equivalent of a wet handshake. The men do the work. And honestly? Based on the data, the conversations that result from those prompt-based openers are just better. More specific. More engaging. More likely to actually go somewhere.

Gender ratios are nearly identical: Hinge is **64% men / 36% women**, Bumble is **62.4% men / 37.6% women**. Both skew male, because apparently men are incapable of meeting people at the grocery store like normal human beings (myself included).

If inbox control and safety are your top priorities, Bumble still has an edge. If you're tired of being the one who always has to start the conversation, Hinge wins.

## The Free Tier Is Where Hinge Buries Bumble

This is the section that should make Bumble's product team lose sleep at night. Let's compare what you get for zero dollars.

| Feature | Hinge Free | Bumble Free |
|---|---|---|
| **Daily Likes/Swipes** | 8 likes | ~100 swipes |
| **See Who Liked You** | Yes (one at a time) | No |
| **Change Location** | Yes, free | No (Premium only) |
| **Dealbreaker Filters** | Yes, free | 1 advanced filter |
| **Cost to Unlock Equivalent** | $0 | ~$55/month (Premium+) |

Read that last row. Hinge gives away for free what Bumble charges $55/month for. Dealbreaker filters that actually work. The ability to change your location without paying. Seeing who liked you. These aren't luxury features. These are basic "let me use the app effectively" features that Bumble locks behind a paywall because their revenue is falling off a cliff and they need the cash.

Hinge's free tier is genuinely usable. You can [get your free likes on Hinge](/blog/how-many-free-likes-on-hinge), set your dealbreakers, and actually see who's interested in you. It's a complete product at $0. Bumble's free tier feels like a demo version that constantly nags you to upgrade. And that's coming from someone who recommends paying for dating apps if you're serious about results.

## When Bumble Actually Makes More Sense (Yes, There Are Reasons)

Look, I just spent 1,500 words dunking on Bumble, so let me be fair. There are real scenarios where Bumble is the better choice.

- **You want sheer volume.** 100 swipes per day vs 8 likes. If you're a "spray and pray" person (no judgment, ok maybe a little), Bumble gives you more shots on goal.
- **You're outside the US, UK, or Australia.** Hinge operates in about 20 countries. Bumble is in 150+. If you're swiping in Bangkok or Buenos Aires, Bumble might be your only option that isn't Tinder.
- **You want BFF mode.** Bumble BFF is legitimately useful if you've just moved to a new city and need friends. Hinge doesn't do this at all. Moving to a new city is lonely. This feature deserves respect.
- **You're over 50.** Bumble tends to have a wider active age range in many markets. Hinge skews younger.
- **The 24-hour urgency mechanic works for you.** Some people thrive under deadlines. If knowing a match expires in 24 hours makes you message faster, Bumble's mechanic is a feature, not a bug.

None of these make Bumble the better overall app. But they make it the better app for specific people in specific situations. And pretending otherwise would be dishonest.

## FAQ

### Is Hinge or Bumble more popular?

Bumble has more users globally (50M+ MAU vs Hinge's 32M registered). But Hinge's daily active user count (22M DAU) suggests higher engagement per user. In the US, UK, and Australia, they're getting closer every quarter. Globally, Bumble still has the edge because it's available in 150+ countries versus Hinge's ~20.

### Is Bumble or Hinge better for relationships?

Hinge, and it's not particularly close. **36% of app-met weddings** come from Hinge vs 20% from Bumble. 77% of Hinge users report seeking serious relationships. Hinge conversations are 40% more likely to result in a date. If you want a relationship, Hinge is the statistically better bet. Check if [Hinge X is worth it](/blog/is-hinge-x-worth-it) if you want to go all-in.

### Is Bumble in decline?

The numbers say yes. Revenue down 10% year-over-year. Paying subscribers down 16%. A 30% workforce reduction. Q4 2025 revenue down 14%. Bumble isn't dead, but it's clearly in a restructuring phase. The question isn't whether Bumble is declining. It's whether the new leadership can reverse it before Hinge eats the rest of their market share.

### Is Hinge or Bumble better for over 40?

This depends on your market. Bumble generally has a wider active age range and more users over 40 in most cities. Hinge skews younger (the "designed to be deleted" branding attracts 25-35 year olds). But in major metros like New York, London, and Sydney, Hinge has a solid 40+ population. Try both. Seriously. Download them, spend a week on each, and see where the people you're attracted to actually are. No blog post can replace that experiment.

### Can I use both Hinge and Bumble at the same time?

Yes, and honestly you probably should if you're seriously looking. Running both apps doubles your exposure and lets you compare results firsthand. Just don't spread yourself so thin that you're sending "hey" to everyone because you're juggling too many conversations. That defeats the purpose. Quality on two apps beats quantity on five.
