<TLDR>

### Match Note in 30 Seconds

- A Match Note can contain up to 255 characters.
- It appears after a mutual match, before the other person starts the conversation.
- The recipient can acknowledge it and continue or unmatch.
- Existing matches do not see a note added later.
- Use it for context that materially affects compatibility, not a generic slogan.

</TLDR>

## What Is a Hinge Match Note?

A Match Note is a short compatibility note attached to the matching step. Hinge suggests using it for a personal value, relationship intention, accessibility or communication need, or another fact a match should know upfront.

It is not a public prompt shown to everyone browsing the profile. Hinge says it appears after both people have liked each other. Before completing the match and starting the conversation, the recipient views the note and chooses to acknowledge it or unmatch.

That makes the note best suited to information that helps both people decide whether continuing makes sense.

## How to Add a Match Note

1. Tap the Profile Photo in the lower-right corner.
2. Tap the Pencil icon to edit the profile.
3. Scroll to **Match Note**.
4. Write up to 255 characters.
5. Mark the note as visible if you want it automatically shown to new matches.

The note applies to future matches. Hinge says existing matches should not see a Match Note added after the match was created.

## What Should You Write?

Use the note when the information would change someone's decision to start a conversation. A useful note is specific, calm, and easy to acknowledge.

### Relationship intention

> Looking for a long-term relationship and taking things slowly. I would rather meet for coffee than text for weeks.

### Schedule or lifestyle

> I work nights, so my reply times can be unusual. Weekend afternoons are usually best for a first date.

### Accessibility or communication

> I am hard of hearing and prefer quieter first-date spots. Happy to choose somewhere that works for both of us.

### Family or location context

> Co-parent with a week-on, week-off schedule and plan to stay in this city. Sharing early so expectations are clear.

These are examples, not scripts to copy blindly. Rewrite the idea in your own voice and only disclose what feels safe.

## What Not to Put in a Match Note

Avoid:

- a long list of people who should not match with you;
- insults about previous dates;
- demands for an immediate phone number or off-app contact;
- sensitive personal details that create a safety risk;
- a generic joke that provides no compatibility context;
- language that conflicts with Hinge's community guidelines.

Direct does not need to mean hostile. “I do not drink, and bars are not my preferred first date” gives someone useful information. “No drinkers, no exceptions, do not waste my time” frames the same fact as a confrontation.

## Match Note vs. Profile Prompt

| Surface | Who sees it | Best use |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Profile prompt | People reviewing the profile | Personality, interests, and conversation hooks |
| Match Note | A person after a mutual Like | Important context before the conversation begins |

If the idea makes the profile more interesting to almost everyone, it probably belongs in a prompt. If it helps a new match make an informed compatibility decision, it may belong in the Match Note.

## A Simple Writing Formula

Try:

**context + practical impact + inviting next step**

For example:

> Sober for three years, so I skip bar dates. Coffee, galleries, and long walks are all great—what would you pick?

The context is clear, the practical impact is easy to understand, and the final question opens a conversation instead of issuing a test.

## FAQ

### Is Match Note public?

No. Hinge describes it as appearing after both people have liked each other, during the match flow.

### Does the other person have to read it?

Hinge says the recipient views the note before the match is complete, then acknowledges it to continue or chooses to unmatch.

### How long can it be?

Up to 255 characters.

### Can existing matches see a new note?

Hinge says existing matches should not see Match Notes added after those matches were made.
