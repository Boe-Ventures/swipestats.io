<TLDR>

A useful breakup message contains three things:

1. appreciation or acknowledgment;
2. an unambiguous ending;
3. the boundary that follows.

> I have appreciated getting to know you, but I do not want to continue dating. I wish you well and will not be pursuing further contact.

</TLDR>

## Is Text the Right Channel?

Text can be appropriate after a few dates, in a mostly text-based connection, when distance prevents a conversation, or when live contact would feel unsafe. A longer established relationship may deserve a call or in-person conversation if both people can participate safely.

You do not owe an in-person breakup where there has been intimidation, coercion, stalking, or violence. Prioritize safety, involve trusted people, and use local professional or emergency resources when needed.

## After One or Two Dates

> Thank you for meeting me. I did not feel the connection I am looking for, so I do not want to schedule another date. I wish you the best.

> I enjoyed talking with you, but I do not see this developing romantically. I wanted to be direct rather than disappear.

> You seem like a good person, but this is not the match I am looking for. Take care.

Do not promise friendship merely to soften the message if you do not want it.

## After Several Dates

> I have valued getting to know you. My feelings are not developing in the way I need for a relationship, so I am ending this here. I am sorry this hurts, and I wish you well.

> I have thought about us and do not want to continue dating. This is a final decision, not a request for a break. I appreciate the time we shared.

## Ending a Relationship

> This is difficult to say, but I am ending our relationship. I care about you and appreciate what we shared, but I do not want to continue as partners. I can talk tomorrow about exchanging belongings and practical details.

> Our relationship is no longer healthy for me, and I am ending it. I will not debate the decision. Please contact me only about returning our belongings.

Separate logistics from emotional negotiation when possible.

## When Safety Is a Concern

> I am ending this relationship. Do not come to my home or workplace and do not contact me again. If you continue, I will seek help.

> I am safe, but I am not available for further contact. I have asked you to stop. Any additional messages will be documented and blocked.

Do not reveal your location or plans. Save relevant records, tell trusted people, adjust account privacy, and contact local services if you fear immediate harm.

## If They Ask for a Reason

You can provide a concise truthful answer without submitting a legal brief:

> I do not feel compatible enough to continue.

> I need a relationship with different communication and commitment, and I have decided to leave.

> I am not comfortable discussing this further. My decision is final.

Avoid cataloging every flaw unless a specific practical truth is necessary.

## If You Receive a Breakup Text

> I am hurt, but I understand. Thank you for being direct. I will respect your decision.

> I would prefer not to stay friends. I need space and will not be in contact for now.

You may ask one clarifying question, but you cannot negotiate somebody into continuing a relationship.

## Do Not Send

- vague “maybe someday” language you do not mean;
- insults or public humiliation;
- threats to reveal private information;
- a message designed to provoke jealousy;
- repeated explanations after you established a boundary;
- an AI-written account of feelings that are not yours.
