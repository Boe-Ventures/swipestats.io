## What Does Rizz Mean? (The Actual Definition, Not the TikTok Version)

Let's start with what the suits at Oxford decided. The official rizz definition, straight from the dictionary people: "style, charm, or attractiveness; the ability to attract a romantic or sexual partner."

You can also use it as a verb. "To rizz someone up" means to charm them. Think of it as the Gen Z rebranding of "game" or "mojo." Same energy, new packaging. Like how every generation reinvents the wheel and acts like they discovered fire.

Kai Cenat, the guy who basically invented the word, put it simply: "Rizz just meant game." That's it. No deep philosophy. No hidden meaning. Just the ability to talk to someone you're attracted to without making them want to fake an emergency phone call.

But here's the distinction that matters. Rizz isn't just flirting. Flirting is an action. Rizz is the underlying quality that makes the flirting actually work. You can flirt with L Rizz and it'll be painful for everyone involved. We've all seen it. We've all been it. (I know I have. My early Tinder days were a war crime against conversation.)

So what is rizz in slang terms? It's the difference between someone who walks into a room and people notice, versus someone who walks into a room and the room stays exactly the same.

## Where Rizz Came From (And Why a Twitch Streamer Owns the English Language Now)

The origin story is almost too good. Kai Cenat and his friends started using "rizz" around 2021 on Twitch. Just a bunch of guys streaming and talking about game. Nobody planned for it to become a dictionary word.

Then TikTok got hold of it in 2022. And once TikTok gets hold of something, it's over. The word spread faster than rumors at a middle school dance.

But the real tipping point? Tom Holland. June 2023. An interviewer asked the Spider-Man actor about his rizz, and this man looked directly into the camera and said: "I have no rizz whatsoever. I have limited rizz." He claimed he used "the long game" instead. This went catastrophically viral. A guy who's literally dating Zendaya saying he has no rizz might be the most humble-brag moment in internet history.

By December 4, 2023, Oxford University Press named "rizz" the Word of the Year. It beat out "Swiftie" and "situationship" in a public vote with over 32,000 people weighing in. Democracy at work, folks.

The etymology is fun too. Linguists call it "clipping from the middle." Cha-**rizz**-ma. Same thing that happened to "refrigerator" becoming "fridge." The word nerds were thrilled. Finally, something they could analyze that wasn't another boring Latin root.

Except Kai Cenat himself disputes the charisma connection. He says he just made the word up. No etymology. No deeper meaning. Just vibes. So now we have academics publishing papers on the linguistic evolution of a word while its creator shrugs and says "bro, I just said a thing." This is what peak 2020s culture looks like.

## The Rizz Taxonomy (Because Gen Z Categorizes Everything)

Not all rizz is created equal. There's a whole classification system, and yes, I'm going to walk you through it like a wildlife documentary narrator.

**W Rizz.** The W stands for Win. You've got game. People are attracted to you. You say things and they land. Your pickup lines don't just work, they work effortlessly. Congratulations, you absolute legend. Now stop reading this article and go outside.

**L Rizz.** The L stands for Loss. You have the charisma of a damp napkin at a gas station bathroom. Everything you say makes people want to swipe left on your existence. If your DMs are drier than the Sahara, this is probably you. Don't worry. We'll fix it. (Maybe.)

**Unspoken Rizz.** This is the big one. The ability to attract without saying a single word. Pure presence. Think about it in dating app terms: your photos, your vibe, that effortless "just existing and people want you" energy. On Tinder, your unspoken rizz IS your profile. Nobody can sense your body language through a screen, so your pictures better be pulling their weight.

**Rizz God / The Rizzler.** Someone with supernatural-level charm. The person who could read a grocery list out loud and somehow walk away with three phone numbers and a date for Friday. You know the type. You hate the type. You want to be the type.

**0 Rizz.** You don't just lack charm. Charm has a restraining order against you. Your last match was a bot trying to sell you crypto. You send "hey" as an opener and genuinely think it's sufficient. (It's not. It has never been sufficient. It will never be sufficient.)

## How to Get Rizz (The Data-Backed, Non-Cringe Version)

You're still reading. Good. That already puts you ahead of every guy who Googled "what does rizz mean," read the first paragraph, and went back to sending "hey" on Tinder. Let's actually fix your game.

### Fix Your Unspoken Rizz First (Your Photos Are Probably Terrible)

I'm not being mean. I'm being statistical. Your photos ARE your unspoken rizz on dating apps. They're the only thing standing between you and the left-swipe graveyard.

Get proper photos. Hire a photographer if you have to. Ditch the bathroom mirror selfie where people can see your toothpaste-stained counter. Ditch the group photo where you're the shortest one and nobody can tell which one is you. Ditch the fish pic. (Unless you caught a shark with your bare hands. Even then, maybe keep it to yourself.)

I wrote a whole guide on [how to get more matches on Tinder](/blog/how-to-get-more-matches-on-tinder) that covers the photo game in painful detail. Read it. Implement it. Your match rate will thank you.

### Master Your Bio (Written Rizz That Doesn't Make People Cringe)

Your bio is your first act of spoken rizz. If it says "I like hiking and tacos" you have 0 rizz. You have negative rizz. You have anti-rizz. Every woman on that app has read "hiking and tacos" four thousand times today and the bar is so low it's underground.

Be specific. Be funny. Be brief. "I once accidentally joined a cult because I was too polite to say no" is infinitely more interesting than "looking for my partner in crime." Your [Tinder bio](/blog/best-tinder-bios) should make someone think "huh, this person might actually be interesting" not "wow, this person is a copy-paste of every other profile on this app."

Three sentences max. Each one should earn its place. If a sentence could appear on literally any other person's profile, delete it and try harder.

### Send Openers That Aren't "Hey" (The Bar Is Underground and You're Still Tripping Over It)

The data is extremely clear on this. Personalized openers get dramatically more responses. Reference something from their profile. Show evidence that you have a functioning brain and used it for two seconds before hitting send.

"Hey" is not an opener. It's the conversational equivalent of a blank stare. "Hey" is what you say when you pass a coworker in the hallway and don't actually want to talk. If that's your A-game, your rizz needs emergency surgery.

### Ask Questions Like You Actually Care (Revolutionary, I Know)

Hinge's 2025 D.A.T.E. Report dropped a stat that should be printed on every dating app's loading screen: **85% of daters are more likely to want a second date when asked thoughtful questions.**

But here's the kicker. **62% of people THINK they ask enough questions. Only 30% of their dates agree.** The delusion is strong. You think you're being engaging. You're not. You're talking about yourself and occasionally saying "that's cool" when they share something.

Questions are rizz fuel. They show genuine interest, which is the entire point. Not interrogation-style "where are you from, what do you do, do you have siblings" rapid fire. Actual curious questions about things that matter. Read our guide on [how to keep a conversation going](/blog/how-to-keep-a-conversation-going-over-text) if you need a cheat sheet.

### Stop Being Needy (Desperation Has Negative Rizz)

Neediness is the number one rizz killer on the planet. It's worse than bad photos. Worse than a boring bio. Worse than that thing you do where you double-text "??" after someone hasn't responded in 20 minutes.

If you're triple-texting someone who hasn't responded, that's not persistence. That's not "shooting your shot." That's L Rizz in its purest, most concentrated form. It's the [dry texting](/blog/dry-texting) death spiral and you're accelerating.

Abundance mindset. There are roughly 4 billion people of your preferred gender on this planet. The person who left you on read is not your last chance at happiness. They're not even in the top ten thousand of your chances at happiness. Act accordingly.

Real rizz comes from genuinely not needing the interaction to go well. When you're having fun regardless of the outcome, people can feel it. It's the difference between "please like me" energy and "I'm having a good time and you're welcome to join" energy. One is magnetic. The other makes people change their phone number.

## FAQ

### Is rizz just flirting?

No. Flirting is an action. Rizz is the underlying quality that makes flirting work. You can flirt with L Rizz and it'll be painful for everyone in the room. Think of rizz as the engine and flirting as the car. A Ferrari body on a lawnmower engine isn't impressing anybody.

### What does W rizz mean?

W stands for Win. It means you've got game. Simple. You can talk to people you're attracted to and they actually want to keep talking to you. The bar isn't high, and yet.

### What does unspoken rizz mean?

Attracting people without saying anything. Pure presence and energy. On dating apps, this translates to your photos and profile doing all the work. If someone swipes right before reading a single word of your bio, that's unspoken rizz.

### What does it mean to rizz someone up?

To charm them. To turn a "who are you" into a "tell me more." It's the verb form. "He rizzed her up at the bar." "She rizzed the entire room without trying." You get the idea.

### Can girls have rizz?

Obviously. Rizz isn't gendered. But dating app dynamics mean the word gets applied to men more often because men statistically need to work harder for matches. When your match rate is 2% versus 41%, the conversation around "how to get rizz" tends to skew masculine. But women with W Rizz? They exist and they're terrifying (in the best way).
