## Do Pick Up Lines Actually Work? (Science Says... Kind Of)

Let's settle this before you scroll straight to the list and copy-paste your way to another ignored message.

I've been writing about dating apps for years now at SwipeStats, and our dataset covers **7,000+ profiles, 294 million swipes, and 3.14 million matches**. So when I say pick up lines work, I'm not pulling that out of thin air. But they don't work the way you think they do.

Irrational Labs ran an A/B test with **5 behavioral scientists, 1,227 Tinder users across 5 US cities**. The result? Funny messages pulled **12% more replies** than earnest, sincere ones. Not a massive gap, but consistent. Humor signals confidence, social intelligence, and the fact that you don't take yourself too seriously (even if you absolutely do).

Then there's Dylan Ryder's AI analysis of **50 million dating app messages** from November 2025. Profile-specific openers like "Your [item] in photo 3 is incredible. Where's it from?" pulled a **73% reply rate**. For context, the average male response rate sits at a pathetic 21%. That's a 340% improvement just from proving you actually looked at someone's profile before messaging them. Revolutionary stuff.

Here's how the different opener types stacked up:

- **Generic compliments** ("you're beautiful"): **8% response rate**. Your grandma could've told you this doesn't work.
- **Try-hard humor** (canned jokes): **12%**. Better than nothing. Barely.
- **Interview questions** ("where are you from?"): **15%**. You're not a hiring manager.
- **Profile-specific personalized openers**: **73%**. Bingo.

Academic research backs this up too. Wade, Fisher & Gaines (2022) found that direct lines like "I find you attractive" actually ranked #1 in perceived effectiveness. Not because they're creative, but because confidence reads well on a screen.

The really interesting finding comes from Cooper et al. (2007): pick up lines act as **personality filters**. Outgoing women gravitate toward humor. Shy women prefer friendly, low-pressure lines. So the "best" pick up line doesn't exist. The best line is the one that matches the person you're talking to. Which means, yes, you actually have to pay attention to their profile. The horror.

A StudyFinds survey of **2,000 singles** found that 60% said they're more likely to respond to a funny or clever [first message](/blog/first-message-on-dating-app). And Tinder's own data shows messages with GIFs get **30% more responses**.

The verdict? Pick up lines work. But the canned, copy-paste ones work about as well as wearing Axe Body Spray to a first date. Personalization beats everything. Every time. No exceptions.

8. **Copy-pasted walls of text.** If your opener is longer than their entire bio, you're doing too much. Way too much.
9. **"I'm not like other guys."** Every guy who says this is exactly like other guys. All of them. Without exception.
10. **"You're not like other girls."** The female version of #9. Just as bad. Maybe worse.

## How to Actually Use a Pick Up Line (The Part You'll Skip)

I know you scrolled past this heading on your first pass. You wanted the lists. I get it. But if you use a great line with terrible execution, you're still going to get ignored. This is the part that actually matters.

### Timing is Everything

Sunday evening between **6-8 PM** gives you a **43% reply lift**. Tuesday and Thursday evenings are also solid. Friday nights are the worst possible time to send an opener because the people worth talking to are actually busy. If you're sending pick up lines at midnight on a Friday, you're telling on yourself.

### Use GIFs (Your Words Need Help)

Tinder's own data shows messages with GIFs get **30% more responses** and conversations last **2x longer**. A well-chosen GIF after your opener can break tension better than any follow-up line. Think of them as punctuation for people who communicate primarily through memes (which is all of us now, let's be honest).

### Personalize or Don't Bother

I'm going to say this one more time for the people in the back: **personalized openers get a 73% reply rate**. Canned lines get 12-21%. The math is not complicated. Mention something specific from their profile. Their prompt answer. Their dog's breed. The restaurant in photo 4. Anything that proves you spent more than half a second deciding to message them.

### Mention Food

This one's weird but the data supports it. Women are **40% more likely to respond** to messages that mention food. "That pasta in your second photo looks incredible. What restaurant?" is doing more work than any pick up line on this page. I don't make the rules.

### The "Rizz" Culture Shift

Scripted lines are dying. The TikTok generation turned "rizz" into a whole personality trait, but real rizz isn't about having the perfect line. It's about reading the room (or reading the profile) and responding with authentic personality. The era of memorizing openers is ending. The era of actually being interesting is here. Bad news for most of you.

Your opener gets you in the door. Your follow-up determines whether you stay. The best pick up line in the world means nothing if your second message is "so wyd." If they respond, match their energy. Ask about something they mentioned. Build on the joke. Don't let the conversation die because you used all your material on the opener.
