## Your Crush Doesn't Want "Hey" (Nobody Does)

Let's start with the elephant in the room. Or rather, the three-letter word sitting in your drafts like a sad little participation trophy.

"Hey."

According to OkCupid's messaging data, sending "hey" gives you an **84% chance of being ignored**. Eighty-four percent. You'd have better odds flipping a coin. Twice. And that's the baseline for men messaging women, where the average reply rate is already a soul-crushing **27%**. So if your opening move is "hey," you're not playing the game. You're forfeiting.

I've been there. Years ago, before I started SwipeStats and spent way too much time analyzing this stuff, I sent "hey" like it was a legitimate strategy. I thought it was chill. Laid-back. Cool, even. It was none of those things. It was lazy, and my inbox reflected that.

Here's what nobody tells you about texting your crush: the biggest barrier isn't finding the perfect words. It's the anxiety that makes you overthink every letter until you've rewritten the same message fourteen times and just send "hey" anyway. The Reddit dating communities are full of people saying the same thing. The best text is the one you actually send. Perfection is the enemy of getting a reply.

So stop workshopping your opening line like it's a TED Talk. Your crush isn't grading you on literary merit. They just want proof you're a person with a pulse and a personality.

## What to Text Your Crush to Start a Conversation (Without Sounding Like a Robot)

Alright, so "hey" is dead. Good riddance. Now what do you actually say to [start a conversation](/blog/how-to-start-a-conversation-on-tinder) with your crush over text?

The answer is embarrassingly simple: **reference something real.**

A shared experience. Something they posted. A conversation you had. An inside joke. Anything that signals "I was paying attention and I'm not copy-pasting this to six other people."

Here's the psychology behind it. There's this thing called the Ben Franklin Effect, documented in a study published in *Human Relations*. The gist: when you ask someone for a small favor, they actually like you more afterward. Their brain rationalizes it as "I must like this person if I'm helping them." Wild, right? So asking your crush for a recommendation, an opinion, or even a stupid favor is actually a better move than trying to impress them.

Also, according to OkCupid data, the optimal message length is **40-90 characters**. That's basically one sentence. Not a paragraph. Not a novella. One well-aimed sentence.

### Openers That Actually Work (Organized by Situation)

**If you just met them:**
- "I'm still thinking about that [specific thing they said]. What was the name of that [restaurant/show/band] again?"
- "So I tried that thing you recommended and it was [honest reaction]. You owe me."

**If you follow them on social media:**
- "Your story about [specific thing] made me realize I need to ask you something completely unrelated."
- "Okay, your [food photo/travel story/take on something] just reminded me of the weirdest thing."

**If you've been orbiting each other's friend group:**
- "I feel like we've been at the same three events this month and I still don't know your take on [shared interest]."
- "So [mutual friend] told me you're into [hobby]. They failed to mention if you're actually good at it."

**If you want to go the opinion route (Ben Franklin style):**
- "Settle a debate for me: is [thing A] or [thing B] better? My friend is wrong and I need backup."
- "I need a second opinion on something and you seem like someone with actual taste."

Notice what all of these have in common? They're specific. They're low-pressure. They give the other person something to respond to. They are, critically, not "hey."

## Things to Text Your Crush to Keep Them Interested (AKA Stop Being Dry)

So you got a reply. Congratulations, you've cleared the lowest bar in human communication (and that's a bar so low it's basically underground). Now comes the part where most people absolutely fumble it.

The number one killer of texting chemistry is the interview trap. Question, answer, question, answer. Back and forth like the world's most boring tennis match. You know the pattern. "What do you do?" "I'm a nurse." "Oh cool. Where do you work?" "Memorial Hospital." "Nice."

Nice? NICE? You might as well hand them a comment card and ask them to rate their experience. If your conversations feel like filling out a form, you need to read our guide on [dry texting](/blog/dry-texting), because that's exactly what you're doing.

Instead, try sharing something from your own day. Invite them into your world instead of interrogating them about theirs. "I just saw a dog wearing sunglasses on the train and I genuinely can't tell if he was pulling it off or not" is infinitely more interesting than "how was your day?"

### The Callback Technique

This one's gold. Reference something specific they told you earlier. Not just "haha yeah," but actually circle back to it. If they mentioned they had a presentation on Wednesday, text them Thursday: "Did you crush that presentation or do I need to send a sympathy playlist?"

It shows you listen. You'd be shocked how rare that is. Our analysis at SwipeStats of [7,000+ dating profiles](/blog/tinder-statistics) and 3.14M matches confirms what you probably already suspect: most matches never lead to a real conversation. Being someone who actually engages puts you ahead of basically everyone.

### Desire vs. Platonic Language

Matthew Hussey talks about this and he's right. There's a difference between "that's so sweet" and "that's really attractive." Between "you're nice" and "I can't stop thinking about what you said." One keeps you in the friend zone. The other doesn't.

You don't have to be explicit. Just stop scrubbing every hint of interest out of your messages like you're sanitizing a crime scene.

### Mid-Conversation Texts That Work

- "You just made me laugh out loud in a meeting. I hope you're proud of yourself."
- "I don't know why but I thought of you when I saw [specific thing]. Make of that what you will."
- "I need you to know that your taste in [music/food/movies] is either perfect or deeply unhinged. Still deciding."
- "That thing you said about [topic] is stuck in my head and I blame you entirely."
- "I'm going to need you to stop being so [funny/interesting/charming]. It's becoming a problem."
- "Okay I have a confession. I've been thinking about [your recommendation/our conversation] all day."

## What to Ask Your Crush Over Text (Questions That Don't Make Them Want to Die)

"How was your day?" is not a question. It's a hostage negotiation with someone's attention span.

If you want to know what to say to your crush over text without putting them to sleep, you need to understand one thing: **open-ended questions destroy yes/no questions every time.** Questions that invite stories, opinions, and hot takes keep people engaged. Questions that can be answered with a single word get answered with a single word. Or worse, left on read.

You might've heard of the 36 Questions study, published in *Personality and Social Psychology Bulletin*. Researchers found that pairs of strangers who asked each other increasingly personal questions developed genuine closeness. Some of them got married. The takeaway isn't to blast your crush with all 36 (please don't). It's that the right question at the right time creates real connection.

### Funny Questions

- "What's the worst piece of advice you've ever been given that you actually followed?"
- "If your life had a theme song that played every time you walked into a room, what would it be?"
- "What hill will you die on that nobody else agrees with?"
- "What's the most unhinged thing in your search history that you'd be willing to admit?"

### Deep Questions

- "What's something you changed your mind about in the last year?"
- "What's a memory that shaped you more than you expected?"
- "If you could have dinner with one person, dead or alive, and they had to be honest about everything, who?"
- "What's something you're weirdly good at that nobody knows about?"

### Flirty Questions

- "What's the first thing you noticed about me? And don't say 'your personality,' I won't believe you."
- "On a scale of 1 to 10, how much trouble am I going to be?"
- "What's your love language? Asking for research purposes only."
- "If I showed up at your door right now, what would we end up doing? Keep it PG. Or don't."

## How to Text Your Crush Without Being Boring (The Actual Rules)

Okay, let's get into the data, because I love data (occupational hazard of running SwipeStats) and the data has opinions about your texting skills.

**Messages referencing specific shared interests significantly outperform generic openers.** This isn't a surprise to anyone who's ever received "hey wyd" at 11 PM and felt their soul leave their body.

Here's one that might sting: **complimenting someone's appearance actually lowers response rates.** According to OkCupid data, words like "pretty," "beautiful," and "sexy" correlate with fewer replies. Complimenting intelligence or accomplishments? That raises response rates. So put down the "you're gorgeous" and pick up "your take on that was brilliant." You'll thank me later.

### Playful Teasing (Done Right)

Teasing is [flirting over text](/blog/how-to-flirt-over-text) with a backbone. It signals confidence. But there's a line between "playfully giving you a hard time" and "being a dick," and a lot of people don't know where that line is.

The rule: tease the situation or the behavior, not the person. "Your music taste is a red flag and I kind of love it" works. "You seem boring" does not. One creates tension. The other creates a block.

### Grammar Matters (Yes, Really)

I know this sounds like your English teacher talking, but the data is clear. OkCupid found that netspeak like "ur," "u," and "wat" significantly kills response rates. Meanwhile, using proper contractions like "don't" and "won't" correlates with a **36-37% reply rate**, which is above average.

You don't need to write like you're submitting a dissertation. Just write like a literate adult. That bar is on the floor and some of you are still tripping over it.

### Quality Over Quantity

Don't flood the inbox. Nobody wants to open their phone to nine unread messages from the same person. That's not enthusiasm. That's a restraining order waiting to happen. Send one good message. Wait for a reply. Have an actual exchange. This isn't a monologue.

## When to Text Your Crush (Timing Is Not a Game, It's Science)

I used to think "wait three days" was an actual rule. Like it was written in some ancient dating scripture alongside "never eat spaghetti on a first date" and "never admit you watch reality TV." It's not. It's garbage advice from a 2005 romcom that aged about as well as Myspace.

Here's what the data actually says.

**60% of singles expect a reply within 1-3 hours.** Not three days. Not "leave them on read to build mystery." One to three hours. And **52% of people actively judge you based on your response speed.** So that whole "playing it cool" strategy? It's making you look disinterested, not desirable.

A 2026 study by Teichmann et al. found something even more specific: a **morning-after text following a first date** correlated with the highest relationship intentions. Not the same night (too eager), not three days later (too cold). The morning after. That's the sweet spot. Women showed even stronger sensitivity to this timing than men.

### The Bookend Strategy

Want a low-effort, high-impact move? [Good morning texts](/blog/good-morning-texts-for-her) and [goodnight texts](/blog/good-night-texts) are the bookends of someone's day. Being the first person they hear from and the last person they think about is a ridiculously powerful position to hold. You don't need to write a sonnet. "Hope your day's good" in the morning and "sleep well" at night does more heavy lifting than you'd expect.

### The Red Flags of Timing

If someone consistently takes **12+ hours to reply**, that's not them being busy. That's declining interest. Especially if they used to reply faster. I'm not saying panic. I'm saying read the room. Or in this case, read the timestamps.

## How to Tell Your Crush You Like Them Over Text (Without Having a Meltdown)

This is the part where everyone panics. The confession. The big reveal. The "I like you and now I'm going to stare at my phone for six hours waiting for a response while quietly dying inside."

I've been here. More than once (don't judge me). And here's what I've learned: the best confessions don't sound like confessions at all. They sound like honesty delivered with confidence.

### The Reluctant Mind Takeover

This is my favorite framework. Instead of saying "I really like you" (which sounds like you're reading from a Hallmark card your grandma picked out), try: "I keep thinking about you and honestly it's a little annoying."

It's vulnerable AND confident. It says "I like you" without sounding like you're proposing marriage. It gives them something to laugh at and respond to. It's human.

### Don't Over-Declare

A hint hits harder than a speech. Always. You don't need to write five paragraphs about your feelings. In fact, please don't. A 2025 study published in SAGE found that securely attached people use warm but balanced language in texts. Not too much, not too little. The vibe is "I'm into you and I'm not freaking out about it."

### Texts That Show Interest Without Desperation

- "I keep thinking about you and honestly it's becoming a whole thing."
- "I had such a good time with you that I'm already trying to figure out when we can do it again."
- "You're annoyingly fun to talk to. Thought you should know."
- "I wasn't expecting you. That's not a complaint."
- "I'm not sure what this is but I like it. A lot."
- "I'm going to be honest: I smile like an idiot every time you text me."

## What NOT to Text Your Crush (The Hall of Shame)

Time for the wall of shame. If you want to [slide into someone's DMs](/blog/how-to-slide-into-dms) and immediately slide right back out, here's your playbook.

**The Double/Triple Text When Ignored**
Sent a message. No reply. So you send another. And another. And maybe a "??" for good measure. Stop. If they haven't replied, they either haven't seen it or don't want to reply. Both situations are made worse by you repeatedly poking them like a kid at a zoo exhibit.

**The Netspeak Offender**
"ur so pretty wyd." According to OkCupid data, messages containing "ur," "u," and "wat" get significantly fewer responses. You have a full keyboard. Use it. You're not paying by the letter.

**The Too-Early Physical Compliment**
"You're beautiful." "You're so sexy." Words like "pretty," "beautiful," and "sexy" actually **lower response rates** according to OkCupid. Save the appearance compliments for after you've actually established a connection. Compliment their brain first. Revolutionary concept, I know.

**The Wall of Text**
Remember: **40-90 characters** is the optimal message length. If you're sending paragraphs, you're writing a journal entry, not a text. Your crush didn't sign up for a book club.

**The Infinite "wyd"**
"wyd" once is lazy. "wyd" on repeat is basically Morse code for "I have nothing interesting to say but I want your attention." Try literally anything else.

**The Unsolicited Life Update**
Nobody asked for a play-by-play of your entire Tuesday. Share moments, not timelines. "I just saw the weirdest thing" beats "So first I woke up at 7, then I had eggs, then I..." You're texting your crush, not narrating a documentary about yourself.

## FAQ

### How do you end a text conversation with your crush?

This question is trending hard (up **1,080% year-over-year** in search volume), which means a lot of you are trapped in conversations you don't know how to wrap up. Here's the thing: not every conversation needs a formal ending. The best ones trail off naturally and pick back up later. But if you want a clean exit, try: "I've got to run but I really enjoyed this. Talk soon?" It's warm, direct, and doesn't leave them wondering if you died.

### What should you text your crush in the morning?

Something light. Something that says "you were on my mind." Not a five-paragraph essay about your dreams. "Good morning, hope today treats you well" works. So does "Woke up thinking about [thing you discussed]. Still can't believe [funny reference]." The goal is to be the first notification that makes them smile, not the one they swipe away. We've got 107 morning message ideas if you need more inspiration.

### How do you confess your feelings over text?

Short version: don't make it a production. Don't preface it with "I need to tell you something." Don't write a letter. Just weave it naturally into conversation. "I like you" doesn't need a drumroll. The best confessions feel like they slipped out because the person couldn't hold it in anymore. "You make me stupid happy and I figured you should know" is way more effective than a formal declaration of feelings.
