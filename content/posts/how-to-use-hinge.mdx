## What Is Hinge (and Why Everyone Won't Shut Up About It)

If you've been anywhere near a single person in the last two years, you've heard someone say "I'm on Hinge now" with the same energy as someone announcing they switched to oat milk. Congratulations, you're trendy. You want to know how to use Hinge without embarrassing yourself? You're in the right place.

But here's the thing. The hype is actually justified for once.

Hinge is a dating app that markets itself as "designed to be deleted." Bold claim for a company that printed **$691 million in revenue** last year (a 26% jump from the year before). Sure, they want you to delete it. Right after you pay them. (Sure, Jan.)

So how does Hinge work? Unlike Tinder, where you mindlessly swipe through faces like you're sorting laundry, Hinge forces you to interact with specific parts of someone's profile. You like a photo. You comment on a prompt. You actually engage with the person as a human being. Novel concept in 2026, I know.

The numbers tell a story worth reading. **38 million users** globally. **18% US market share** and climbing. It's the fastest-growing major dating app, and when The Knot reported that **36% of dating-app marriages in 2025** came from Hinge, that shut up a lot of the skeptics.

The user base skews toward people who actually want something real. **87% of Hinge users say they're looking for serious relationships**, compared to roughly 30-40% on Tinder. The gender split is 60/40 male to female, which isn't perfect, but it's a paradise compared to Tinder's 75/25 sausage fest. **90% of users are aged 23-36**, so if you're in that range, this is your dating pool.

And since Jackie Jantos took over as CEO in 2025, the app has been pushing even harder on the "quality over quantity" angle. Whether that's corporate spin or genuine product philosophy, the results speak for themselves.

## How to Set Up a Hinge Profile That Doesn't Suck

Here's where most people fail before they even start. You download Hinge, throw up some photos from 2019, write "I like tacos and The Office" in your prompts, and then wonder why nobody likes you back. It's not the algorithm, champ. It's you.

### Photos: The Formula That Actually Works

Hinge requires **6 photos**. Not suggests. Requires. And before you groan about not having enough good photos of yourself, that's exactly the problem. If you can't find 6 photos where you look like a functioning adult, you've got bigger issues than your dating life.

Here's the formula that works:

- **Slot 1: Clear solo headshot.** No sunglasses. No hat. Eye contact with the camera. This is your handshake. Make it firm.
- **Slot 2: Full-body shot.** Proves you're not hiding anything below the neck. Not a gym selfie. A normal photo of your whole person existing in the world.
- **Slot 3: Activity shot.** You doing something interesting. Hiking, cooking, playing guitar, whatever. Something that proves you occasionally leave your apartment.
- **Slot 4: Social shot.** You with friends or family. Proof that other humans tolerate your presence voluntarily.
- **Slot 5-6: Wildcards.** Travel, pets, a candid laugh. Fill in the personality gaps.

**Selfies underperform across the board.** Gym selfies are a war crime against your match rate. I'm not exaggerating. Every piece of data we've seen at SwipeStats confirms this.

And here's something that hit me. Anti Confidence Girl (a dating content creator with a massive following) admitted she procrastinated for two months just getting 6 good photos for her Hinge profile. Two months. If someone whose literal job is dating advice finds this hard, you're allowed to struggle with it too. But you still have to do it.

Don't skip **photo prompts and voice prompts** either. Most people ignore these, which is exactly why using them makes you stand out. A 10-second voice prompt telling a funny story does more for your profile than your fourth hiking photo ever will.

### Prompts: Your Written Personality Test (Don't Fail It)

Hinge gives you **3 prompts** chosen from over 100 options. Think of these as your personality's job interview. And most of you are showing up in sweatpants.

The rules are simple:

- **Invite conversation, don't list hobbies.** "Typical Sunday: watching football" tells her absolutely nothing she wants to know about you. What is she supposed to say to that? "Cool, me too"? Dead conversation before it started.
- **Be specific and a little weird.** "Unpopular opinion: espresso martinis are a dessert" gives someone something to agree with, disagree with, or roast you about. All three lead to conversation.
- **Balance humor and vulnerability.** All-jokes profiles bomb because they signal you can't be serious. All-serious profiles bomb because they signal you can't be fun. Mix it up.
- **Never use cliches.** If your prompt response could appear on 10,000 other profiles, delete it. "Looking for my partner in crime" should be punishable by actual crime.

If you need inspiration (and you do), check out our guide on [the best Hinge prompts](/blog/best-hinge-prompts) for specific examples that actually work.

### Vitals, Virtues, and Vices (Fill. Them. Out.)

Hinge lets you list your height, religion, politics, career, education, and more. It also has a unique **Vices section** covering drinking, smoking, and drugs. This is actually useful because it handles the awkward "so... do you smoke?" conversation before it ever needs to happen.

**Leaving sections blank looks lazy.** Because it is. Every empty field is your profile whispering "I couldn't be bothered." And if you couldn't be bothered to fill out your profile, why would anyone bother matching with you?

## How the Hinge Algorithm Actually Works (Nobel Prize Math Meets Your Love Life)

Alright, strap in. This is where it gets nerdy. And honestly? Understanding how the [Hinge algorithm](/blog/hinge-algorithm) works gives you a genuine advantage over the 90% of users who just swipe and pray.

Hinge's matching system is built on the **Gale-Shapley algorithm**, a stable matching algorithm that literally won its creators a Nobel Prize in Economics. The same math that matches medical residents to hospitals is now deciding whether you get a date on Friday night. What a time to be alive.

The algorithm weighs three types of signals:

1. **Profile signals.** The quality of your photos, how complete your prompts are, whether you've filled out your vitals. Basically, did you try?
2. **Intent signals.** Your swiping habits, whether you leave comments when you like someone, how fast you reply to messages. Are you using the app like you mean it?
3. **Outcome signals.** Do your matches actually turn into conversations? Do those conversations lead to dates? The algorithm tracks this. If you match and ghost, Hinge notices.

Here's the kicker. In **November 2025**, Hinge rolled out a major algorithm update. Commented likes now get weighted significantly higher than plain likes. People who comment when they send a like saw a **double-digit increase** in their match rate. That's not a small tweak. That's Hinge flat-out telling you: put in effort or get left behind.

**Activity matters, but not the way you think.** Daily, consistent use gets rewarded. But spending 3 hours doom-swiping in one sitting actually hurts you. The algorithm reads that as desperation, not enthusiasm. Log in, send your thoughtful likes, respond to your messages, log out. Treat it like going to the gym, not binge-watching Netflix.

Your **"Most Compatible"** suggestion is Hinge's best guess at your soulmate, generated fresh every day. Users shown as someone's Most Compatible are **8x more likely to go on an actual date**. Eight times. And most people scroll right past it to look at Standouts instead. Stop doing that.

One more thing that nobody wants to hear. If the profiles you're seeing suck, your profile probably sucks too. The algorithm matches you with people at your perceived level. Want to see better profiles? Become a better profile. Simple as that. Brutal, but simple.

## The Like Economy: Likes, Roses, and Standouts Explained

Hinge runs on a carefully designed economy of attention. Understanding how it works is the difference between getting dates and getting frustrated.

### Free Likes (Your Daily Currency)

Free users get **8 likes per day**. No secret stash. No bonus likes at midnight. And honestly? That constraint is a feature, not a bug. It forces you to be selective, which trains the algorithm to show you better matches over time.

The golden rule: **ALWAYS comment when you like someone.** Every piece of data. every content creator, Hinge's own internal research. All of it says the same thing. Commented likes get dramatically more responses than plain likes. A plain like says "I think you're attractive." A comment says "I think you're attractive AND I have something to say to you." Guess which one gets a reply.

And for the love of everything holy, don't mass-like everyone. That trains the algorithm to think you have zero standards, and it'll start showing you to people with equally low standards. You become the bottom of someone else's feed. Congratulations, you played yourself.

### Roses: The Hinge Power Move (Use Wisely)

Roses are Hinge's premium currency, and they're more powerful than most people realize. You get **1 free Rose per week**, resetting on Sunday. Mark your calendar. Or don't. Hinge will remind you.

Here's why Roses matter. Sending a Rose puts you at the **top of someone's "Likes You" queue**. If they have 100 likes and 20 roses waiting, the roses show first. That's not a small advantage. That's cutting the entire line.

You can buy more: 3 for about $9, 12 for about $24, 50 for about $75. But here's my honest take. Save your Roses for someone you're genuinely excited about. Using a Rose as a Hail Mary on someone way out of your league is just lighting money on fire with extra steps.

### Standouts: Not What You Think

Every day, Hinge shows you **10 Standout profiles** that refresh at midnight. Most people assume these are the hottest people on the app. They're not. They're the profiles Hinge's algorithm thinks are most compatible with you based on your behavior patterns.

The catch? You need to spend a Rose to contact a Standout. And here's the insider tip most guides won't tell you: those same Standout profiles might also appear in your regular feed eventually. So if you're patient and not desperate (a big "if" for some of you), you might be able to like them for free. Don't waste a Rose if you can wait.

## Messaging on Hinge: Where Matches Go to Die (Unless You Read This)

Matching is step one. Actually converting that match into a date is where most people fall apart like a cheap suit in a rainstorm. And I'm not guessing here. I've seen enough SwipeStats data to know that the gap between "getting matches" and "getting dates" is where dreams go to quietly decompose.

**"Hey" is literally the worst opener.** This isn't my opinion. This is Hinge's own internal research. The word "hey" as a conversation starter is the dating app equivalent of a limp handshake. You wouldn't walk up to someone at a bar, say "hey," and then stand there staring at them like a golden retriever waiting for a treat. (If you would, we need to have a very different conversation.)

Here's what actually works:

- **Reference something specific from their profile.** Not their main photo. The weird detail. The third prompt. The voice note. Show you actually looked at their profile for more than 0.3 seconds. "I noticed you said pineapple belongs on pizza. I need you to defend this position immediately" is infinitely better than "nice smile."
- **Ask a question that's easy to answer but fun to think about.** "What's the story behind that photo in Portugal?" beats "How was your weekend?" every single time. Give them a reason to type more than three words.
- **Don't ask for their number in the first message.** I know some pickup artist on YouTube told you to "move off the app fast." That advice is for Tinder, not Hinge. Hinge users expect a little more rapport before they hand over their phone number to a stranger on the internet. Build it. You're not closing a sales deal.
- **Aim to set up a date within the first week.** Conversations that drag on for two weeks rarely convert to actual dates. The excitement fades. Someone else comes along. You run out of things to text about. Move with purpose, not desperation.
- **Mirror their energy.** If they're writing paragraphs, write paragraphs. If they're keeping it short and punchy, match that. Nothing kills chemistry faster than someone writing essays to your one-liners (or vice versa).

And let's talk about the **Your Turn limit**, because this trips up more people than it should. You can only have **8 unanswered conversations** at once. If you've sent 8 messages that nobody's replied to, you're locked out of starting new conversations until you clean house. Think of it as Hinge's way of telling you to stop collecting matches like Pokemon cards and actually talk to people. Unmatch the dead conversations. Free up your slots. This isn't a museum exhibit of people who ghosted you.

Here's something I've learned from my own dating life: the conversations that feel effortless in the first few messages almost always lead to better dates. If you're forcing it over text, the coffee date isn't going to magically fix that. Trust the vibe check.

The [Hinge subscription](/blog/hinge-subscription) question. Every dating app has a premium tier designed to separate you from your money. Hinge has two. Let's break down what you actually get.

| Feature | Free | Hinge+ (~$20-30/mo) | HingeX (~$50/mo) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily likes | 8 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| See who liked you | One at a time | All at once | All at once |
| Preference filters | Basic (age, distance) | Advanced (height, education, politics) | Advanced |
| Skip the Line | No | No | Yes |
| Priority Likes | No | No | Yes |
| Roses | 1/week | 1/week | 1/week |

The Hinge dating app free tier is genuinely usable. **8 likes per day, 1 rose per week, basic filters.** That's enough to get started and enough to get dates if your profile is solid. Most free apps give you just enough rope to hang yourself with. Hinge gives you enough rope to actually do something useful.

**Hinge+** is worth it if you're getting likes but can't see who they are. Seeing all your likes at once means you can prioritize the people you're actually interested in instead of clearing them one by one like you're defusing bombs.

**HingeX** is the nuclear option. Priority placement in people's feeds, Skip the Line to appear before other profiles, the works. But here's the catch: **a bad profile with HingeX is still a bad profile.** You're just paying to show your bad profile to more people, faster. That's not a flex. That's embarrassing.

Dating coach Koen Hovalo puts it perfectly: **Profile x Exposure x Texting = Dates.** Paying only helps the Exposure variable. If your Profile is a zero, zero times anything is still zero. Fix your profile first, then consider paying.

## Common Mistakes That'll Tank Your Match Rate (You're Probably Making Three of These)

I've looked at thousands of dating profiles through SwipeStats. The same mistakes come up so consistently it's almost impressive. Like, did you all read the same "How to Fail at Dating" manual? The worst part? Most of these are easy to fix. You're just too lazy or too unaware to fix them.

- **Mass-liking everyone.** The algorithm learns you have zero taste and starts treating you accordingly. Be picky. It literally improves your results. Every time you like someone you're not actually interested in, you're training Hinge's algorithm to think that's what you want. Garbage in, garbage out.
- **Photos that look like security camera footage from 2011.** Blurry, poorly lit, cropped from a group photo where you can barely tell which pixel is you. Get new photos. It's 2026. Your phone has a better camera than most professional setups from 10 years ago. There's no excuse. Zero.
- **Prompts that are just lists of hobbies.** "I love hiking, travel, and trying new restaurants." Cool, Brad. So does literally everyone else on this app. That's not a personality. That's a default human settings menu. You might as well write "I enjoy breathing and sometimes consuming food."
- **Disqualifying language.** "Don't swipe if you can't hold a conversation." "Not looking for hookups, PLEASE." "If you voted for [politician], keep scrolling." Every negative statement pushes away three people for every one it filters out. You're not a bouncer at an exclusive club. You're a person on a dating app. Lead with what you want, not what you don't.
- **Not filling out your Vitals.** When you leave your height blank, people don't think "oh, he's probably normal height." They think "he's definitely 5'4" and hiding it." (Nothing wrong with being 5'4". Hiding it is the problem.) Same goes for career, education, and all the other fields. An incomplete profile tells people you're either hiding something or you don't care. Neither is attractive.
- **Ignoring voice prompts.** This is a free differentiator that **90% of guys skip**. A voice prompt lets someone hear your actual voice, your humor, your energy. It's basically a free personality boost sitting right there, and you're leaving it untouched like the last piece of pizza nobody wants to claim. Use it.
- **Preference filters set way too narrow.** Your future partner might not check every single box you've imagined in your head. If you filter for 5'8"+, college-educated, non-smoking, moderate-drinking women within 5 miles who also want kids, you might be looking at 12 profiles total. Loosen up a little. Life surprises you sometimes.
- **Sending likes without comments.** I know I've said this already. I'm saying it again because it's that important. A plain like on Hinge is like waving at someone from across a crowded room and hoping they somehow notice you specifically. Comment. Always. It takes 10 extra seconds and it doubles your chances.

## Is Hinge a Good Dating App? Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Hinge free?

Yes, and the free version is actually usable. **8 likes per day, 1 rose per week, basic preference filters.** You won't have every bell and whistle, but you'll have enough to get matches if your profile doesn't look like it was assembled during a fire drill. Is Hinge a good dating app on the free tier? Better than most.

### How does Hinge work for guys?

Same mechanics, different experience. The **60/40 male/female split** means more competition for men, but it's still way better than Tinder's 75/25 ratio. Your photos matter even more because women on Hinge tend to be pickier (they're here for relationships, not casual validation). We wrote a full guide on [how Hinge works for guys](/blog/how-does-hinge-work-for-guys) if you want the detailed version.

### Is Hinge better than Tinder?

Different tools for different jobs. Hinge is the serious relationship app. Tinder is the volume play. If you want something real, Hinge wins. If you want maximum options with minimal effort, Tinder's your move. We break this down properly in our [Hinge vs Tinder](/blog/is-hinge-better-than-tinder) comparison.

### What is the 8-conversation rule on Hinge?

You can only have **8 unanswered conversations at a time**. If you've sent 8 opening messages and nobody's replied, you're locked out of starting new chats until someone responds or you unmatch. It forces you to actually follow through on conversations instead of collecting matches like they're stamps. Honestly? It's one of the best features on the app. Most people just don't realize it.

### Does Hinge work for people over 40?

Yes, but you'll be in the minority. **90% of Hinge users are 23-36.** That said, the over-40 segment is the fastest-growing demographic on the app. The algorithm doesn't discriminate by age as long as your preferences are set appropriately. And honestly, the "designed for relationships" angle makes Hinge a better fit for the 40+ crowd than any swipe-based alternative.

### Can I reset my Hinge account?

You can, but tread carefully. Deleting and recreating gets you a fresh start, but Hinge tracks device IDs and phone numbers. Do it too often and you risk getting banned entirely.

### What makes "Most Compatible" so special?

It's Hinge's algorithmically curated best guess at who you'd actually click with. Users shown as someone's Most Compatible are **8x more likely to go on a date**. That's not marketing fluff. That's the highest-converting feature on any dating app, period. And three-quarters of first dates from Hinge lead to second dates. Stop ignoring it.

### How many likes do you get on Hinge per day?

**8 likes per day** on the free tier. That's roughly one like every three hours if you spread them out. Hinge+ and HingeX give you unlimited likes, but quality still beats quantity every time.

### Can you get shadowbanned on Hinge?

Yes. If you mass-like everyone, reset your account too often, or get reported multiple times, Hinge can quietly throttle your visibility without telling you. Your profile is technically still there, but nobody's seeing it. It's the dating app equivalent of being a ghost at your own party. If you suspect this happened, you've probably earned it.
