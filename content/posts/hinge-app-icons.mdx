## The Bottom Bar: Your Five New Best Friends (and One Frenemy)

Let's start with the hinge app icons you stare at every single day. Five little symbols across the bottom of your screen, each one a gateway to a different flavor of rejection or hope.

From left to right:

- **H (Discover):** This is home base. The big stylized H that looks like the Hinge logo (because it is). Tap this to browse profiles, like photos, skip people, and generally pretend you're being selective when you're really just killing time on the toilet.
- **Star (Standouts):** The star icon takes you to Hinge's curated "hot people" feed. Profiles here require a [rose to interact with](/blog/hinge-roses), which is Hinge's polite way of saying "these people are out of your league unless you pay."
- **Heart with number (Likes You):** Shows profiles of people who already liked you. Free users see them blurred (because Hinge doesn't believe in free happiness). [Hinge+ and HingeX subscribers](/blog/hinge-x-vs-hinge-plus) can see every face clearly.
- **Chat bubble (Matches):** Your conversations live here. Every match, every unread message, every conversation you let die a slow death. It's a graveyard with a friendly icon.
- **Profile silhouette:** Your profile settings, photos, prompts, and all the things you probably haven't updated since you first downloaded the app.

That's it. Five buttons. If you were struggling with these, I want you to know it's okay. Not everyone grew up with an iPad in their crib.

## Discover Feed Icons: Where the Swiping Actually Happens

The Discover tab is where you'll spend 90% of your Hinge time, so let's decode the hinge symbols you'll see on every profile.

**The heart button** is your Like. Tap it on any photo or prompt to send that specific piece of content as your like. This is the key difference between Hinge and Tinder. You're not liking the whole person (too much commitment). You're liking their second photo or that prompt about their unpopular opinion on pineapple pizza. If you need a deeper walkthrough on this, we wrote a whole guide on [how to like someone on Hinge](/blog/how-to-like-someone-on-hinge).

**The X button** skips the profile. Gone. Poof. Unlike Tinder, there's no dramatic left-swipe animation. Just a cold, clinical X. You can also swipe the card down to skip, if tapping a button feels too committal for your noncommittal ass.

**The rose icon** is the premium super-like. It bumps you to the top of someone's Likes You feed. You get **1 free rose per week** (resets Sunday, no rollover, use it or lose it). Extra roses cost money. Lots of money. The rose is basically Hinge saying "your regular like isn't special enough."

**The comment field** appears after you tap the heart. You can type a message to send along with your like. Do this. Always do this. A like with a comment gets significantly more responses than a naked like. Sending a like without a comment is like showing up to a job interview and just staring at the interviewer.

Free users get **8 likes per day**. That's it. Eight chances to make an impression before Hinge tells you to go outside and touch grass. [Check when those likes reset](/blog/when-do-hinge-likes-reset) if you're the type who needs to optimize every last swipe.

## The Hinge Heart Icon: It Does More Than You Think

The heart is Hinge's Swiss Army knife. It shows up everywhere and means something slightly different each time, which is confusing if you have the attention span of a goldfish (so, most of us).

**Heart on a photo/prompt in Discover:** You're sending a like. This is the primary use. You tap the heart on the specific piece of content that caught your eye, optionally add a comment, and pray.

**Heart icon in messages:** You can "like" individual messages in a conversation, the same way you heart a text on iMessage. It's low-effort acknowledgment. The "I saw your message but have nothing to add" button. Don't rely on it as your entire conversational strategy or you'll end up with a dead chat faster than you can say "so what are you looking for?"

**Heart icon on the bottom nav (Likes You tab):** This one shows you who's already liked your profile. A little red badge with a number appears when new likes come in. Free users see blurred thumbnails (Hinge's "pay up or squint" business model). Subscribers see everything unblurred, which is either exciting or deeply humbling depending on the day.

So when someone asks "what does the heart mean on Hinge?" the honest answer is "depends where you tapped it." Context is everything. Sort of like dating itself.

## Roses and Standouts: The Premium Petal Economy

The star icon on your bottom bar opens the Standouts feed. Think of it as Hinge's VIP section. The algorithm hand-picks profiles it thinks are "top" picks and puts them behind a velvet rope made of roses.

You cannot send a regular like to a Standout profile. Roses only. And since you get **1 free rose per week**, Hinge is essentially showing you a buffet and handing you one fork.

Here's how the rose economy breaks down:

- **1 free rose/week.** Resets every Sunday. Does not accumulate. If you forget to use it, congratulations, you played yourself.
- **Extra roses cost real money** in bundles. Neither Hinge+ nor HingeX gives you extra roses. They're a separate purchase. Always.
- **Most Compatible card:** This pops up at the top of your Discover feed once per day. It's the algorithm's single best guess for you. You can like it for free (no rose needed), which is Hinge throwing you a bone after picking your pocket on Standouts.

Hinge claims that roses are **2x more likely to lead to a date**. They never specify "2x more than what." A regular like? Shouting at someone from across a parking lot? Standing outside their window with a boombox like John Cusack in Say Anything? We'll never know. But the rose does land at the top of someone's feed, so there's logic behind the claim even if the math is conveniently vague.

## Profile Badges That Actually Tell You Something

Beyond the navigation and action icons, Hinge slaps various badges on profiles. Here's what each hinge symbol means when you see it on someone's card.

**Green checkmark (Verified):** This person completed Hinge's selfie verification. They took a real-time photo that matched their profile pics. Does this mean they're not a catfish? Mostly. Does it mean they look exactly like their photos? Absolutely not. We all have camera angles we've mastered. But at minimum, they're a real human being, which is more than you can say about half the profiles on some other apps.

**"New Here" badge:** This profile joined Hinge within the last ~7 days. Fresh meat, basically. These users tend to get a visibility boost from the algorithm because Hinge wants to hook them early (pun painfully intended). If you see this badge, that person is still optimistic about online dating. Give them a week.

**Green dot:** This person is active on Hinge right now or was very recently. It's the "they're holding their phone" indicator. Useful if you want to time your like for maximum impact. Creepy if you think about it too hard. Don't think about it too hard.

**"Active Today" / "Active Yesterday" text:** Shows up below someone's name. Less precise than the green dot but still tells you the person hasn't deleted the app and fled to a monastery. If you see "Active 3 days ago" or later, they're either busy, in a relationship, or they've emotionally moved on without bothering to delete.

**Blue dot:** This one appears in your Matches tab on specific conversations. It means there's unread content. A new message, a new match. Something you haven't looked at yet. It's Hinge's tiny guilt trip reminding you that someone is waiting for a reply you're never going to send.

## Your Turn, Their Turn: The Matches Screen Decoded

The chat bubble icon on your bottom bar takes you to your Matches screen, and Hinge organizes it with a system that functions like a passive-aggressive accountability partner.

**"Your Turn"** means you owe someone a reply. The ball is in your court. Hinge puts these conversations front and center so you can't pretend you "didn't see the message." You saw it. Hinge knows you saw it. The blue dot knows you saw it.

**"Their Turn"** means you're waiting on a response. These conversations get pushed down below Your Turn, which is Hinge's way of saying "stop obsessing over why they haven't texted back and go reply to the people who actually want to talk to you."

In 2024-2025, Hinge rolled out **Your Turn Limits**. If you don't respond to Your Turn conversations within a certain window, the match can expire. This was Hinge's nuclear option against the epidemic of people who collect matches like Pokemon cards but never actually [start a conversation](/blog/how-to-start-a-conversation-on-hinge). Controversial? Yes. Effective at forcing people to engage? Also yes.

There's also a **Match Note** feature (added in 2025) that lets you jot down private notes about a match. Their dog's name, the restaurant they mentioned, the fact that they spelled "you're" wrong three times. Nobody sees these notes but you. Use them. Your memory is worse than you think it is.

## The Boring But Important Stuff (Settings Icons You'll Tap Once and Forget)

Every app has a settings section full of icons nobody thinks about until something goes wrong. Hinge is no different.

**Gear icon (Settings):** Found inside your profile tab. This is where you manage your account, subscription, notifications, and all the administrative stuff that keeps your dating life technically functional.

**Bell icon (Notifications):** Controls what alerts you get. New likes, new messages, the "You haven't opened Hinge in 3 days" guilt trip push notification. Customize this or you'll get pinged every time someone in a 50-mile radius breathes in your direction.

**Sliders icon (Preferences/Filters):** This is where you set your deal-breakers. Age range, distance, height, religion, drinking habits. Hinge+ and HingeX unlock extra filters beyond the basics. If you're still seeing profiles that make you mutter "why is this person in my feed," your filters need work.

**Shield icon (Safety):** Block, report, and manage safety features. If someone is being creepy, this is your button. Use it without guilt. The app exists to connect people, not to give weirdos a platform.

## What About the Actual Hinge App Logo?

Might as well cover this while we're cataloging every visual element like archaeologists at a dig site.

**Hingie** is the name of Hinge's mascot. It's a small, blobby, faceless character that pops up in the app's loading screens and marketing. Hingie has no mouth, which is either a design choice about listening or a commentary on how most people communicate on dating apps (poorly). The fun detail: Hinge says Hingie "dies" when someone deletes the app after finding a real relationship. Morbid branding. I respect it.

The **H lettermark** is the bold "H" you see on the app icon and throughout the interface. Simple, recognizable, looks good at 24 pixels. Not much else to say.

The **H-i ligature** in the Hinge wordmark is a subtle design touch where the H and i connect. It's meant to symbolize two people coming together. Romantic? Sure. Will you ever notice it while frantically swiping at 11 PM? Not a chance.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What do the icons on Hinge mean?

The five bottom navigation icons are: H (Discover feed), Star (Standouts), Heart (Likes You), Chat bubble (Matches), and Profile silhouette. Inside the Discover feed, you'll see a heart (like), X (skip), and rose (premium super-like). Profile badges include the green checkmark (verified), green dot (active now), and "New Here" tag. For a full walkthrough on [how to use Hinge](/blog/how-to-use-hinge), we've got you covered.

### What is the arrow thing on Hinge?

If you're seeing what looks like an arrow or "skip" indicator, that's the X button. Tapping it or swiping down on a profile skips that person. They won't know you passed. There's no Rewind feature on free Hinge (unlike Tinder), so once you skip someone, they're gone unless the algorithm resurfaces them later. Be sure about that X.

### What's the difference between a like and a rose on Hinge?

A like is free (you get **8 per day**) and lands in someone's Likes You pile in chronological order. A rose is a premium like that jumps to the **top** of their feed and signals stronger interest. Roses cost money beyond your 1 free weekly rose. Think of a like as a wave across the room and a rose as walking up and introducing yourself. One gets noticed. [Learn more about the daily like limit](/blog/how-many-likes-a-day-on-hinge).

### Do the Hinge icons change with a subscription?

The icons themselves stay the same. But what's behind them changes. Hinge+ unlocks unlimited likes and unblurs everyone in your Likes You tab. HingeX adds priority likes and enhanced recommendations. The heart still looks like a heart. Your wallet just gets lighter. See our full [Hinge subscription breakdown](/blog/hinge-subscription) if you're weighing whether to upgrade.
