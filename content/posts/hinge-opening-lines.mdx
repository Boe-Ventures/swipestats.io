<TLDR>

### A Reliable Hinge Opener Structure

**specific detail + honest reaction + easy question**

> Your prompt says you plan trips around bakeries, which is an excellent policy. Which city has earned a return visit?

</TLDR>

## How Hinge Opening Lines Work

In Discover, tap the Heart on a specific photo or prompt. You can attach a comment before sending the Like. If the recipient accepts, the comment becomes the starting point for the match.

Hinge's current Convo Starters page says its research found 72% of daters were more likely to consider a potential match when the Like included a message. That describes reported consideration, not a guaranteed response or date.

The best reason to add a comment is simpler: it gives the other person something clear to answer.

## Lines for Prompt Answers

- “You chose the most controversial food opinion possible. What evidence are you bringing to court?”
- “Your ideal Sunday sounds suspiciously organized. Which part is non-negotiable?”
- “I appreciate the specific travel rule. What trip proved it was necessary?”
- “That is a strong two-truths-and-a-lie set. I am guessing the middle one—what did I miss?”
- “You said you want to learn pottery. Are we aiming for mugs or abstract accidents?”

Quote or paraphrase the prompt detail so the comment cannot be mistaken for a mass-send.

## Lines for Photos

- “That view looks worth the climb. Which trail is it?”
- “Your dog appears to be conducting the photo shoot. What is their management style?”
- “Is that meal homemade? I need the story and possibly the recipe.”
- “That concert photo has excellent energy. Who was playing?”
- “The tiny museum sign caught my attention. Was the exhibition as strange as it looks?”

Ask about the story around the photo rather than rating the person's body.

## Travel Openers

- “Which place changed your mind after you arrived?”
- “You have one free weekend in that city. What is the plan?”
- “Best meal from the trip, and what made it memorable?”
- “Was that a carefully planned route or a fortunate wrong turn?”
- “Which destination looked better in real life than online?”

## Food Openers

- “You listed three great restaurants. Which one gets the first-date reservation?”
- “What is the dish you order to judge a new place?”
- “Your baking photo looks professional. What was the hidden failure rate?”
- “Choose one: excellent coffee or excellent pastry?”
- “I disagree with your pizza position, respectfully. State your case.”

## Flirty but Low-Pressure Lines

- “You have a compelling profile. I would like the story behind that last prompt.”
- “This feels like enough overlap for a conversation. Which shared interest should we start with?”
- “Your profile made the Like easy; choosing the first question was harder.”
- “I suspect we would have a good museum disagreement. Want to test that theory?”
- “You seem fun, and I am choosing directness. What are you looking forward to this week?”

Early flirtation should create room, not obligation.

## What Hinge's Convo Starters Actually Do

In supported regions, Hinge may show up to three AI-generated conversation tips based on the public profile. Hinge says the feature does not draft or send a message on the user's behalf.

Treat those tips as topics. Write the final line yourself, verify that it is true to the profile, and avoid repeating personal information in a way that feels invasive.

## Opening-Line Mistakes

- adding a comment unrelated to the selected photo or prompt;
- copying a line that assumes facts not in the profile;
- opening with a sexual remark;
- using an insult as “banter”;
- asking three questions at once;
- following up repeatedly when there is no match or response.

## FAQ

### Can I message before matching on Hinge?

You can attach a comment to a Like. Ordinary chat opens after the recipient accepts and a match is created.

### Is a comment required?

No. Hinge allows a Like without a comment.

### Does commenting double the chance of a date?

Hinge currently reports stronger consideration for Likes with messages, but no public statistic can guarantee an individual outcome.

### Should I compliment a photo?

You can, but a question about the context usually gives the recipient more to work with than a generic appearance compliment.
