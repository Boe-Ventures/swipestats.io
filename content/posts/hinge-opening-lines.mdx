## The Three Ingredients of a Hinge Opening Line That Works

Every good hinge opener has three things. Miss one and you're back in the pile with the "hey beautiful" crowd.

**1. A specific reference to THEIR profile.** Not "nice pics" or "cool bio." Something that proves you actually spent four seconds reading instead of swiping like you're defusing a bomb. Mention the name of the restaurant they listed. Reference the specific mountain they climbed. Quote their own words back at them.

**2. A personality reveal.** Your response needs to show who YOU are, not just that you noticed them. Anyone can say "that's cool." Only you can share your personal hot take about their pineapple-on-pizza stance. Give them a reason to think "this person is actually interesting" instead of "this person has a pulse and internet access."

**3. An easy question.** Low bar to respond. High ceiling to explore. "What's the story behind that photo?" works. "Tell me about your deepest fears and life goals" does not. You're starting a conversation, not conducting a therapy intake.

Here's the rule that ties it all together: **like prompts, not photos.** That 47% stat isn't a coincidence. When you respond to someone's words, you're engaging with their personality. When you like their photo, you're just telling them you have functioning eyeballs. Not exactly a competitive advantage.

Hinge's own research found that "two truths and a lie" tested as the **#1 opener format, performing 31% better than "hey what's up."** And assertive openers like "Free this week?" drove **98% stronger responses** for men. Stop being polite. Start being direct.

## Hinge Opening Lines for Every Prompt Type (The Good Stuff)

Alright. You want lines. I'll give you lines. But I'm also going to tell you WHY each one works, because if you understand the mechanics, you can write your own instead of copy-pasting like a robot with a Hinge subscription.

### "Two Truths and a Lie" Prompts

This is the ultimate hinge icebreaker. Hinge literally tested this and it's the [best performing opener format](/blog/best-hinge-openers) on the platform.

- **"Okay, the skydiving one is DEFINITELY the lie. Nobody who picks that font for their Instagram actually jumps out of planes."** (Works because: specific guess + playful insult = they HAVE to correct you.)
- **"I'm going with #2 as the lie because I refuse to believe anyone has actually eaten a scorpion willingly. But if I'm wrong I need the full story."** (Works because: strong opinion + follow-up demand.)
- **"All three are lies. You made this whole thing up. I can tell."** (Works because: absurd confidence is funny. They'll either laugh or prove you wrong. Win either way.)
- **"I guessed right on the last person's two truths and a lie. I'm 1 for 1 this year and I'm not about to lose my streak. #3 is the lie."** (Works because: turns it into a game with stakes.)

### Travel and Adventure Prompts

Every other person on Hinge has "I love to travel" somewhere in their profile. The bar here is on the floor and yet people still trip over it.

- **"Okay but did you actually eat street food in Bangkok or did you chicken out and find the nearest McDonald's? No judgment. I found a Subway in Marrakech and almost cried with relief."** (Works because: vulnerability + specific detail + gentle tease.)
- **"That Patagonia photo is incredible. How destroyed were your knees after? Mine still file complaints after a casual hike in New Jersey."** (Works because: compliment + self-deprecation + easy response.)
- **"I see you've been to Japan. I need to know your position on conveyor belt sushi. This will determine the entire future of our relationship."** (Works because: escalation humor. Stakes are comically high.)
- **"Everyone says 'I love hiking' on here but I need specifics. Are we talking a paved trail with a gift shop at the top, or 'might die out here' energy?"** (Works because: calls out the cliche while genuinely asking what they mean.)

### Food and Restaurant Prompts

Food opinions are the easiest debate starters on the planet. Nobody is neutral about food.

- **"Your pizza take is wrong and I can prove it with a blind taste test. Name the borough."** (Works because: challenge + call to action. You just proposed a date without saying "want to go on a date?")
- **"Okay so you listed tacos as your weakness. I respect that. But I need to know: hard shell or soft? This is a dealbreaker and I'm not sorry."** (Works because: treats their answer with fake gravity. Fun and easy to respond to.)
- **"The fact that you put ranch on pizza tells me everything I need to know about you as a person. And somehow I'm still here."** (Works because: mock horror + "I'm still interested" subtext.)
- **"I'm genuinely offended by your sushi restaurant pick. I know a place that'll change your life. Or at least your Thursday night."** (Works because: confident + low-key date suggestion.)
- **"You said you can cook Italian but can you make carbonara without cream? Because 90% of people can't and I'm tired of being disappointed."** (Works because: specific challenge. Shows YOU know food too.)

### Music and Entertainment Prompts

Nobody can resist defending their taste in music. It's wired into our DNA right next to "fight or flight."

- **"I see Taylor Swift in your top artists and I just want you to know I will learn every word to every album if that's what it takes. I'm not above it."** (Works because: self-awareness about effort. Funny because honest.)
- **"Your music taste is either really good or a cry for help and I genuinely can't tell which. Walk me through your top 5."** (Works because: playful ambiguity + genuine curiosity.)
- **"Hot take: your #1 show isn't even the best show by that creator. I will die on this hill. Come fight me."** (Works because: debate invitation. People love defending things they love.)
- **"Okay we need to talk about the fact that you listed both country AND EDM. I need to understand the person who contains these multitudes."** (Works because: treats the contradiction as fascinating, not weird.)

### "The Way to Win Me Over Is..." and Similar Prompts

These prompts are literally an instruction manual. They're telling you exactly what to say. And somehow people still blow it.

- **"You said the way to win you over is 'making me laugh so hard I snort.' Challenge accepted. I'm workshopping material as we speak. Expect callbacks."** (Works because: directly addresses their stated desire + shows effort.)
- **"You want someone who remembers the little things? I remember my coworker's cousin's dog's name. You're in safe hands."** (Works because: absurd specificity proves the point hilariously.)
- **"'Cook me dinner and don't be weird about it.' I can do exactly one of those things and I'm not telling you which."** (Works because: honest self-deprecation + mystery.)

### The Controversial Opinion / Hot Take Prompts

This is where you get to be polarizing. And polarizing is good. **85% of daters say they're more likely to want a second date** with someone who asks thoughtful questions. Engaging with their hot take IS a thoughtful question.

- **"I ALSO think cereal is a soup and I've been waiting my whole life for someone to agree with me on this. Everyone looks at me like I'm unhinged."** (Works because: intense agreement creates instant bonding.)
- **"Your hot take about movie theaters is so wrong it physically hurt me to read it. Let me take you to one and prove my case."** (Works because: disagreement + date invite in one sentence. Smooth.)
- **"I need you to defend this opinion in 3 sentences or less. If you can't, you have to buy me coffee."** (Works because: gamification + stakes + date suggestion.)
- **"This is the most unhinged opinion I've ever seen on this app and I'm including the guy who said water is a seasoning. I respect it."** (Works because: escalation humor + respect. You're entertained, not offended.)

## Hinge Opening Lines for Photos (When the Prompts Give You Nothing)

Some people put zero effort into their prompts and leave you with nothing but photos to work with. This is annoying. But it's not a death sentence if you know [how to comment on Hinge](/blog/how-to-like-someone-on-hinge) the right way.

### Travel Photos

- **"Where is that? It looks like the kind of place where the wifi doesn't work and you're somehow happier because of it."** (Works because: specific observation + relatable insight.)
- **"I can tell by your tan in that photo that you were there at least a week. Where'd you stay? I'm building my 'pretend I have PTO' travel list."** (Works because: humor + genuine question.)

### Pet Photos

This is the cheat code. Literally the easiest layup in online dating.

- **"I need to be upfront: I'm here for your dog. If things work out between us that's a bonus but the dog is priority #1."** (Works because: everyone thinks this is funny even though everyone says it. The delivery is what matters.)
- **"What's your dog's opinion on strangers? Because I need to know if I'm going to have to win over two of you."** (Works because: assumes the relationship is progressing. Confident and funny.)

### Activity / Hobby Photos

- **"Okay so you surf. Please rate your skill level honestly on a scale from 'stood up once and cried' to 'could probably teach lessons.'"** (Works because: the scale is funny. They'll want to place themselves on it.)
- **"I see you climbing in that photo and I just want you to know I can barely climb a flight of stairs without needing a water break. Are you okay dating below your altitude?"** (Works because: self-deprecation + direct "dating" mention.)

### When the Profile Is Basically Empty (The Nuclear Option)

Some profiles give you absolutely nothing. No prompts, bad photos, maybe a first name and a distance. This is where most people give up. Fair enough. But if you're determined:

- **"Your profile is giving 'witness protection program' vibes and honestly I respect the mystery. Give me one fact about yourself and I'll give you one back."** (Works because: acknowledges the elephant in the room while making it a game.)
- **"I'm going to be honest, your profile gave me very little to work with so I'm just going to ask: what's the last thing that made you laugh so hard you couldn't breathe?"** (Works because: direct honesty is disarming. The question is easy and fun to answer.)

## Voice Notes: The Cheat Code You're Ignoring

Here's something that surprised me in the data. **Voice notes boost your odds of getting a date by 40-48%.** That's a bigger advantage than almost any text-based opener.

Think about why. Text is flat. You can't hear tone, humor, confidence, or warmth through words on a screen. A voice note fixes all of that in 15 seconds.

**65% of daters say hearing someone's voice helps them gauge genuine interest.** And **35% of Gen Z specifically want more voice notes** on dating apps. The demand is there. The supply is not. Which means if you send one, you immediately stand out.

The data shows most voice notes get sent around **10 PM EST** (likes peak at 8 PM, messages at 9 PM). That late-night window is when people are most receptive to something personal and unexpected.

### How to Actually Use Voice Notes as Hinge Openers

Don't overthink this. A voice note opener should be:

- **15-30 seconds max.** This is not a podcast episode. Get in, make a point, get out.
- **Reference something from their profile.** Same rule as text. Specific beats generic.
- **Smile while you talk.** People can hear a smile. This sounds corny. It's also true.
- **End with a question.** Give them something to respond to.

Example: "Hey, I saw your prompt about the worst cooking disaster you've ever had and I just need to tell you that I once set off my smoke alarm making toast. Not even fancy toast. Regular toast. So I need to know, can yours top that?"

That's it. 20 seconds. Personal, funny, and impossible to ignore. Way more memorable than the 400th "love that photo!" sitting in their inbox.

## Mistakes That Murder Your Hinge Opening Lines Before They Land

### Saying "Hey" (The Data on Why It's Dead)

Only 1 in 500 Hinge conversations start with "hey." Not because it's banned. Because it works so poorly that almost nobody bothers anymore. "Hey" communicates one thing: you have nothing to say but felt obligated to say something. It's the wet handshake of [starting a conversation on Hinge](/blog/how-to-start-a-conversation-on-hinge). Just stop.

### Appearance-Only Compliments

"You're gorgeous." "Love your smile." "Your eyes are stunning." These tell her you find her attractive. She already knew that. You swiped right. The whole system is built on mutual attraction. You're not delivering news. You're just filling space.

Go read our guide on [how to compliment a girl's picture](/blog/how-to-compliment-a-girls-picture) if you genuinely don't know what else to say. It's better than this.

### Too Forward Too Fast

There's a canyon between "assertive" and "creepy." Assertive is "Free this week? I know a great spot for tacos." Creepy is anything that mentions their body, implies a physical encounter, or uses the phrase "we should cuddle." Read the room. The room is your phone screen and she can screenshot your message.

### Copy-Pasting the Same Line to Everyone

They can tell. They always can tell. You know that opener you send to every match because it "works pretty well"? It works pretty well the way a form letter works pretty well. Nobody feels special reading it. And **43% of Gen Z women admit they wait for the other person to initiate deeper conversation**, which means your first impression is doing ALL the heavy lifting. Don't waste it on a template.

### Waiting Days to Respond

The 24-hour cliff is real. **72% more likely to get a date** when you respond within a day. For men, the response rate drops **25% after just 6 hours.** Every hour you wait, the conversation gets colder. She matched with other people. She forgot what your profile looked like. She moved on.

Hinge built Your Turn Limits specifically because this was such a massive problem. The feature increased responsiveness by **20%**. Take the hint.

### What are the best hinge opening lines for guys?

Also consider that Hinge launched its Convo Starters AI feature in December 2025, which means even the app itself knows you need help. Reference their prompts. Be specific. Be confident. Don't be weird.

### Do comments really work better than likes on Hinge?

Yes, and it's not close. Comments are **2x more likely to lead to a date.** Likes on prompts are **47% more likely to lead to dates** than likes on photos. If you're using Hinge without sending comments, you're playing the game on hard mode for no reason.

### What to comment on Hinge when you don't know what to say?

Start with their text prompts. Pick the one you have the strongest reaction to and respond honestly. If nothing sparks a reaction, ask yourself whether you're actually interested in this person or just swiping. If you genuinely can't think of anything, a voice note saying "I saw your profile and wanted to reach out but honestly your prompts left me speechless, so tell me something about yourself that would surprise me" is better than a generic like.

### What is the 8 conversation rule on Hinge?

The 8 conversation rule is a community guideline suggesting you aim to have at least 8 back-and-forth messages before suggesting a date. It's not an official Hinge feature. The idea is that [8 exchanges build enough rapport](/blog/how-to-use-hinge) to make a date suggestion feel natural rather than rushed. That said, assertive openers that suggest meeting sooner perform 98% better for men. So use 8 messages as a rough guide, not a law.
