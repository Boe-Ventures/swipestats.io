<TLDR>

### Send a Hinge Like

1. Open Discover.
2. Tap the Heart on a specific photo or prompt.
3. Optionally add a short, relevant comment.
4. Tap Send Like.

In Standouts, the equivalent action uses a Rose. If someone likes you first, review them in Likes You and choose Match or skip.

</TLDR>

## How Likes Work in Discover

Hinge asks you to Like a particular part of a profile rather than the profile in the abstract. Tap the Heart beside a photo or prompt, add a comment if there is something genuine to say, and confirm with **Send Like**.

If the recipient accepts it, the profile moves to Matches and either person can continue the conversation. A sent Like is an invitation, not a message receipt or a promise that the person has reviewed it.

## Should You Add a Comment?

Hinge's current Convo Starters documentation says its research found daters are more likely to consider a potential match when the Like includes a message. The strongest comment usually proves you read the profile and gives the other person an easy opening.

Try:

- a specific question about the place or activity in a photo;
- a brief response to the actual prompt;
- a playful disagreement that stays respectful;
- a concrete shared interest.

Avoid generic compliments, sexual comments, copied openers, and interview-style question lists. A plain Like is fine when no natural comment comes to mind; forced personalization is not better personalization.

## What Happens When Someone Likes You?

Incoming Likes appear in the **Likes You** tab. Tap the Chat icon to match, with or without an opening message, or skip when the profile is not a fit.

Hinge+ and HingeX include subscriber tools for seeing incoming Likes more broadly. The free app still supports matching, messaging, and a limited number of outgoing Likes.

Hinge does not promise one permanent free-Like number for every account and market. Use the counter and reset information shown in your app.

## Likes vs. Roses vs. Priority Likes

| Action | Where it is used | What Hinge says it changes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Like | Discover | Sends interest on a photo or prompt |
| Rose | Standouts or Discover | Places the sender at the top of Likes You |
| Priority Like | HingeX | Gives the Like higher placement for a period of time |

Placement is not acceptance. Roses and Priority Likes can make interest more visible, but the recipient still decides whether to match.

All users can access Standouts, where sending interest requires a Rose. Hinge says users receive one free Rose each week and that free Roses do not accumulate.

## Can You Undo a Like?

Hinge documents an undo control for only the most recent **Skip**. Its current Discover guide does not document recalling a Like after you confirm Send Like.

Review the selected photo, comment, and profile before sending. If you later match and no longer want contact, you can unmatch, but that permanently removes the conversation.

## A Better Selection Rule

Send a Like when you would genuinely want to talk to this person—not because you are trying to satisfy a daily quota. Check relationship intentions and obvious incompatibilities first, then choose the photo or prompt that creates the clearest conversation.

No public formula guarantees that a certain comment length, sending time, or purchase will produce a match. Those claims confuse anecdotes with product mechanics.

## FAQ

### Can I Like an entire Hinge profile?

The app asks you to select a specific photo or prompt, then send the Like from there.

### Can I message before matching?

You can attach a comment to a Like. Full chat becomes available when the other person accepts and you match.

### Do comments guarantee more matches?

No. Hinge reports stronger consideration for Likes with messages, but no comment guarantees an individual response.

### Can free users send Likes?

Yes, with a limit shown in the app. Hinge+ and HingeX offer unlimited outgoing Likes.
