## Does Hinge Have a Customer Service Phone Number? (Spoiler: No)

Let me save you twenty minutes of Googling increasingly desperate variations of "hinge support phone number." Hinge has zero phone support. None. You cannot call a human being at Hinge and explain your situation through tears. That's not a service they offer.

Here's what actually exists:

- **In-app support:** Settings > Help Center. It's a chatbot that occasionally connects you to a real person. Think customer service roulette.
- **Email:** hello@hinge.co. This is your best bet for account-specific issues. Write clearly, be polite, and prepare to wait.
- **Help center:** help.hinge.co. A graveyard of FAQ articles that may or may not address your specific problem.
- **Contact form:** hinge.co/contact. Same as email but with extra steps.

There IS a corporate phone number floating around (+1 310-759-9580) buried in their privacy policy. Before you get excited, that's Match Group's corporate line. It's for investors and legal inquiries, not for you to call and ask why your match disappeared. For that, read our guide on [Hinge match disappearing](/blog/hinge-match-disappeared).

**Set realistic expectations.** Responses take days, not minutes. If you're locked out of your account on a Friday night, you're watching Netflix this weekend. I emailed Hinge support once about a bug and got a response four days later. By then I'd already fixed it, gone on two dates, and forgotten I'd sent the email. Their turnaround makes the DMV look efficient.

## Do You Need a Phone Number for Hinge?

Short answer: not anymore, technically.

Long answer: for years, a phone number was the ONLY way to create a Hinge account. No phone, no swiping. Hinge used it as their primary anti-catfish mechanism. One phone number equals one account. Simple, effective, and annoying if you're the kind of person who changes numbers like you change Spotify playlists.

But Hinge has loosened up. You can now sign up with:

- **Phone number (SMS):** Still the default and recommended method.
- **Email:** The newer alternative. Works fine.
- **Google sign-in:** For the Android faithful.
- **Apple sign-in:** For people who spend $1,200 on a phone and want to use every feature.

So does Hinge require a phone number? Not strictly. But the phone method is still what Hinge pushes first, and it's what most troubleshooting guides assume you're using. If you're creating a fresh account and don't want to hand over your number, email or Apple sign-in will get you through the door.

The one-account-per-phone-number rule still applies though. Hinge isn't stupid. Well, their algorithm is debatable. But their identity verification isn't.

## Hinge Phone Number Verification: How It Actually Works

If you go the phone route, here's the play-by-play that somehow still confuses people:

1. Open Hinge. Tap "Sign in with Phone."
2. Enter your phone number. Use your real one. Not your ex's. Not a random number you found.
3. Hinge sends a **6-digit verification code** via SMS from a short code number.
4. Enter the code. Done.

That's it. Four steps. Fewer steps than making instant ramen, and yet the internet is full of people who can't get past step 3.

A few things worth knowing:

- **VoIP numbers get rejected.** Google Voice, TextNow, Burner, and most virtual numbers don't work. Hinge actively blocks VoIP ranges because people use them to create multiple accounts or dodge bans. If you're thinking "I'll just use my Google Voice number," save yourself the frustration.
- **One number, one account.** If your number is already tied to a Hinge account (even a deleted one), you might hit a wall. The system remembers.
- **Account recovery relies on it.** If you lose access and your number is your only login method, that number is your lifeline. Treat it accordingly.

The "We Met" feature, selfie verification, and account recovery all tie back to your login method. So whatever you use to sign up, keep access to it.

## Hinge Not Sending Verification Code? Here's the Fix

This is the part where 80% of you landed. Your code isn't arriving, you've tapped "Resend" fourteen times, and now you're convinced Hinge hates you personally. Probably not. (Probably.) Let's troubleshoot.

### Check the obvious stuff first

- **Signal and connection.** SMS needs cellular service, not WiFi. If you're in a basement or a Faraday cage disguised as a building, step outside. I once spent ten minutes raging at Tinder in a parking garage before realizing I had zero bars. Don't be me.
- **Full inbox.** Some phones silently reject SMS when storage is full. Clear your messages if you're hoarding texts from 2019 like they're love letters.

### The real culprits

- **Carrier blocking short codes.** This is the number one reason verification codes don't arrive, and almost nobody thinks of it. Prepaid carriers (Cricket, Boost, Metro) are notorious for blocking short code messages by default. Call your carrier and ask them to enable short code SMS. It's free and takes five minutes.
- **"Too many requests" error.** You got rate-limited. Hinge caps how many codes you can request in a window. Stop tapping resend and **wait 24 hours**. Seriously. Go outside. Touch grass. The app will still be there tomorrow.
- **Outdated app version.** Check your App Store or Play Store. Outdated versions of Hinge have known issues with verification. Update first, troubleshoot second.

### If none of that works

- **Try email verification instead.** This is the backup nobody talks about. If your phone number won't cooperate, switch to email sign-up. It bypasses the SMS system entirely.
- **Contact Hinge support.** Email hello@hinge.co with your phone number, a description of the issue, and the words "verification code not arriving." They've seen it a thousand times and can sometimes push it through on their end.

## Can You Use Hinge Without a Phone Number?

Yes. And I'll be honest about why most people are asking this question.

**The legitimate reasons:** You don't have a phone number (international users, VoIP-only setups), you want more privacy, or your carrier blocks short codes and you're sick of dealing with it. Totally valid. Use email, Google, or Apple sign-in and move on with your life.

**The less legitimate reason:** You got [banned from Hinge](/blog/hinge-ban) and you're trying to sneak back in with a virtual number.

Look, I'm not your mom. But I'll tell you what Hinge confirmed themselves: **they retain ban data indefinitely.** Not six months. Not a year. Forever. And they don't just check your phone number. They check:

- Your device ID
- Your home WiFi network (yes, really)
- Payment information tied to your account
- Photos and profile data

So you can get a burner number, a new email, and a fake mustache, and Hinge might still figure out it's you. Google Voice specifically is a coin flip. Sometimes it works, usually it doesn't. Hinge has gotten progressively better at detecting VoIP ranges.

If you're dealing with a ban you think was unfair, your best move is to appeal it directly rather than trying to play James Bond with burner phones. If you want to compare how Tinder handles the same situation, we covered that in our [Tinder phone number](/blog/tinder-phone-number) guide.

And if you're curious whether you might be [shadowbanned](/blog/hinge-shadowban) instead of fully banned, that's a different problem entirely. Worth checking before you nuke everything and start over.

## How to Change Your Hinge Phone Number (The Bad News)

Here's where Hinge earns its reputation for being, shall we say, less than flexible.

**You cannot change your phone number in the app.** There is no setting for this. No menu option. No buried toggle. It does not exist.

If you need to change the phone number associated with your Hinge account, here is the official process:

1. Email hello@hinge.co
2. Provide your old phone number
3. Request account deletion
4. Wait for them to process it
5. Create a brand new account with your new number

Read that again. They delete your **entire account**. Every match. Every conversation. Every carefully crafted response to "What's your most irrational fear." Gone. Obliterated. Like it never happened. It's the dating app equivalent of burning down your house because you wanted to repaint the kitchen. Thanos-snapping your love life because you switched to T-Mobile.

Is this good design? No. Is it the reality? Yes.

**Prevention tip:** Link your Apple ID or Google account to your Hinge profile NOW, while everything is working fine. That way if your phone number changes, you still have a way to log in. Think of it as an insurance policy for your love life. You wouldn't drive without car insurance (I hope), so don't swipe without a backup login.

If you're considering a full [account reset](/blog/how-to-reset-hinge) anyway, losing your data might not sting as much. But if you've got active conversations with someone you actually like, for the love of everything, switch your login method before you change carriers.

## What Hinge Actually Does With Your Phone Number

This is the part where I put on my boring-but-important hat. Your phone number on Hinge is private. Matches cannot see it. Random people cannot look you up by number. If someone claims they found you on Hinge through your phone number, they're lying. They found you the old-fashioned way: by swiping.

Here's what Hinge uses your number for:

- **Verification:** Proving you're a real person and not a bot selling crypto.
- **Account recovery:** Getting back into your account when you inevitably forget your password.
- **One-account enforcement:** Making sure you're not running three profiles because one isn't getting enough likes.

### The privacy policy breakdown

Hinge updated their privacy policy in November 2025, and here's what matters:

- **After you delete your account:** Hinge deletes your phone number data within **3 months** of account closure. Not instant, but not forever either.
- **Data sharing:** Hinge shares your phone number with "service providers" for customer care, security, and payment processing. Standard stuff. They're not selling it to telemarketers.
- **Your rights:** You can request access to your data or request deletion at any time. Hinge is required to comply under GDPR and CCPA, depending on where you live.

If privacy is your primary concern and you haven't signed up yet, Apple sign-in with Hide My Email is probably your most private option. It gives Hinge a relay email address instead of your real one, and you never have to hand over a phone number at all. ## FAQ

### Does Hinge have a phone number for customer service?

No. There is no Hinge support phone number. Contact them at hello@hinge.co, through in-app support, or via help.hinge.co. The corporate number in their privacy policy is for Match Group corporate inquiries, not your account issues.

### Can you find someone on Hinge by their phone number?

No. Hinge does not have a phone number search feature. You can't type in someone's number and find their profile. Discovery is based on Hinge's algorithm, location, and preferences. Not phone numbers.

### Why did I get a Hinge verification code I didn't request?

Two possibilities, and one of them is awkward. Either someone mistyped their phone number during sign-up and accidentally entered yours. Or someone with access to your number is trying to create a Hinge account. Like, say, a partner who told you they deleted the app. Do with that information what you will. Don't enter the code either way.

### Can I use Google Voice for Hinge?

Usually no. Hinge actively blocks most VoIP number ranges, and Google Voice falls into that category more often than not. Some people report it working, but it's unreliable enough that I wouldn't count on it. Use a real carrier number or go with email sign-up instead.

### How many Hinge accounts can you have per phone number?

One. Hinge enforces a strict one-account-per-phone-number policy. If you try to create a second account with the same number, you'll either be asked to log into the existing account or be blocked entirely.
