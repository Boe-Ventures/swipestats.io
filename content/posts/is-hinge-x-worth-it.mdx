## What Is Hinge X? (The Membership Nobody Can Explain Properly)

So. What is Hinge X?

It's Hinge's top-tier premium subscription. The one that used to be called "Hinge Preferred" before someone in marketing decided that name didn't sound expensive enough. It sits at the top of a three-tier system that looks something like this:

| Feature | Free | Hinge+ ($30/mo) | Hinge X ($50/mo) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily likes | 8 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| See who liked you | 1 at a time | All at once | All at once |
| Advanced filters | No | Yes | Yes |
| Roses per week | 1 | 1 | 1 (+ bonus) |
| Priority Likes | No | No | Yes |
| Enhanced Recommendations | No | No | Yes |
| Skip the Line | No | No | Yes |

If you're on free Hinge right now, rationing your 8 daily likes like emergency rations during a zombie apocalypse, I feel you. Eight likes is barely enough to swipe through your morning commute. And if you're swiping right at the average male rate of 53% (which [our data from 7,000+ profiles](/blog/dating-app-statistics) confirms), those 8 likes evaporate faster than your confidence after a week of no matches.

The real question isn't "what is Hinge X." It's whether those extra features justify the extra $20 over [Hinge+](/blog/is-hinge-plus-worth-it). Let's break them down.

## What Does Hinge X Actually Give You? (Besides a Lighter Wallet)

### Skip the Line

This is the headline feature. The one Hinge uses to justify charging you more than a Netflix subscription.

Skip the Line is essentially an always-on passive boost. Your profile sits near the top of compatible users' Discover feeds. Not for 60 minutes like a regular [Hinge Boost](/blog/hinge-boost) (which costs $10 per pop). Twenty-four hours a day, seven days a week, for as long as you're subscribed.

In a city like New York or London where there are 100,000+ users, this matters. Your profile isn't rotting on page 47 of someone's Discover feed while they run out of patience at page 3. In a town with 500 Hinge users? You're probably already showing up for everyone anyway. Save your money.

### Priority Likes

When you like someone on Hinge X, your like surfaces at the top of their "Likes You" tab and stays there for 7 days. After that, it sinks back to wherever it would normally sit.

Think of it as cutting the line at a club. Except the club is someone's phone screen, and the bouncer is Hinge's algorithm. No badge. No indicator. The other person has zero idea you paid for this. Which is probably for the best, because "I paid $50/month to make sure you see my face first" isn't exactly a flex.

It's a queue-position advantage. Not a magic wand. If your opening line is "hey" and your first photo looks like it was taken with a potato, Priority Likes just means they'll reject you faster. Congrats.

### Enhanced Recommendations

This is where things get... hand-wavy.

Enhanced Recommendations supposedly uses your real-time behavior (likes, skips, how long you linger on a profile like a creep) to surface more compatible matches. In theory, the algorithm learns what you want and delivers better options.

In practice? There's a well-documented "frontloading" phenomenon. The first 20 or so profiles in each session on Hinge X are noticeably more attractive. Then the quality drops off a cliff like Wile E. Coyote running out of road. Some users swear it works. Others call it a straight-up placebo. I've tried it, and honestly? The first few profiles each session felt like Hinge was rolling out the red carpet. Then profile 25 hit and it felt like the algorithm forgot I was paying.

This is the most skeptically received feature of the bunch, and for good reason.

### The Hinge+ Stuff You Also Get

Hinge X includes everything from Hinge+, which means:

- **Unlimited likes.** No more 8-a-day rations. Swipe to your heart's content (or until carpal tunnel sets in).
- **See who liked you.** The full queue, not one breadcrumb at a time. This alone is worth the price of Hinge+ for most guys.
- **Advanced filters.** Height, education, politics, drinking, kids. Finally, you can filter out people who think astrology is a personality trait.

These are the features that actually move the needle. The Hinge X exclusives are nice-to-haves stacked on top.

## How Much Does Hinge X Cost? (Prepare Your Credit Card)

Here's the damage:

| Plan | Total Price | Per Month |
|---|---|---|
| 1 month | $49.99 | $49.99 |
| 3 months | $99.99 | ~$33.33 |
| 6 months | $149.99 | ~$25.00 |

Big caveat: **Hinge uses dynamic pricing based on your age and location.** So the guy in Austin might pay less than the guy in Manhattan. Hinge hasn't exactly published their pricing matrix (why would they, that would be honest), so your mileage may vary.

For context, [Tinder Platinum](/blog/tinder-platinum) runs about $40/month and Bumble Premium will set you back $60/month. Hinge X sits comfortably in the middle of the "I'm paying too much for this" spectrum.

Let's do some napkin math. If Hinge X gets you roughly 12 matches per 500 likes (based on the test data below), and you're sending 500 likes over the course of a month on the $50 plan, that's about **$4.17 per match**. On the free tier, you're capped at roughly 240 likes per month (8/day x 30 days). Even if you matched at the same rate, you'd get maybe 5-6 matches. For free.

So the real cost of Hinge X isn't just the subscription. It's whether those extra matches turn into actual dates or just more conversations that die after three messages.

## Does Hinge X Actually Work? (Someone Ran the Numbers)

This is the part you actually came for. Let's talk data.

YouTube creator Jerrad Ross ran a controlled experiment. Same profile, same photos, 500 likes on each tier. Here's what happened:

| Tier | Likes Sent | Matches | Match Rate | Time to Complete |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Hinge X | 500 | 12 | 2.4% | ~2 weeks |
| Hinge+ | 500 | 8 | 1.6% | ~2 weeks |
| Free | 500 | 10 | 2.0% | ~2 months |

**Hinge X produced 50% more matches than Hinge+** with the same effort. That's real. Using the same photos, Hinge X also pulled 2x more matches than Tinder Platinum in a similar test. So if you're weighing Hinge vs Tinder, Hinge's premium tier seems to deliver more bang for your buck.

But here's the part that's going to hurt.

Notice how Free (10 matches) actually beat Hinge+ (8 matches)? The free tier took way longer because of the 8 likes/day cap, but the raw match rate was higher. This tells you something uncomfortable: paying for Hinge+ without Skip the Line doesn't necessarily get you more matches. It just gets you through the stack faster. The algorithm might even treat paying users differently in ways we can't fully see.

Another creator, DI Lee, spent a full week going hard on Hinge X. His takeaway? "So many matches." Sounds great, right? Except most of those matches ghosted. He booked exactly **1 actual date** from a week of heavy usage. One. He called the experience an "unhealthy relationship with my phone" and pointed out that unlimited likes removed the natural stopping point that free Hinge forces on you. Without that brake pedal, he was swiping like a lab rat pressing a pellet button.

Hinge also has a built-in throttle: if you have 8 or more matches awaiting a response, the app blocks new matches until you deal with the ones you have. So even if Skip the Line is working overtime, Hinge itself will pump the brakes if you're hoarding matches like some kind of digital dragon sitting on a pile of unread conversations.

Here's another fun data point from DI Lee's testing: he sent **70 personalized comments/openers and got exactly 0 responses.** Just hitting "like" without a comment worked better. Which means all those hours you spend crafting the perfect opening line? Maybe don't bother. (I'm half kidding. Good prompts and openers still matter. But the data is brutal.)

The honest takeaway: **more matches does not equal more dates.** The quality of your profile, your photos, and your ability to hold a conversation still matter more than any subscription tier. Hinge X turns up the volume. It doesn't change the song.

## Hinge X vs Hinge Plus: Is the Extra $20 Worth It?

This is the real decision for most people. You've already decided you're willing to pay for Hinge. The question is whether $30/month (Hinge+) or $50/month (Hinge X) is the right call.

Hinge+ gives you the features that actually change how you use the app:

- Unlimited likes (huge)
- See all your incoming likes (huge)
- Advanced filters (genuinely useful)

Hinge X adds the visibility layer:

- Skip the Line (the real differentiator)
- Priority Likes (helpful but not transformative)
- Enhanced Recommendations (jury's still out)
- All Hinge+ features included

So the $20/month question boils down to: **is passive visibility worth it?**

If you're in NYC, LA, London, or any city where the user pool is massive? Probably yes. Skip the Line actually has room to push you ahead of thousands of other profiles. That's meaningful.

If you're in a mid-size city or smaller market? Probably not. The pool is small enough that you're already showing up for most compatible users. You're paying $20/month for a turbo boost in a parking lot.

For a deeper breakdown of the Hinge+ side of this equation, check out our full [Hinge Plus review](/blog/is-hinge-plus-worth-it). We go way deeper on whether the base subscription is worth it. Spoiler: for most guys, yes. For most women, no.

## Who Should Get Hinge X (And Who's Flushing Money Down the Toilet)

### The Sprint Dater

You just moved to a new city. Or you just got out of a relationship and you're ready to get back out there with the desperation of someone who just realized their ex already has a new profile up. You want maximum results in 1-2 months. You'll subscribe, go hard, then cancel.

This is the ideal Hinge X user. The sprint strategy works because Hinge's user pool replenishes when you take a break. Leave for a month, come back, and there's a fresh batch of people who haven't seen your face yet. Subscribe, blitz, cancel, repeat if needed.

### The Big City Dweller

Skip the Line and Priority Likes matter when there are 100,000+ users competing for attention. In Manhattan, your profile is one of thousands being shown in any given Discover session. The visibility boost from Hinge X actually has room to work.

### The Profile-Optimized User

Your photos are solid. Your prompts are good. You get some matches already. Hinge X amplifies what's already working. It's steroids for a body that's already in shape. You won't win a bodybuilding competition if you've never been to a gym. (Sorry, that metaphor got away from me.)

### Who Should NOT Get Hinge X

Let me be clear about who is absolutely lighting money on fire:

- **Your photos look like they were taken by a security camera in 2011.** No amount of Skip the Line saves a blurry selfie in a dimly lit bathroom. Fix your profile photos first.
- **You live in a town with 3 Hinge users** (and one of them is your ex). Skip the Line doesn't help when there's no line to skip.
- **You think paying $50/month will fix your personality.** It won't. Hinge X makes your profile more visible. If your profile is a dumpster fire, you're just making the dumpster fire more visible. Good job.
- **You're already getting decent matches on Hinge+ or free.** If the current system is working, don't throw money at it out of impatience. That's like remodeling a kitchen because dinner took 10 minutes longer than you wanted.
- **You can't afford it without wincing.** If $50/month puts a dent in your budget, spend that money on a better haircut and a friend with a decent camera. The ROI will be better, I promise.

## The Verdict: Is Hinge X Worth It?

Let me make this simple.

**Worth it for:** Serious daters in major cities with already-optimized profiles who want to run a 1-3 month sprint. Get the 3-month plan at $33/month. Use it aggressively. Cancel when you've got a few solid connections going.

**Not worth it for:** Everyone else. And I mean that. If you're in a smaller market, if your profile needs work, or if you're already matching at a rate you're happy with, Hinge X is an expensive upgrade that won't move the needle enough to justify the cost.

**The compromise:** Hinge+ at $30/month. Unlimited likes and seeing your full like queue are the two features that actually change your daily experience on the app. Everything else is gravy.

And look. I've said it before and I'll say it again. No subscription saves a bad profile. Not Hinge X. Not Tinder Platinum. Not Bumble Premium. If you're not getting matches now, the answer isn't to spend more money. The answer is to [fix your photos](/blog/best-dating-profile-photos), write [better prompts](/blog/best-hinge-prompts), and be someone worth swiping right on.

Once you've done that? Yeah. Hinge X might be the accelerant you need.

## FAQ

### Does Hinge X give you unlimited likes?

Yes. But so does Hinge+. This isn't a reason to upgrade from Hinge+ to Hinge X. It's a reason to upgrade from free to either paid tier. When your likes reset stops mattering once you're paying.

### Can people see if you have Hinge X?

No. There's no badge, no indicator, no little crown icon next to your name. Your secret is safe. Nobody will ever know you dropped $50/month to show up in their feed.

### Is Hinge X worth it for guys?

More so than for women, yeah. Men typically have lower match rates on Hinge (and every other app). At SwipeStats, we've seen this across our dataset of 294 million swipes. If you're a guy, the visibility boost from Skip the Line has a bigger relative impact because you're starting from a lower baseline. Still need a good profile though. This isn't charity.

### Is Hinge X worth it for women?

Usually not. Women generally receive significantly more likes on the free tier already. Paying for visibility when you're already visible is like buying a spotlight for a lighthouse. Hinge is generally a strong app for relationship-seeking daters regardless of subscription tier.

### How much does Hinge X cost per month?

$49.99/month on the monthly plan. $33.33/month on the 3-month plan ($99.99 total). $25/month on the 6-month plan ($149.99 total). But Hinge uses dynamic pricing, so your actual number might differ based on your age and location. Check the app for your real price.

### What happened to Hinge Preferred?

It got rebranded to Hinge X. Same concept, new name. If you're searching for "Hinge Preferred membership" info, you're in the right place. It's this. You're looking at it.

### Can I get a Hinge X discount?

Hinge doesn't openly advertise discounts, but they do offer dynamic pricing that sometimes results in lower costs for younger users or less competitive markets. The 3-month and 6-month plans also effectively give you a discount over the monthly rate. No promo codes floating around though. Sorry.
