<TLDR>

You cannot text somebody into liking you. You can make your own interest clear and see whether he meets you there:

> I enjoyed talking with you last night. You mentioned the new food market—want to go together Saturday?

</TLDR>

## Begin With a Real Reason

- “Your recommendation was excellent. What should I watch next?”
- “How did the interview go?”
- “I saw the exhibit you mentioned and thought of you.”
- “You made a strong claim about the city's best tacos. I need evidence.”
- “I liked meeting you and wanted to continue the conversation.”

Specific context gives him something easier to answer than a bare “hey,” though “hey” is not a moral failure.

## Ask and Share

Do not make him perform the whole conversation, and do not interview him. Pair a question with something from your own life:

> What got you into cooking? I finally learned one pasta dish and have been behaving as if I own a restaurant.

This creates a path in both directions.

## Flirt Clearly

- “I like talking with you.”
- “You looked very good tonight.”
- “You are becoming my favorite notification.”
- “I keep thinking about that story you told.”
- “I am attracted to you, in case I was being too subtle.”

Men do not share one secret texting psychology. Respond to the person and the relationship in front of you, not a theory about an entire gender.

## Ask Him Out

Waiting for him to initiate may preserve ambiguity, but it does not necessarily protect you from rejection. A direct invitation is efficient:

> I am enjoying this. Want to get coffee at North Street Saturday afternoon?

If he cannot make it and wants to meet, he can suggest another time. If he repeatedly avoids a plan, step back instead of optimizing the message.

## Timing and Double Texting

Reply when you have the attention to reply. Deliberately waiting an exact number of hours can make you less natural without making you more desirable.

A second message is reasonable when it adds context, corrects something, or follows up once after a good exchange. Several unanswered messages designed to force engagement are not.

## Notice Reciprocity

Look for a pattern:

- he initiates sometimes;
- he answers with substance;
- he asks about you;
- he remembers earlier details;
- he responds warmly to flirting;
- he helps make plans.

No single behavior proves attraction. If the interaction is consistently one-sided, believe the pattern rather than trying a more strategic version of yourself.

## Do Not Use These Tactics

- jealousy bait;
- fake emergencies;
- pretending to share his interests;
- sexual messages without consent;
- recruiting friends to test him;
- withholding warmth as punishment;
- claiming feelings you do not have.
