So you Googled "how many free likes on Hinge" because your like counter hit zero and you're sitting there staring at a profile you actually wanted to interact with. Classic.

The answer is **8 free likes per day**. That's your budget. Eight shots at convincing another human being you're worth their time before Hinge cuts you off like a bartender at last call. Compare that to Tinder's roughly 100 daily swipes and Bumble's 80, and Hinge starts to feel like the strict parent who only lets you have two cookies after dinner.

But here's the thing most people miss: the limit is the feature. And once you understand how it actually works, you can stop panicking about the number and start making those 8 likes do some real damage.

## How Many Free Likes Do You Get on Hinge? (The Hard Cap)

Let me spell it out one more time for the people in the back.

**8 likes per day.** Confirmed. Still the limit in 2026. Hinge has not changed this number, and if you read some blog from 2022 saying it's 10, that information is outdated. It used to be 10. Then Hinge said "nah, 8 is enough" and here we are.

The mechanics:

- **Reset time:** 4:00 AM local time, every single day
- **Rollover:** Zero. None. If you use 5 today, you get 8 tomorrow. Not 11. Not 13. Just 8. The leftover likes vanish into the void like your motivation to go to the gym on January 15th.
- **Outbound only:** The 8-like cap applies to likes you SEND. There is no daily limit on how many likes you can receive. So if 200 people want to like your profile in one day, Hinge won't stop them. (Though let's be honest, if you're reading this guide, 200 incoming likes is probably not your current problem.)

For context, this makes Hinge roughly **10-12x more restrictive** than [Tinder](/blog/tinder-vs-hinge) or Bumble at the free level. That's not accidental. Hinge wants you to actually read someone's prompts before deciding to like them. Radical concept, I know.

## When Do Hinge Free Likes Reset? (It's Not Midnight)

Your hinge free likes reset at **4:00 AM local time**. Every day. Without exception.

Not midnight. Not when you wake up. Not when you close and reopen the app (nice try). Four in the morning. If you're awake at 4 AM swiping on Hinge, you either work nights or you need to seriously reconsider some life choices. Either way, no judgment. Okay, a little judgment.

### Your Turn Limits: The Hidden Second Wall

Here's where it gets spicy. Even if you have all 8 likes sitting there ready to go, Hinge can block you from using them.

It's called **Your Turn Limits**. Hinge rolled this out in September 2024, and it works like this: if you have **8 or more unanswered conversations** where it's your turn to reply, you can't send new likes. Period. This applies to both free AND paid users. Yes, even the people dropping $49.99 a month on HingeX.

So your 8 daily likes can effectively drop to **zero** if you've been ghosting your matches. That person who sent you a thoughtful message three days ago? Respond to them or unmatch. Clear the queue.

Hinge is basically your mom telling you to finish your dinner before asking for dessert. Annoying? Sure. But you know she's right.

## The Hinge Like Limit: What 8 Likes Actually Gets You

Let's do some math that nobody asked for but everybody needs.

**8 likes per day x 30 days = 240 outbound likes per month.** That's your free-tier ceiling.

Now let's run those 240 likes through reality. Our data from thousands of real dating profiles shows average match rates on dating apps hover around **1-5% for men**, and significantly higher for women. Let's be generous and assume you're hitting 5%.

**240 likes x 5% match rate = 12 matches per month.** That's about 3 per week.

That's assuming you're using all 8 every single day. Most people don't. Life gets in the way. You open the app, send 3 likes while waiting for your burrito, and forget about it until tomorrow.

And the gender disparity? Absolutely brutal. The average guy on Hinge gets **3-5 incoming likes per week**. The average woman gets **99+ per day**. That's not a typo. That's the dating app economy, and it's about as fair as a poker game where one player starts with all the chips.

Then there's the Standouts section. Those are the [top profiles](/blog/hinge-roses) in your area, the ones Hinge considers the most popular. You can't interact with them using regular likes. You need Roses. And you get exactly **1 free Rose per week**. One. Per week. So if you're banking on Standouts to carry your dating life, I've got some bad news.

With **15 million monthly active users** on Hinge as of 2025, competition is real. Your 8 likes aren't just competing against other profiles. They're competing against the sheer volume of attention the people you're liking already receive.

## Free vs. Paid: Is Unlimited Likes Worth Your Rent Money?

Alright, let's talk about throwing money at the problem. Because that's the American way. (Works for everything except dating apps with bad photos.)

Here's what your credit card can buy:

- **Hinge+ ($29.99/month):** Unlimited likes, see all incoming likes at once (instead of one at a time), advanced preference filters
- **HingeX ($49.99/month):** Everything in Hinge+, plus Priority Likes (your likes jump to the top of the recipient's feed for 7 days) and Skip the Line placement in recommendations

These prices are "dynamic," which is corporate speak for "we charge different people different amounts." Users over 30 often see higher prices. Hinge calls this personalization. Everyone else calls it an age tax.

Only about **5% of Hinge users** actually pay for premium. That means 95% of people on the app are working with the same 8 likes you are. You're not as disadvantaged as you think.

Now for the dirty truth that no dating app wants you to hear: **unlimited likes will not fix a bad profile.** Sending 200 likes a day with photos that look like they were taken by a security camera in 2011 just means 200 people see your security camera photos. You're not scaling success. You're scaling failure.

Hinge's CEO Jackie Jantos has called the free product "sacred." The 8-like limit is intentional design, not just a paywall to squeeze subscriptions out of you. (Though it definitely also does that.)

When does upgrading actually make sense? If you're already getting decent match rates and want to scale up. If your profile converts at 5-10% and you just want more volume, [Hinge+ might be worth it](/blog/is-hinge-plus-worth-it). If you're getting zero matches on your 8 daily likes, save your money and fix your profile first. No subscription tier in the world is going to make a boring prompt about "loving adventures" suddenly attractive.

## How to Make Your 8 Free Hinge Likes Actually Count (Instead of Wasting Them)

You've got 8 bullets. Stop firing blindly into the dark.

### Always Comment. Always.

I cannot stress this enough. Hinge's own internal data shows that sending a like with a comment makes you **3x more likely to get a response** compared to a bare heart tap. Three times. That's not a marginal improvement. That's the difference between getting ignored and getting a conversation.

A plain like with no comment is the digital equivalent of a limp handshake. You walked up to someone, said nothing, nodded vaguely, and walked away. Would that work in a bar? Then why would it work here?

Respond to a specific prompt. Ask a follow-up question. Make an observation that shows you actually looked at their profile for more than 0.3 seconds. Give them something to respond TO.

### Be Ruthlessly Selective

Mass-liking (even when you only have 8) signals low standards to Hinge's algorithm. YouTube dating coaches, Reddit threads, and Hinge's own design team all agree: reducing your likes to only people you're genuinely interested in raises your inbound quality over time.

If you're sending all 8 likes to profiles you're lukewarm about just because you feel like you "should use them," you're playing yourself. The algorithm is watching.

### Time Your Likes Like a Normal Human Being

Peak activity on dating apps happens between **7-10 PM**. Sunday nights are the busiest window of the week. Sending your likes at 6 AM when everyone is asleep or commuting is like shouting into an empty room. You might feel productive. You're not.

Stack your likes for evening hours when people are actually on the app, bored, and ready to match. That's when your notification has the best chance of getting opened instead of buried.

### Open the App Every Day

Hinge's algorithm rewards consistent daily activity. If you disappear for a week and then binge-send 8 likes in a panic, you'll get worse results than someone who sends a few likes every single day. The algorithm interprets inactivity as disinterest and tanks your visibility.

Think of it like going to the gym. Consistency beats intensity. Showing up for 15 minutes daily will outperform a single 3-hour marathon session once a month. (This metaphor also applies to your actual gym attendance, by the way. Go to the gym.)

### Fix Your Profile. Seriously.

Your prompts and photos are the real bottleneck. Not the like limit. Not the algorithm. Not Mercury being in retrograde.

If your [profile looks like everyone else's](/blog/best-hinge-profiles-for-guys), you'll get results like everyone else's. And everyone else's results kind of suck. Write prompts that say something specific about you. Get photos where you actually look like a person someone would want to spend time with. Read our guide on the [best Hinge prompts](/blog/best-hinge-prompts) if you need a starting point.

Hinge's 2025 algorithm update uses deep learning and weights commented likes more heavily. This change resulted in a **"double-digit increase in matches"** across the platform. Translation: the system is literally designed to reward people who put in effort. Wild concept.

## FAQ

### How many free likes a day on Hinge?

**8.** Resets at 4:00 AM local time. No rollover. Use them or lose them.

### Do Hinge likes roll over?

No. You get a fresh 8 every day at 4 AM regardless of how many you used (or didn't use) the day before. There is no way to bank or accumulate extra likes.

### When do free likes reset on Hinge?

4:00 AM local time, every day. Not midnight. Not noon. Not "whenever you restart the app." 4 AM.

### Did Hinge decrease free likes?

Yes. The limit used to be 10 likes per day. Hinge dropped it to 8 a few years ago. It hasn't changed since, and there's no sign it's going back up.

### Can you see who likes you on Hinge for free?

Yes, but one at a time. Free users see incoming likes revealed individually as they scroll their feed. Paid users (Hinge+ and HingeX) see the entire queue of everyone who's liked them, all at once.

### How do I get more free likes on Hinge?

You don't. There's no hack, no trick, no secret settings menu. Hinge gives you 8 per day and that's it. Instead of looking for workarounds, focus on making each like count: comment on every one, be selective, and [fix your profile](/upload) so your incoming likes do the heavy lifting.
