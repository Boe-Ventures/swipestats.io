## Does Hinge Sell Gift Cards? (No, and It's Kind of Hilarious)

Let me save you some time. I spent way too long digging into this so you don't have to.

Hinge does not sell gift cards. There is no "hinge gift card" product. There is no gifting flow in the app. There is no "Send as a Gift" button hiding in a menu somewhere. I checked. Twice.

The best part? If you google "hinge gift card," the top results are from hardware stores selling gift cards that happen to feature door hinges on the packaging. Tinder is the only major dating app that actually sells gift cards. You can grab them at Target or on tinder.com. Bumble? No. Match? No. OkCupid? Absolutely not. Hinge? You already know the answer. You're reading a whole article about it.

So if you were planning to slide a Hinge premium gift card into someone's birthday envelope next to a Starbucks card, that plan is dead. But keep reading, because the workaround is stupid simple.

## How to Actually Gift a Hinge Subscription (Without Hacking Anything)

Since Hinge won't take your money directly on someone else's behalf, you need to go through the app stores. It's not elegant, but it works. Think of it like buying someone a gas station gift card because the fancy restaurant doesn't have their own. The food still tastes the same.

### For iPhone Users (Apple Gift Card)

This is the cleanest route if your gift recipient is an iPhone person.

1. **Buy an Apple Gift Card.** You can get these at basically every retail store on earth, or online at apple.com. Get one for at least $30 (enough for one month of Hinge+) or $90 if you want to be generous with 6 months.
2. **Give it to the person.** Physically, digitally, via carrier pigeon. Your call.
3. **They redeem it** to their Apple ID balance through the App Store.
4. **They open Hinge** and purchase the subscription. The app store balance covers it.

One caveat: Apple sometimes requires a backup payment method (credit card) on file even when you have gift card balance. It's annoying but it's Apple being Apple. They won't actually charge the card if there's enough balance.

### For Android Users (Google Play Gift Card)

Same concept, different ecosystem.

1. **Buy a Google Play Gift Card.** Available everywhere. Amazon, Walmart, your local bodega. Everywhere.
2. **They redeem it** to their Google account.
3. **They purchase Hinge+ or HingeX** through the Play Store.

That's it. Three steps. You're not defusing a bomb here.

### The "Just Venmo Them" Method

I'm going to be real with you. If you're close enough to someone that you're buying them a dating app subscription (think about that for a second), you're probably close enough to just send them money.

Venmo them $30 with a note that says "upgrade your love life" or "your profile needs professional help" or whatever level of roast your friendship can handle. It lacks the wrapped-gift energy of a physical card, sure. But it gets the job done and nobody has to pretend they're excited about an Apple Gift Card.

### What Hinge+ Gets You

- This is the tier most people should be on. Period.

- Everything in Hinge+

### Which Tier Should You Gift?

The **3-month Hinge+** plan at $59.99 is the sweet spot. It's enough time for someone to actually use the features, cheap enough that you won't resent them if they never open the app, and it doesn't carry the slightly aggressive energy of gifting someone six months of premium dating.

If you're debating [Hinge X vs Hinge Plus](/blog/hinge-x-vs-hinge-plus), save your money and go with Plus unless this person is treating online dating like a competitive sport.

## Is a Hinge Subscription Actually Worth Gifting? (Or Are You Wasting Your Money?)

This is the real question, isn't it. You're not just asking "can I gift this?" You're asking "should I?"

Let's look at some data, because I'm not just going to tell you to trust your gut. Your gut got you here, reading about Hinge gift cards on a Saturday. Your gut needs supervision.

### The Numbers Don't Lie (But They Might Disappoint You)

YouTube creator Jerrad Ross ran a test across all three Hinge tiers, sending **500 likes** on each. The results?

- **HingeX:** 12 matches
- **Hinge+ (paid):** 8 matches
- **Free tier:** 10 matches

Read that again. Free Hinge got more matches than Hinge+ in his test. HingeX barely edged out free. The difference? Free took about 2 months to send 500 likes because of the daily cap, while the paid tiers blasted through them in days.

So the real value of premium isn't that you'll get more matches per like. It's that you get **unlimited likes**, which means you can play the numbers game way faster. Instead of sending 8 likes a day and waiting like you're writing letters during wartime, you can send 50 in a sitting.

### The Price Comparison That'll Make You Rethink Everything

According to BMO's 2026 Date-flation Report, the average US date costs **$189**. Millennials average **$252 per date**. And **47% of singles** say dating isn't "financially worth it."

A 6-month Hinge+ subscription costs $89.99. That's less than one dinner at a mid-range restaurant in any major city. Less than one round of drinks at a rooftop bar in Manhattan (and that's before the $18 tip your date judges you for not leaving). You're essentially buying someone half a year of enhanced dating potential for the price of a single mediocre evening out.

When you frame it like that, it's actually one of the less stupid things you can spend money on.

### The Brutal Caveat

Premium won't fix bad photos. I need you to hear this. If your friend has security camera selfies as their main photo, a bio that says "just ask," and their idea of a prompt response is "I like food," no amount of Skip the Line or Enhanced Recommendations will save them. You'd be better off sitting them down and [uploading their data](/upload) to SwipeStats so they can see how bad things really are.

A Hinge subscription is a multiplier. If you multiply zero by two, you still get zero. Make sure their profile is worth multiplying first.

### The Sprint Strategy

Here's what actually works: tell your gift recipient to use the subscription as a **1-2 month sprint**, not a lifestyle. Go hard. Send tons of likes. Have conversations. Set up dates. Then cancel and go back to free.

The [Hinge matching system](/blog/hinge-algorithm) actually refreshes your profile when you return after a break. You get shown to a new batch of people. So the play is: sprint, rest, sprint. Not pay $30 a month forever while swiping on the toilet during your lunch break and calling it "putting yourself out there."

## FAQ

### Does Hinge have a gift card?

No. Hinge does not sell official gift cards and has never offered one. You'll need to use Apple or Google Play gift cards as a workaround.

### Can you buy someone a Hinge subscription?

Not directly through Hinge. Your best bet is an app store gift card for the right amount, or just sending them money. Nobody at Hinge HQ built a gifting feature, and I wouldn't hold my breath waiting for one.

### How much does a Hinge subscription cost?

[Hinge+](/blog/is-hinge-plus-worth-it) ranges from **$15 to $29.99 per month** depending on plan length. [HingeX](/blog/is-hinge-x-worth-it) ranges from **$25 to $49.99 per month**. The longer you commit, the cheaper it gets.

### Can you use a prepaid Visa card for Hinge?

Yes. Hinge accepts prepaid Visa and Mastercard gift cards for direct purchase. This is actually another gifting option if you don't want to go the app store route. Just hand them a prepaid card with enough balance loaded on it.

### Do other dating apps have gift cards?

Only Tinder sells official gift cards (available at Target and on tinder.com). Bumble, Hinge, Match, and OkCupid don't offer them. It's a weirdly untapped market, honestly. Someone at Hinge's product team is leaving money on the table.
