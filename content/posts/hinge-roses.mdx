<TLDR>

### Hinge Roses in 30 Seconds

- A Rose tells someone you are especially interested and places you at the top of their Likes You screen.
- You can send a Rose from Discover or Standouts. Standouts accepts Roses rather than ordinary Likes.
- Hinge gives members **one free Rose each week**. Free Roses do not accumulate.
- Purchased Roses remain available until used.
- A Rose changes visibility and priority; it does not guarantee that the person will match.

</TLDR>

## What Does a Hinge Rose Do?

A Rose is a higher-priority form of interest. Hinge says it appears at the top of the recipient's Likes You screen, making it difficult to miss when they review incoming interest.

The recipient still chooses whether to accept. Queue priority is not evidence of compatibility, and “impossible to miss” does not mean “guaranteed to match.”

## How to Send a Rose in Discover

1. Tap the heart on a photo or prompt answer.
2. Add an optional comment.
3. Choose the Rose icon instead of **Send Like**.

A specific comment can give the recipient something to respond to if they accept. Do not attach a generic compliment merely because the Rose feels high-stakes.

## How to Send a Rose in Standouts

1. Open Standouts with the star icon.
2. Tap the Rose on the photo or prompt you want to Like.
3. Add an optional comment.
4. Send the Rose.

Hinge describes Standouts as profiles receiving a lot of attention that still meet your preferences. A new set appears regularly, and some Standouts may later appear in Discover, but Hinge does not guarantee that they will.

## When Does the Free Hinge Rose Reset?

Hinge gives one free Rose every week. Its current help article does not promise one universal clock time for every account, so use the balance and renewal information shown in your app.

The important rule is that free Roses do **not** stack. If you do not use the current free Rose, you will still have one after the next weekly refresh—not two.

Purchased Roses are different: Hinge says they do not expire until used.

## Free Roses vs. Purchased Roses vs. HingeX Priority Likes

| Feature | Main difference |
| --- | --- |
| Free Rose | One weekly Rose; does not accumulate |
| Purchased Rose | Remains available until used |
| HingeX Priority Like | Receives earlier queue treatment while looking like a regular Like |

A Rose is visible as a stronger signal. A Priority Like looks ordinary while receiving delivery priority. Neither guarantees acceptance.

## How to Use a Rose Deliberately

Use a Rose when the profile fits your actual preferences and provides a real reason to connect. The Standouts label alone is not a reason.

Before sending:

- check Dealbreakers and relationship intentions;
- choose the exact photo or prompt that created interest;
- add a short comment only when you have something specific to say;
- avoid spending it merely because the free Rose is about to reset.

## Does a Hinge Rose Work Better?

It receives more prominent placement. That is a documented product difference. Hinge does not publish enough independent, current outcome data to promise a universal match multiplier for every user.

SwipeStats also cannot infer a Rose's causal effect from an export unless the relevant event and impression data are present. A match after a Rose is an outcome, not proof that the same pair would not have matched from a regular Like.

## FAQ

### How many free Roses do you get on Hinge?

One free Rose per week.

### Do free Hinge Roses stack?

No. An unused free Rose does not accumulate into a larger balance at the next refresh.

### Do purchased Roses expire?

Hinge says purchased Roses remain until used.

### Can you send a normal Like in Standouts?

Standouts uses Roses rather than ordinary Likes.

### Does a Rose guarantee a match?

No. It places the sender prominently in Likes You, but the recipient still decides whether to match.
