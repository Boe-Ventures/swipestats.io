## Why Your Hinge Feed Looks Like a Post-Apocalyptic Wasteland

Let's talk about why you're here instead of, you know, on a date.

Hinge uses a [Gale-Shapley algorithm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gale%E2%80%93Shapley_algorithm) combined with deep learning to decide who sees your profile and who you see. Think of it as a matchmaker that judges you silently every time you open the app. Except unlike your aunt at Thanksgiving, this matchmaker has actual data to back up its opinions.

The algorithm assigns you a desirability score based on how other people interact with your profile. Not just whether they swipe right or left. Hinge tracks "watch time," which means how long someone lingers on your profile before making a decision. If people are flicking past you faster than I scroll past LinkedIn motivational posts, your score tanks. Hard.

Here's the fun part. The algorithm then pairs you with people it thinks are in your "tier." Low score? You see low-scoring profiles. It's like being sorted into houses at Hogwarts, except every house is Hufflepuff and nobody asked to be there.

Over time, you exhaust your local pool. You've been shown to most people nearby. They've already decided they're not interested (ouch). The discover feed starts recycling the same faces like a sad Spotify playlist on repeat. **51% of users in a wikiHow poll reported getting zero matches per week.** Zero. That's not a dry spell. That's a drought.

This is the death spiral that makes people google "how to reset Hinge" at midnight. And honestly? A reset can help. But only if you understand what it does and doesn't fix.

## How to Reset Hinge with Fresh Start (The Soft Option That Actually Works)

Hinge quietly rolled out Fresh Start as a built-in feature, and it's the closest thing to a dating app mulligan you're going to get without nuking everything. Here's exactly how to do it.

### Step-by-Step: The Hinge Fresh Start

1. Open Hinge. Tap your profile icon.
2. Go to **Settings** (the gear icon).
3. Tap **Delete Account**.
4. When asked why, select **"Not happy with Hinge"** or **"Prefer not to say."** Both work. "Not satisfied" is the more reliable trigger based on user reports, but either option gets you where you need to go.
5. Hinge will offer you a **Fresh Start prompt** instead of deleting your account. Accept it.
6. That's it. Your feed resets. Two minutes. Less time than your last failed conversation lasted.

### What Gets Reset (And What Doesn't)

This is important because people expect Fresh Start to be a magic wand, and it's more like a slightly enchanted stick.

**Gets wiped clean:**
- Your swipe history. Everyone you passed on comes back into rotation.
- Your discover feed. Fresh faces. New possibilities. A reason to open the app that isn't masochism.
- Previous passes. That person you accidentally skipped at 1 AM? Back in the mix.
- Pending likes in your current city (gone forever, so if you had incoming likes you hadn't seen, tough luck).

**Stays exactly the same:**
- Your existing matches and conversations. Nobody disappears.
- Your blocks. Anyone you blocked stays blocked (small mercies).
- Your profile content. Photos, prompts, bio. All untouched.
- Your underlying desirability score. This is the part most people miss. Fresh Start does NOT reset the algorithm's opinion of you. It just clears the deck.

### The Boost Window

Dating coaches report clients going **"from zero to 10 matches within 10 minutes"** after using Fresh Start. That tracks. You get a temporary visibility boost for roughly **24-72 hours** while Hinge re-shows your profile to the local pool. It's like being the new kid at school again. Everyone's curious. For about three days. Then you're back to eating lunch alone unless you actually gave them a reason to stick around.

### The Cooldown

You can use Fresh Start roughly **every 30 days.** There's no official hard limit on total uses, but roughly once a month is the pace. Try to use it more often and the option just won't appear.

### Troubleshooting: "I Don't See the Fresh Start Option"

This happens. Don't panic (or do, I'm not your therapist). Fresh Start won't appear if:

- Your account is paused. Unpause first.
- Your account is new. You haven't been around long enough to need a reset. Give it a few weeks.
- You used it recently. Wait out the 30-day cooldown.
- You're on an old app version. Update Hinge. This isn't a Nokia 3310. Your apps need updates.

## How to Reset Hinge Algorithm Without Deleting Anything (The Stealth Approach)

Maybe you don't want to use Fresh Start. Maybe you already used it this month. Maybe you just enjoy making things harder for yourself (a personality trait that probably extends to your dating life). Whatever the reason, there are ways to reset the Hinge algorithm without touching the delete button.

### The 1km Radius Hack

This one sounds stupid. It works anyway.

1. Go to your preferences and set your distance to **1 km** (or 1 mile). Set it as a **dealbreaker.**
2. Now swipe very, very selectively. Only like profiles you're genuinely into. Send thoughtful comments. Behave like someone who has standards.
3. Gradually expand your radius over the next week or two.

What happens: you force the algorithm to re-learn your preferences from scratch within a tiny pool. It's like starting a new save file on a video game but keeping your character. The algorithm recalibrates based on your new, supposedly more refined swiping behavior.

### Other Stealth Tactics

- **Update your preferences.** Change your age range, distance, or dealbreakers. This shakes up the feed and forces Hinge to reevaluate who it shows you.
- **Be more selective with your likes.** If you've been carpet-bombing every profile with a pulse, stop. Hinge gives you [a limited number of likes per day](/blog/how-many-likes-a-day-on-hinge) for a reason. The algorithm literally watches how picky you are and adjusts accordingly.
- **Refresh your profile content.** New photos, new prompts. Hinge's 2025 algorithm update reportedly produced a **"double-digit increase in matches"** for users. Profile changes signal to the algorithm that you're active and worth redistributing.

### A Warning That Should Scare You

Repeatedly deleting and recreating your account is a red flag that triggers [shadowbans](/blog/hinge-shadowban). I've seen it happen. I've written about it. If Hinge catches on that you're gaming the system with serial account resets, you'll go from "stale feed" to "invisible profile" real fast. And invisible is worse than ugly, because at least ugly people can improve their photos.

## The Nuclear Option: Delete Everything and Start Fresh

Alright. You've tried the gentle approaches. Fresh Start gave you a temporary bump that faded faster than your New Year's resolution to go to the gym. The stealth tricks didn't move the needle. Your account has been stale for six months and you suspect you might be [shadowbanned](/blog/hinge-shadowban). Time for the nuclear option.

### When to Consider a Hard Reset

- Your account has been inactive or stale for **6+ months.**
- You suspect a shadowban (check the signs).
- Your profile needs a total overhaul and you want the new-user boost to showcase the new you.
- You got [banned](/blog/hinge-ban) and somehow got your account back but it still feels dead.

### Step-by-Step: The Full Account Delete

This is not "uninstall the app and hope for the best." That does nothing. Your profile stays active for up to 24 hours after you delete the app, then gets hidden. Not deleted. Hidden. Like your chances of finding love. You need to actually delete the account.

1. Open Hinge. Go to **Settings > Delete Account.**
2. Confirm you want to delete, not Fresh Start.
3. **Unlink your account** from Facebook, Google, or Apple ID. Go to each platform's connected apps settings and revoke Hinge's access.
4. **Wait 24-72 hours minimum.** 48-72 is safer. This gives Hinge time to process the deletion and clear some of your data from their active systems.
5. Cancel any subscriptions separately. Deleting your account does NOT cancel [Hinge Plus](/blog/is-hinge-plus-worth-it) or [Hinge X](/blog/hinge-x-vs-hinge-plus) billing. You'll still get charged. Ask me how I know.

### What You Need for the New Account

Here's where it gets annoying.

- **New phone number.** Hinge stores your old phone number. Creating a new account with the same number links you back to your old data. Get a prepaid SIM or use a Google Voice number.
- **New email.** Same reason. Fresh email, fresh start.
- **Ideally a new device.** Hinge tracks device IDs. This is the "ideally" part because buying a new phone for Hinge is genuinely insane behavior (though I've seen people do it). At minimum, log out of everything Hinge-related, clear your phone's advertising ID, and reinstall from scratch.
- **New photos.** Hinge uses **photo hash detection** that can identify reused images. Don't just re-upload the same six photos. Take new ones. Crop differently. Change the lighting. Or better yet, take actually good photos this time.

### The Face Check Problem

Hinge's selfie verification (Face Check) retains your facial geometry data for **90 days after account deletion.** If you were banned, that jumps to **2 years.** So if you delete your account, create a new one, and immediately verify your face, Hinge can connect the dots.

The play? Wait to verify. Let the 90-day window pass if you can. Or don't verify immediately on the new account. Build some activity first. Yes, [verification boosts trust](/blog/hinge-verification-not-working), but verifying too early on a post-delete account is like walking into the police station wearing a disguise. You're drawing attention to the exact thing you're trying to hide.

### The New-User Boost Window

The first **72 hours** on a fresh account are critical. Hinge floods your profile to new users to gauge interest. This is your Super Bowl. Your Met Gala. Your one shot (thanks, Eminem).

If you create the account with half-finished prompts and one blurry photo from 2021, you just wasted the most valuable visibility window you'll ever get. Have your [profile fully optimized](/blog/hinge-profile-examples) before you sign up. Not after. Before.

## Fresh Start vs Hard Reset: The Honest Comparison

Let's lay it out so you can stop overthinking this (who am I kidding, you're going to overthink it anyway).

| | **Fresh Start** | **Hard Reset** |
|---|---|---|
| **Effort** | Low (2 minutes) | High (new number, wait period, rebuild) |
| **What You Keep** | Matches, conversations, blocks | Nothing. Absolutely nothing. |
| **Boost Quality** | Moderate (24-72 hr visibility bump) | Strong (full new-user boost) |
| **Risk** | Minimal | Shadowban if Hinge detects it |
| **Best For** | Stale feed, want to see new faces | Total overhaul, suspected shadowban |
| **Frequency** | Every ~30 days | Once. Maybe twice if you're careful. |
| **Score Reset** | No (same desirability score) | Yes (but it rebuilds based on same behavior) |

The bottom line? Fresh Start is the aspirin. Hard reset is surgery. Most people need the aspirin. If you're reaching for the scalpel, make sure the patient (your profile) is actually worth saving first.

## What to Fix Before You Reset (Or You'll Be Back Here in 3 Weeks)

I need you to hear this clearly. If you reset your account and change nothing about your profile, you will end up in the exact same spot within weeks. The algorithm re-assigns a similar desirability score based on the same behavior. A reset opens a window. Your profile is what climbs through it. Or faceplants on the windowsill. Your call.

### Fix Your Photos First

Your photos are doing 90% of the work and yours are probably doing about 10% of their potential. Here's the minimum:

- **No group shots as your first photo.** She's not going to play detective to figure out which one you are. She'll just skip you entirely.
- **No sunglasses in every pic.** One outdoor shot with shades? Fine. Every photo looking like you're in witness protection? No.
- **At least one clear face shot.** Eyes visible, decent lighting, not a zoomed-in crop from a wedding photo where you can still see your cousin's elbow.
- **Show your life, not your bathroom mirror.** Activity shots. A photo of you cooking. You with friends. You doing literally anything besides holding your phone at arm's length.

Need actual examples? Check out [the best Hinge profiles](/blog/best-hinge-profiles-for-guys) and steal ideas from people who are doing it right.

### Fix Your Prompts

Generic prompts get generic results. "I'm looking for someone who doesn't take themselves too seriously" is the prompt equivalent of a plain white t-shirt. It fits everyone and excites nobody.

Be specific. Be funny. Be revealing. "I'll know it's real when you let me control the aux cord without judging my Taylor Swift phase" tells someone a hundred times more about you than "I value honesty and good vibes." If you need inspiration, read the guide on [best Hinge prompts](/blog/best-hinge-prompts) and stop writing your prompts like a corporate mission statement.

### Fix Your Swiping Habits

If you're right-swiping on everyone, the algorithm tanks your score. I said this already. I'll keep saying it until it sinks in. Be selective. Send [thoughtful openers](/blog/how-to-start-a-conversation-on-hinge) with your likes instead of just tapping the heart and praying. Hinge literally rewards users who write comments with their likes. The feature exists. Use it.

### The Pre-Reset Checklist

Before you hit that reset button (Fresh Start or nuclear), make sure you've got:

1. **6 strong photos.** Varied backgrounds, clear face, at least one full body, at least one activity shot.
2. **3 killer prompts.** Specific, funny, or thought-provoking. Ideally all three.
3. **A swiping plan.** You will be selective this time. You will send comments with your likes. You will not revert to swiping like a casino slot machine the second you see a pretty face.

The new-user boost gives you a window. Don't waste it the same way you wasted the last one.

## FAQ

### How often can you use Fresh Start on Hinge?

Roughly **every 30 days.** There's no official published limit on total lifetime uses, so you can use it monthly if you want. But if you need to reset every single month, the problem isn't your feed. It's your profile. Or your face. Probably your profile, though. Let's be optimistic.

### Does Hinge Fresh Start reset your likes?

Yes. Pending outgoing likes and incoming likes you haven't acted on get cleared. If someone liked you yesterday and you hadn't seen it yet, it's gone. Fresh Start wipes the slate, including the slate you didn't know had anything on it.

### Can you delete Hinge and start over with the same phone number?

Technically yes. Practically, your visibility will drop because Hinge links data to your phone number. You'll get a weaker new-user boost (if any) and the algorithm may reconstruct your old score faster. A new phone number is strongly recommended for a genuine hard reset.

### What happens when you delete your Hinge account?

All your data gets permanently deleted. Matches, conversations, likes, profile info. Gone. Your subscriptions do NOT cancel automatically. You need to cancel [Hinge Plus](/blog/is-hinge-plus-worth-it) or Hinge X through the App Store or Google Play separately, or you'll keep getting billed for an app you're no longer using. Which would be the most expensive ghost story of your life.

### Does resetting Hinge clear your ELO score?

Fresh Start: partially. It resets your feed and swipe history but NOT your underlying desirability score. The algorithm still has opinions about you. A hard reset: yes, technically, since it's a brand new account. But here's the catch. If you upload the same mediocre photos and swipe the same reckless way, the algorithm will reassign you a similar score within weeks. A reset changes the starting point. Your behavior determines where you end up.

### If you delete the Hinge app, does it delete your profile?

No. Deleting the app is not the same as deleting your account. Your profile stays active for up to 24 hours after you stop using the app, then gets hidden from the feed. But it's not deleted. It's just lurking in the database like an embarrassing MySpace page you forgot about. To actually delete, go through Settings > Delete Account inside the app.

### How many times can you use Fresh Start on Hinge?

There's no confirmed official cap. Users report being able to use it roughly once every 30 days indefinitely. But again. If you're resetting monthly and still not getting matches, the app isn't the problem. [Check your stats](/upload). Look at your profile objectively. Or ask a brutally honest friend, the kind who will tell you that your third prompt makes you sound like a serial killer. Everyone needs one of those friends.
