## Your Texts Are Boring (And That's Why You're Single)

Let's get something out of the way. You're not bad at dating. You're bad at texting. And in 2026, that's the same thing.

**54% of Gen Z** say they're more comfortable talking about relationship stuff over text than in person. The first date isn't coffee anymore. It's whatever you type into that little rectangle with your clumsy thumbs. If your text game reads like a DMV form, you're not getting to the coffee stage.

Here's the brutal math. A man needs to send **18 messages** on dating platforms to get a single reply. Women? Five. That's not a gap. That's a canyon. And most guys are trying to cross it by typing "hey" and hoping for the best. (Spoiler: the best never comes.)

And before you blame your face or the algorithm or Mercury being in retrograde, **95% of dating app users** say grammar and spelling matter. **68%** will left-swipe you for a typo alone. Your phone is autocorrecting "you're" to "your" and nobody's correcting your match rate back up.

I've been running [SwipeStats](/) for years now. We've analyzed **294 million swipes** and **3.14 million matches** from over 7,000 profiles. You know what the data screams? The people who get matches aren't hotter. They're just less boring over text.

So. Let's talk about how to be flirty over text without making everyone cringe. Let's fix your thumbs.

## The 7 Rules of Flirting Over Text (That Your Thumbs Need to Learn)

These aren't vibes. These aren't "trust the process" platitudes from some life coach selling a $400 webinar. These are flirting tips over text backed by research, data, and the collective suffering of millions of people who texted "wyd" and never heard back.

### Rule 1: Murder the "Hey" (Your Opener Is a Loaded Gun)

"Hey" is not an opener. "Hey" is what you say to a coworker you don't like when you pass them in the hallway. It communicates nothing except "I exist, and I'm too lazy to prove I'm worth talking to."

OkCupid's data shows that including **"you mention"** in a first message gives you a **50% higher response rate**. That means actually reading someone's profile and referencing something specific. Revolutionary concept, I know.

Interest-based openers that reference things like their favorite band, food preferences, or movie taste beat physical compliments every single time. Telling someone "you're beautiful" as an opener is the texting equivalent of a participation trophy. It means nothing because you'd say it to anyone.

And here's something that sounds made up but isn't: the words "hola" and "howdy" outperform "hi" and "hello" as [openers](/blog/tinder-openers). Being even slightly weird is better than being forgettable.

Try these instead of "hey":

1. "Your profile says you hate pineapple on pizza. I need you to defend this position immediately."
2. "I see you've been to Japan. Please rank: ramen, sushi, or the vending machines that sell everything."
3. "Your dog has better photos than most humans on this app. What's their name?"
4. "I'm going to need you to explain that third photo. Is that a sword?"
5. "We matched, which means either you have great taste or your thumb slipped. Let's find out."
6. "Your bio made me actually laugh, which is more than most stand-up specials do. The bar is low and you cleared it."

### Rule 2: Keep It Light or Keep It Moving

Nobody wants to receive an emotional deep-dive from someone they matched with six minutes ago. Flirting over text is supposed to feel like a tennis match, not a therapy intake session.

Matthew Hussey has this framework I like: "When someone hears the word 'trouble,' there's a sexiness to that." The point is that playful tension creates attraction. You want them to think "this person is fun" before they think "this person has depth." Depth comes later. Fun comes first.

If your text wouldn't make a friend smile, it's not going to make a stranger swoon.

1. "I have a feeling you're trouble. The good kind, but still."
2. "I was going to play it cool and wait to text, but I have the self-control of a golden retriever."
3. "Be honest. On a scale of 1 to 10, how competitive are you? Because I need to know what I'm getting into."
4. "I'd challenge you to a staring contest but we're not at that level yet. Rain check?"
5. "I'm going to warn you right now. I'm very good at making people laugh and very bad at being serious."

### Rule 3: Be Specific Enough to Prove You're Not a Bot

Generic compliments are the fast food of flirting. They fill the space but nobody feels good about it afterwards. "You're so pretty" gets a "thanks" and then silence. "The way you described your obsession with true crime podcasts makes me think you'd be great at solving my mysterious disappearance" gets an actual conversation.

The framework is simple. "This reminded me of you" beats "you're cute" every time. When you connect something from the real world to something about them, you're proving you actually pay attention. The Cut's analysis of successful texters found this was the single biggest differentiator between people who get responses and people who get ghosted.

1. "I just walked past a bookstore and thought of you. Which is weird because we've exchanged four messages. You've already infiltrated my brain."
2. "I saw someone walking a husky today and immediately thought about how jealous you'd be. Am I right?"
3. "That restaurant you mentioned? I looked it up. You might be a genius."
4. "I was listening to [band they mentioned] and I get it now. You win."
5. "Your take on [specific thing from their profile] has been living in my head rent-free."

### Rule 4: Ask Questions That Aren't a Police Interrogation

"What do you do for work?" is not flirting. It's LinkedIn. "Where are you from?" is not flirting. It's customs and border protection.

Open-ended, playful questions create space for someone to be interesting. Closed questions create space for someone to say "fine" and put their phone down. You want to spark imagination, not collect data.

Hypotheticals are your secret weapon. They let people be creative, funny, and revealing without feeling like they're filling out a survey.

1. "If you could teleport anywhere right now, where would you go? And you can't say 'bed.'"
2. "Controversial question: what's the most overrated food that everyone pretends to love?"
3. "You get one superpower but it has to be completely useless. What are you picking?"
4. "What's the hill you'll die on that makes everyone look at you weird?"
5. "Be honest. What's the most unhinged thing in your search history right now?"

### Rule 5: Emojis Are Seasoning, Not the Whole Damn Meal

Here's what the research says. A study published in the NIH/PMC found that **emoji use increases perceived warmth and closeness** between texters. People who use emojis get more second dates according to a PLOS ONE study. They're not just decoration. They're emotional shorthand.

But here's the catch. Cavalheiro, Prada, and Rodrigues published a study in 2024 showing that **reciprocal emoji use creates warmth, while mismatches create friction**. Translation: if they send you a message with zero emojis and you respond with six, you look unhinged. Mirror their style. If they're a one-emoji person, be a one-emoji person.

Good emoji usage looks like this:

1. "That made me laugh way too hard at work, thanks for that 😂" (one emoji, placed naturally)
2. "Honestly? I'm a little nervous to meet you. In a good way 🙈" (adds vulnerability the words alone don't carry)
3. "You're dangerous and I mean that as a compliment 🔥" (the emoji IS the punchline)
4. "Saturday works. I'll be the one looking suspiciously excited 😏" (sets a tone words alone can't)

### Rule 6: Break the Pattern Before They Break Up With You

Here's a stat that should terrify you: **99% of guys only use plain text** when messaging matches. That's according to Marni from The Wing Girl Method. You're all doing the exact same thing. Plain text, one line, maybe a question mark if you're feeling fancy.

Voice notes. GIFs. A photo of something funny you saw. A screenshot of a song you're listening to. These aren't try-hard moves. They're pattern interrupts. Your brain is wired to ignore the familiar and notice the different. Every message that looks exactly like the last 40 messages in their inbox gets treated like the last 40 messages in their inbox.

Vary the timing, the medium, and the energy. Send a voice note that's just you laughing at something. Send a photo of the worst latte art you've ever seen. Send a GIF that's so specific to your conversation that it couldn't have been sent to anyone else.

Be the notification that makes them actually want to unlock their phone.

### Rule 7: Text to Meet, Not to Marry

Texting is the bridge. It is not the destination. If you've been texting someone for three weeks and haven't met up, you don't have a connection. You have a pen pal.

A 2026 peer-reviewed study by Teichmann et al. found that **texting the next morning after a first date** signals the highest romantic interest. Waiting two days came across as "distant." Texting immediately after was perceived as slightly "needy" but still better than the two-day wait. The sweet spot? Next morning. You're welcome.

But the bigger point is this: text flirting exists to create enough interest and comfort to get to the real thing. "Let's grab coffee this Saturday" is sexier than 47 more messages about your favorite pizza toppings. At some point, you have to bridge the gap between your phone and actual human interaction.

Ask for the date. Use your words. Stop overthinking it.

## How to Flirt Over Text on Dating Apps (It's Different, Trust Me)

Flirting with someone you met at a party is one thing. Text flirting on dating apps has a completely different set of rules because you're starting from zero. No body language. No eye contact. No shared experience beyond the fact that you both thought "eh, why not" and swiped right.

**Hinge** does the heavy lifting for you. Prompts exist so you can [start a conversation on Hinge](/blog/how-to-start-a-conversation-on-hinge) without inventing a reason to talk. Reference their prompt. Disagree with their answer. Ask a follow-up. This is not hard. Yet somehow people still open with "hey."

**Tinder** gives you less to work with, which means your [opener](/blog/best-tinder-openers) needs to be sharper. Their bio and photos are your only ammunition. Reference something specific. If their profile gives you nothing, ask yourself why you swiped right in the first place.

**Bumble** puts the ball in her court first. She has to message first, which means when she does, you have exactly one chance to prove her instinct was right. Do not waste that message on "haha thanks." Match her energy, escalate gently, and steer toward an actual conversation.

The transition from in-app chat to actual texting or a phone number exchange should happen naturally. Once you've had a solid back-and-forth (think 10-15 messages, not 3), say something like "This is easier over text. Want to move this conversation?" If they're interested, they'll say yes. If they're not, no amount of in-app messaging was going to change that.

## The Text Crimes Hall of Shame (Don't Do These)

I've seen it all. I've probably done half of it. Learn from the collective humiliation.

**The Triple-Text Tornado.** You sent a message. They didn't reply. So you sent another one. And another. Stop. They didn't miss your text. Their phone works fine. They saw it. They chose not to respond. The third text doesn't make you seem persistent. It makes you seem like you own a wall full of Post-it notes with their name on it.

**The Unsolicited Autobiography.** Nobody needs a 500-word essay about your childhood at 1am. Texting is a conversation, not a confessional. Save the deep stuff for when you've actually met and can read each other's faces. Your trauma dump at midnight is not the flex you think it is.

**Grammar Crimes.** I already told you. **68% of people** swipe left over spelling errors. Proofread your texts. "Your" and "you're" are different words. "There," "their," and "they're" are three different words. If you can't tell them apart, you're not ready to flirt with anyone. You're ready for a refresher course.

**Being TOO Available.** Responding in 0.3 seconds every single time tells someone you have absolutely nothing going on. **52% of daters** judge you positively for fast responses, sure. But there's a difference between "responsive" and "sitting in the dark staring at my phone waiting for you to text back." Have a life. Or at least fake having one.

**The Breadcrumb Trail.** Texting once every four days with "haha" and expecting someone to stay interested. **44% of singles** say inconsistent communication means the connection is going nowhere. Either show up or show yourself out.

## FAQ: Quick Answers for Your Overthinking Brain

**How do you flirt over text without being too obvious?**

Use playful teasing instead of direct compliments. "You're funny" is obvious. "I hate that you made me laugh at my desk just now" is flirty without being neon-sign obvious. Let the subtext do the work. If every text screams "I LIKE YOU," you've killed the tension that makes flirting fun in the first place.

**How do you start a flirty conversation with someone new?**

Reference something specific about them. Their profile, something they said, a photo, a shared interest. Anything that proves you're talking to THEM and not copy-pasting from a template. Check our [rizz pickup lines](/blog/best-rizz-pickup-lines) if you need more inspiration, but honestly, specificity beats cleverness every time.

**What's the best time to send a flirty text?**

Evenings between 7-10pm tend to get the best response rates. People are done with work, winding down, and actually have the mental bandwidth to be charming. Morning texts work too (especially the day after a date, per the Teichmann study). Avoid 2am unless you want your intentions to be very, very clear.

**Is it different when you flirt with a guy over text vs a girl?**

The fundamentals are the same. Be specific. Be funny. Be interested. But generally, guys respond better to directness and bold energy (we're simple creatures). Women tend to prefer the build. Teasing, callback humor, showing you remember details. Both genders want to feel like you're paying attention. That part never changes.

**How do you know if someone is flirting with you over text?**

They ask follow-up questions. They use your name. They send texts that don't need to be sent (checking in, sharing random things, "this reminded me of you"). Their response time is consistent and fast. They tease you. If someone is giving you one-word answers and responding six hours later every time, they're not flirting. They're being polite. There's a painful difference.
