<TLDR>

### Send the GIF With a Sentence

> This is me pretending I am calm about seeing you Friday.

The GIF carries the reaction; the caption carries the meaning.

</TLDR>

## GIF Ideas for a New Match

Search for a reaction that fits the conversation, then add context:

- **playful wave:** “Making a slightly more animated entrance than ‘hey.’”
- **dramatic thinking:** “Considering how seriously to take your controversial prompt.”
- **impressed reaction:** “My response to the meal in your third photo.”
- **friendly applause:** “Strong opener. Credit where it is due.”
- **tiny celebration:** “We matched, and the committee is pleased.”

Avoid a celebrity reaction that could be read as mocking the person's appearance or identity.

## After a Date

- **happy dance:** “Accurate summary of my walk home.”
- **chef's kiss:** “The date—and the dessert.”
- **calendar or waiting reaction:** “Me being normal about our next plan.”
- **warm smile:** “I had a genuinely lovely time.”
- **dramatic swoon:** “Your story about the train deserved this reaction.”

If you want another date, say so. Do not make the recipient decode a clip to learn whether you enjoyed the evening.

## Good-Morning GIFs

- **sleepy character:** “Morning. Current operating capacity: 12%.”
- **coffee pouring:** “I hope your first cup arrives quickly.”
- **sunrise reaction:** “A cheerful visual from someone who is not yet cheerful.”
- **motivational entrance:** “You have the presentation today. Go be prepared and mildly intimidating.”
- **gentle wave:** “No rush to reply—just saying good morning.”

## For an Established Partner

- **air kiss:** “Saving the non-digital version for later.”
- **countdown:** “Three sleeps until I see you.”
- **proud applause:** “You handled that hard conversation beautifully.”
- **mischievous reaction:** “I have a plan for tonight. Want details?”
- **cozy scene:** “My preferred plan with you and no outside obligations.”

## When a GIF Is the Wrong Tool

Use words for:

- an apology;
- a boundary;
- defining the relationship;
- conflict;
- consent;
- changing or canceling plans;
- any message where ambiguity could hurt someone.

A funny reaction can soften tension, but it can also make the other person feel dismissed.

## Keep It Accessible

Animated images can be hard to interpret for people using screen readers or anyone unfamiliar with the source. Add a caption that describes the intended reaction.

Avoid rapid flashing or visually intense GIFs. If the platform does not support alt text, the accompanying sentence becomes even more important.

## Match the Platform

Not every dating app supports GIFs or media attachments in ordinary chat. Check the current composer rather than assuming a feature exists. If it does not, do not pressure someone off-app only to send a reaction image.

Hinge's current help material documents messages, emoji, message Likes, and Voice Notes, but not a general GIF attachment. Product capabilities can change by version and test.

## FAQ

### What is a good first flirty GIF?

A light reaction tied to a real profile detail, paired with a sentence that is easy to answer.

### Should I send a GIF without text?

Only when the meaning is already obvious between you. A caption reduces ambiguity.

### Can a GIF revive a dead chat?

It can create one new opening. If there is no response, do not keep sending reactions.

### Are sexual GIFs okay?

Only after explicit mutual consent and within platform rules. Do not surprise someone with explicit content.
