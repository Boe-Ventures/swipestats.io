<TLDR>

Dirty lines are for consenting adults in an already reciprocal, sexual conversation. Do not send explicit material as a cold opener. Ask first:

> Can I make this conversation a little less innocent?

</TLDR>

## Suggestive, Not Graphic

- “You are making it difficult to keep this conversation respectable.”
- “I have a less innocent answer, if you want it.”
- “My thoughts about you are not suitable for the group chat.”
- “You looked dangerously good tonight.”
- “I would like to continue this somewhere with fewer distractions.”
- “I keep thinking about that kiss.”
- “Tell me whether I should behave.”
- “I have an idea for our next date, and it is not entirely wholesome.”

These give the other person room to lean in, redirect, or decline.

## Playful Questions

- “More teasing or more direct?”
- “Should I tell you what I am thinking?”
- “What kind of compliment would make you blush?”
- “Do you want the polite answer or the honest one?”
- “What part of our last date should we repeat?”
- “Is this a conversation we should continue in person?”

Questions are not magic consent forms. Listen to the actual answer.

## For an Existing Partner

- “I miss the way you look at me when you know exactly what I want.”
- “I have been replaying last night.”
- “Come home when you can. I have plans for us.”
- “I want to hear what you want tonight.”
- “That message earned my full attention.”

Use language that fits your shared history. Copying an aggressive line into a relationship that has never used that tone can still feel jarring.

## When Not to Send One

Do not use a sexual line when:

- you have not established that sexual conversation is welcome;
- the other person's age is unknown;
- they are at work or in a context where the message creates risk;
- they have slowed down, changed the subject, or asked you to stop;
- a power imbalance makes refusal difficult;
- you are trying to revive an unanswered conversation through shock.

## Dating-App Boundaries

Public dating profiles are not consent to sexual messages. Tinder's current Community Guidelines prohibit sexual content on public profiles and allow it in private conversation only when everyone is comfortable. Hinge similarly emphasizes ongoing, clear consent.

If someone unmatched after a sexual message, do not find them on another platform to explain or try again.

## Protect Privacy

Assume digital messages can be saved. Do not include identifying details in intimate media, pressure anyone for images, or share screenshots with friends. Never forward intimate content without explicit permission.

## If It Lands Badly

Do not argue that it was a joke. Say:

> I misread the tone. I am sorry. I will not continue in that direction.

Then change the subject only if they want to keep talking. Otherwise, stop.
