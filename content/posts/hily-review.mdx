## What Is Hily? (And Why Is It Plastered All Over Your Snapchat?)

Hily is a dating app whose name stands for "Hey, I Like You." I know. I wish I was joking.

Founded in 2017 by Alex Pasykov and headquartered in Las Vegas (fitting, since using this app is basically gambling), Hily has racked up **31-35 million registered users** with roughly **5-6 million active** at any given time. It's the dating app equivalent of a mid-tier chain restaurant. Not terrible. Not memorable. Exists.

The app won a Red Dot Design Award in December 2025, which is like winning "Best Dressed" at a party where everyone showed up in cargo shorts. Good for them. But a design trophy doesn't mean the app actually helps you get a date.

Hily's big selling point is "AI-driven matching." The algorithm supposedly goes beyond just your face and analyzes your conversation patterns, interests, and word choice. Which sounds impressive until you remember that the AI is learning from your awkward "hey wyd" openers. Garbage in, garbage out, friend.

You've probably seen Hily ads while watching Snapchat stories or scrolling TikTok. Their ad pipeline is aggressive. Like "guy who double-texts after 30 seconds" aggressive. They target the 18-35 crowd with promises of smarter matching and fewer creeps. Half of that is true. I'll let you guess which half.

## Is Hily Legit? (The Question Everyone's Actually Googling)

Let's address the elephant in the room. Is hily legit, or is it one of those apps that exists solely to harvest your credit card info and send you messages from bots named "Jessica, 23, 2 miles away"?

Short answer: it's not a scam. But it lives in what one YouTube reviewer accurately called "the gray zone." Not magic, not fraud. Just... there.

Hily does have photo verification. You take a selfie matching a specific pose, and the app compares it to your profile pics. This actually works pretty well. Catfishing is significantly harder here than on apps that let you upload whatever stock photo you found on Google Images.

They also launched something called Consent Guard in October 2025, which uses AI to detect and block unsolicited explicit content before it reaches the recipient. **38% of Hily users had received explicit content** before this feature rolled out. So nearly four in ten people were getting surprise anatomy lessons in their DMs. Consent Guard is genuinely a good feature and one I wish every app would steal.

But here's where it gets ugly. Despite the verification, bots still slip through. Ghost matches are real. You'll match with someone, send a message, and hear nothing back for the rest of your natural life. And that **62% male to 38% female** user ratio means if you're a straight guy, you're competing with nearly two dudes for every woman on the platform. Reddit users have called Hily "the worst app for men," and while that's probably an exaggeration (have they seen Zoosk?), the frustration is understandable.

The app does have a "risk score" system that evaluates users and blocks contact if someone's score gets too high. Think of it like a credit score, but for creepiness. If the algorithm decides you're sketchy, you're done. Which is a cool concept in theory. In practice, I've seen real users complain about being flagged for absolutely nothing.

## How Hily Works (When It Actually Works)

The core mechanics are what you'd expect. See a profile. Swipe right if interested. Swipe left if not. Match if mutual. It's the same mechanic [Tinder popularized](/blog/tinder-review) a decade ago, and nobody has meaningfully improved on it since. Including Hily.

Where Hily tries to differentiate is the AI algorithm. The app claims to learn from your behavior over time. Not just who you swipe on, but how you interact. Dialogue depth, shared interests, word choice, time spent on profiles. It builds a behavioral model and supposedly serves you better matches as it learns.

Does it work? Sort of. The matches I got felt marginally more relevant than a completely random shuffle. But "marginally more relevant" is doing a lot of heavy lifting in that sentence. It's like going from a 2% match rate to a 2.3% match rate. Technically an improvement. Practically, still painful.

Hily also has a compatibility quiz with up to 40 questions (minimum 10). Plus an "Icks & Clicks" feature where you swipe on statements to indicate what attracts or repels you. It's basically a personality test dressed up in dating app clothes. Fun? Sure. A revolutionary matching breakthrough? Let's not get carried away.

There's also Stories (because every app needs to be Instagram now), live streaming (because every app needs to be Twitch now), and video calls. Available in only **19 countries**, which means if you don't live in the US, UK, or Western Europe, Hily basically doesn't exist for you. Compare that to Tinder, which works in over 190 countries. It's not even close.

## Is Hily Free? (Technically Yes. Practically? LOL.)

Is hily free to use? The marketing says yes. The reality says "lol, good luck with that."

The free tier lets you browse profiles, match with people, post stories, and watch live streams. Sounds reasonable. Here's what the free tier doesn't let you do:

- See who liked you (those profiles stay blurred, staring at you like ghosts behind frosted glass)
- Send unlimited messages (because apparently talking to your matches is a premium feature now)
- Filter by distance under 31 miles
- Actually match with people in your area (**75-80% of free user matches fall outside practical dating distance**)

Read that last one again. Three quarters of your matches will be people you can't realistically meet. That's not a free dating experience. That's a free exercise in frustration.

And then there's the free trial trap. Hily offers a 7-day free trial that auto-enrolls you into the most expensive weekly plan when it expires. You have to cancel, and you have to cancel BOTH in the app AND in your app store subscription settings. Miss either one and congratulations, you're paying for an app you thought was [free](/blog/free-dating-apps-without-payment).

## Hily Premium: What Your Money Gets You (And What It Doesn't)

Let's talk about what hily premium actually costs. Brace yourself. This is not a fun conversation.

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Weekly | $24.99/week |
| 3 Months | $74.99 ($25/month) |
| Annual | $119.99 ($10/month) |

That weekly price is genuinely unhinged. **$24.99 per week** works out to roughly $100 a month. For a dating app. With 5 million active users. You could get Tinder Platinum, [Bumble Premium](/blog/bumble-review), AND [Hinge+](/blog/hinge-review) for less than that.

Premium gets you unlimited likes, advanced filters, the ability to see who liked you, and incognito mode. Basic stuff that other apps give you at lower price points.

But wait, there's more. Even WITH a premium subscription, Hily sells add-ons. Boosts cost **$5.99/week**. Unblurring likes costs **$7.99 for 5 unblurs**. Compatibility checks are sold separately. You're paying for a subscription and then paying again for features within that subscription. It's like buying a movie ticket and then being charged extra for the screen to be in focus.

Hily requires a **10-day opt-out notice** before auto-renewal and offers **no money-back guarantee**. If you forget to cancel, that money is gone. Poof. Into the Las Vegas desert air where Hily HQ sits.

## Hily vs Tinder: The Comparison You Came Here For

Look, if you're reading a hily app review, you're probably trying to decide between this and the apps you already know. So let's do the hily vs tinder breakdown honestly.

**User base:** Hily has roughly 5-6 million active users. Tinder has over 75 million. That's not a competition. That's a minor league team challenging the Yankees. Our dataset at SwipeStats covers **7,000+ real Tinder profiles and 294 million swipes**, and even with Tinder's brutal match rates (the average guy matches around **1-2%** of the time), you're still working with a pool that's 15x larger.

**Algorithm:** Hily's AI matching is legitimately more sophisticated than Tinder's. It analyzes conversation depth and behavioral patterns, not just swipe direction. Whether that translates to better dates is debatable. But the tech is trying harder.

**Pricing:** Both apps have aggressive monetization. Tinder nickel-and-dimes you with tiers and add-ons. Hily does the same but at higher price points with fewer users to match with. At least when Tinder overcharges you, there are millions of people to overpay for.

**Match quality:** Hily claims smarter matching through AI. But a smarter algorithm working with a smaller pool still gives you fewer options than a dumber algorithm with a massive pool. Math doesn't care about your feelings.

**Why you'd pick Hily:** You're genuinely fed up with looks-only swiping and want an app that tries (emphasis on tries) to match on compatibility. You also don't mind paying a premium for that.

**Why you'd pick Tinder:** Literally everything else. More people. More locations. More established. Better odds through sheer volume. Not perfect. But it's the devil you know.

## The Stuff Hily Actually Gets Right

I've been harsh. Let me give credit where it's earned.

**Verification is solid.** The selfie-pose matching system makes catfishing genuinely difficult. I encountered fewer obviously fake profiles on Hily than on most apps I've reviewed. That matters. Nobody wants to invest three days of witty banter only to discover "she" is a 45-year-old dude in a crypto scam ring.

**Consent Guard is innovative.** Blocking unsolicited explicit content before the recipient sees it is a feature that should be industry standard. The fact that Hily built it first (October 2025) is genuinely commendable.

**The AI matching tries harder.** Most dating apps treat the algorithm like a mystery box and hope you don't ask questions. Hily at least articulates what it's doing: analyzing conversation depth, shared interests, behavioral patterns. Whether it works perfectly is debatable. That they're trying at all puts them ahead of apps that are basically just randomized slideshows of faces.

**Icks & Clicks is fun.** Swiping on statements to build a compatibility profile is a better icebreaker than most apps offer. It gives you something to talk about in your first message that isn't "hey" or "so what do you do for fun."

**The risk score system filters bad actors.** Users who get reported or exhibit sketchy patterns get flagged and eventually blocked. It's not foolproof, but it adds a layer of safety that not every app bothers with.

## The Stuff That Makes You Want to Throw Your Phone

Now the fun part. Here's why this hily review lands at 2.5 and not higher.

**The paywall is suffocating.** Hily's free tier is essentially a trailer for the paid app. Blurred likes, distance matches you can't actually visit, limited messaging. It's designed to create FOMO intense enough to make you open your wallet. And it works. Which is the point. Which is also why I'm annoyed by it.

**Ghost matches are everywhere.** Those blurred profiles in your "Likes" tab? A solid chunk of them are inactive accounts or users who swiped right six months ago and forgot Hily existed. You're paying to unlock the profiles of people who aren't coming back. It's like buying a ticket to a concert where half the band didn't show up.

**19 countries is embarrassingly limited.** Tinder works in 190+. [Bumble](/blog/bumble-review) is in 150+. Even [OkCupid](/blog/okcupid-review) covers more ground. If Hily wants to be a serious contender, they need to stop pretending the entire world is just the US, UK, and parts of Europe.

**No LGBTQ+ same-gender preferences.** In 2026. I shouldn't need to explain why this is a problem. Some reviewers, including Photofeeler, have described the app as "made by men, for men." The lack of inclusive options doesn't help that perception.

**Cancellation is deliberately confusing.** You need to cancel in TWO places: the app itself AND your app store subscription. Miss one and you keep getting charged. This is the kind of dark pattern that makes consumer protection agencies sharpen their pencils.

**Extra charges within premium.** Boosts, unblurs, compatibility checks. All sold separately even if you're already paying $25 a week. At some point, "freemium" becomes "we will extract every dollar possible from your lonely soul." Hily crossed that line and kept walking.

**Tiny X buttons on ads.** The close buttons on Hily's in-app ads are so small you'd need the precision of a brain surgeon to hit them. Accidental clicks are basically guaranteed. This is either incompetent design or intentional manipulation. Given everything else, I'll let you guess which one I think it is.

## Hily FAQ

### Is Hily a real dating app?

Yes. It's a real company, founded in 2017 by Alex Pasykov, headquartered in Las Vegas. **31 million+ registered users**. Not a scam. Just aggressively monetized.

### Who owns Hily?

Hily Corp., founded and run by Alex Pasykov. Based in Las Vegas, Nevada. They also commissioned the 2026 Dating T.R.U.T.H. Report, which produced some actually useful statistics about the state of modern dating.

### How do I cancel Hily?

Two steps. This is important, so I'm going to say it slowly. Step one: cancel in the Hily app settings. Step two: cancel in your App Store or Google Play subscription settings. Both. You need to do both. If you only do one, you'll keep getting charged. Yes, this is annoying. No, they're not going to fix it.

### Is Hily premium worth it?

For most people? No. The weekly plan at $24.99 is offensive. The annual plan at $119.99 (about $10/month) is the only tier that approaches reasonable. Even then, you're paying for access to a pool of 5 million users when apps like Tinder give you 75 million. The math doesn't math.

### Does Hily have fake profiles?

Fewer than most apps, thanks to the selfie verification system. But they're not zero. Bots still get through, ghost profiles still exist, and some of those blurred "likes" are from accounts that haven't been active since the Biden administration.

### What age group uses Hily?

Primarily **18-35**, with the average user being around **31-38**. It skews slightly older than Tinder's core demographic. The App Store gives it **4.3/5 from 285,000+ reviews**, and it has **10M+ downloads on Google Play**. Those are decent numbers. They just don't translate to a massive active user base.

### Is Hily safe?

Safer than average, honestly. Photo verification, Consent Guard, and the risk score system all contribute to a more secure experience than many competitors. But "safer than average" doesn't mean "safe." Keep your guard up. Meet in public. Tell someone where you're going. The basics still apply no matter how fancy the app's AI claims to be.
