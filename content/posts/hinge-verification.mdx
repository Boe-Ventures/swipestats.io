<TLDR>

### Hinge Verification in 30 Seconds

- Selfie Verification compares a short video selfie with the face in your profile photos.
- A successful check adds a verified badge, but it does not prove someone's name, intentions, background, or safety.
- Optional Selfie Verification and required Face Check can have different data-retention rules.
- Changing profile photos can remove the badge and require another check.
- Hinge support cannot manually verify a profile for you.

</TLDR>

## What Does Verified Mean on Hinge?

The verified badge means Hinge's system found that a live person in a video selfie matched the face in the profile photos used during the check.

That is useful, but narrow. The badge does **not** confirm a person's legal name, employment, relationship status, criminal history, or intentions. Hinge itself says verification is not a guarantee of identity or safety. Keep normal dating-safety precautions even when a profile is verified.

## How to Get Verified

Hinge's current instructions are:

1. Open the profile area from the Photo icon.
2. Tap the gray check mark below your name.
3. Continue and allow camera access.
4. Follow the prompts to record the video selfie.

You need at least one clear, front-facing profile photo where you are the only subject. Hinge recommends an unfiltered, well-lit image without sunglasses or anything cropping the face.

If the check fails, try a clear background, move closer to the camera, remove filters and sunglasses, and confirm that your current profile photos clearly show the same person. Support cannot override the result manually.

## How the Check Works

Hinge describes two technical steps:

- a **liveness check** intended to detect whether a real person is recording the video; and
- **3D face authentication**, which compares facial geometry from the video selfie with profile photos.

The result is a match decision and, when successful, a badge on the profile. Your video selfie is not added to the dating profile.

## Optional Selfie Verification vs. Required Face Check

Hinge says Selfie Verification is voluntary in many places. In some regions it instead requires a biometric Face Check. The required version can also be used to detect whether other accounts are using the same person's likeness.

Availability and legal requirements vary by location, so the screen shown inside the app is more reliable than an old country list. Hinge also operates separate age-assurance checks in some jurisdictions; those should not be treated as identical to the optional badge process.

## What Data Does Hinge Keep?

For optional Selfie Verification, Hinge says:

- the underlying facial-geometry template is usually deleted after the process, within 24 hours;
- the video selfie is not retained after processing;
- three screenshots, the verification result, and an age estimate can be retained for the life of the account, then handled under its privacy policy.

For required Face Check regions, Hinge says facial-geometry data can be retained for the life of the account plus the applicable period after closure. That is a meaningful difference. Read the notice presented in your region before consenting rather than assuming every verification flow has the same retention policy.

## Why Did My Verification Disappear?

Verification is based on the photos present at the time of the check. If you replace them, especially the clear face photo used for comparison, Hinge may remove the badge. Update the photos and run the verification flow again.

A missing badge does not by itself mean the account is fake or restricted. It may simply mean the profile changed or the verification needs to be repeated.

## Is Hinge Verification Worth Doing?

It can reduce one point of uncertainty for someone reviewing the profile. It is not a substitute for a complete, specific profile or thoughtful conversation.

If you choose to verify, do it because the limited trust signal is useful and you accept the stated data practices—not because a badge is proven to increase matches. Hinge does not publish a current universal conversion lift that should be promised to every user.

## FAQ

### Does the badge prove a profile is safe?

No. It shows that a live face matched the profile photos during the check. Hinge explicitly says it is not a safety guarantee.

### Can Hinge support verify me manually?

No. Hinge says its support team cannot manually verify a photo on a user's behalf.

### Does Hinge keep the video selfie?

Hinge says the video is not retained after the optional verification process, but it keeps three screenshots and other results under its stated retention policy. Required Face Check has different rules.

### Can changing photos remove verification?

Yes. Hinge says a photo change may cause the badge to disappear, after which you can try the check again.
