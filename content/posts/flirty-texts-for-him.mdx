## The Science of Why His Heart Rate Spikes When You Text

You want flirty texts for him. I get it. Before we hand you 101 of them to copy like it's an open-book exam (it is, and you're welcome), let's talk about what's actually happening in his brain when your name lights up his screen.

**Dopamine doesn't fire when he reads your text. It fires before he reads it.** Dr. Danielle Forshee, Psy.D. at PsychoWellness Center, explains that the dopamine system activates during anticipation of a reward, not the reward itself. That little buzz in his pocket? His brain just pulled the lever on a neurological slot machine. The same variable-reward mechanism that keeps people feeding quarters into machines at 3 AM in Vegas is the same one making him fumble for his phone when he sees your name.

So your flirty text isn't just cute. It's pharmacological.

A **2026 study by Teichmann et al. in the Journal of Social and Personal Relationships** found that text timing has a U-shaped effect on attraction. Reply too fast and you look desperate. Too slow and you look disinterested. The sweet spot creates maximum anticipation without triggering "oh she's just not that into me" panic. Basically, you want to be the restaurant that's hard to get a reservation at. Not the one that seats you immediately because nobody else wants to eat there.

Here's where it gets interesting for this specific article. **Luo & Tuney's research in Computers in Human Behavior** found that positive texts improve relationship satisfaction, but only when written in the sender's own voice. Copy-paste jobs from a listicle? The brain can tell. (Yes, I see the irony of telling you this in a listicle. Use these as inspiration, not scripture. Tweak them. Make them yours. Otherwise his subconscious will file you under "generic" and I'll have written 3,500 words for nothing.)

One more thing. A **PubMed study of 400 college students** found that men text to relax and feel in control, not primarily for pleasure. Which means the flirty texts that work best on guys aren't the ones that put pressure on them. They're the ones that make him feel cool, wanted, and low-stakes all at once. Less "we need to talk about us" and more "I had a weird dream about you and a trampoline." Keep it light. He'll come to you.

## 3 Rules Before You Hit Send (That 99% of Women Ignore)

I've been on the receiving end of every type of text imaginable. The good, the unhinged, the ones that are clearly screenshots from a Reddit thread. Before you fire off a single message from this list, burn these three rules into your brain.

### Rule 1: Give Value, Not "Hey"

"Hey what are you up to?" tells a guy two things about you: you're bored, and you're boring. At the same time. In six words. Impressive, honestly.

Matthew Hussey hammers this point and he's right. Every text you send should bring something to the table. A funny observation. A callback to something he said. A photo of something absurd you just saw. "Hey" brings nothing. It's the text equivalent of showing up to a potluck empty-handed and then asking what everyone else brought.

If you struggle with [opening lines on dating apps](/blog/best-hinge-openers), the same principle applies to texting. Lead with value or don't lead at all.

### Rule 2: Kill the Question Marks

"I'm checking out this new Thai place on Saturday, you should come" beats "Do you wanna hang out this weekend maybe?" every single time.

Questions invite rejection. Statements invite participation. Confidence is attractive regardless of gender, and nothing says "I know what I want" like removing the question mark from your invitation. You're not asking permission. You're opening a door and letting him decide if he's smart enough to walk through it.

### Rule 3: Standards with a Wink

The most powerful flirty text a woman can send isn't dirty. It's this: "I'm not really a one-hour time slot kind of girl, but if you want to take me on a proper date this week, I'd be into that."

That text does three things at once. It sets a standard (you expect effort). It's playful (not scolding). And it tells him exactly what to do next (plan a date). Most guys are drowning in vague signals. Give him clear ones wrapped in charm and he'll swim toward you like his life depends on it.

## When to Send What (Your Flirty Texting Cheat Sheet)

Not all texts are created equal and not all moments deserve the same energy. Sending a naughty text at 8 AM on a Monday when he's in a team meeting is chaos. Sending an innocent one at 11 PM on a Friday is a missed opportunity. Here's your roadmap.

**Early Dating (1-3 dates in):**
- Stick to innocent and funny texts. You're building rapport, not writing fanfiction about him.
- One good morning text per week max. More than that at this stage and you'll trip the "too much too soon" alarm.
- Let him wonder about you a little. Mystery is your best accessory right now.

**Established (you've had the "what are we" talk):**
- Bold, naughty, and seductive questions are all fair game.
- Morning and goodnight texts can become a daily ritual without being weird.
- This is where the real fun starts. You've earned the right to be shameless.

**Long Distance:**
- Morning and goodnight texts are non-negotiable. They're your daily connective tissue.
- Naughty texts fill the physical gap. Don't be shy about it. He's far away and probably miserable.
- Seductive questions keep the anticipation alive for the next time you see each other.

**After a Fight:**
- NOT the naughty ones. Obviously. "We need to talk about what happened" followed by "what are you wearing" is a level of emotional whiplash that no relationship can survive.
- Go innocent or funny. A disarming text after tension shows emotional maturity. "I'm still annoyed but I also miss your face, so there's that." If you need inspiration for how to break tension with words, the same principles from [conversation starters on Hinge](/blog/how-to-start-a-conversation-on-hinge) apply here. Lead with something that invites warmth, not escalation.

## FAQ

### How do I flirt over text without being too obvious?

Use implication instead of declaration. "I had a dream about you" says everything without saying anything specific. "You looked really good last night" is a compliment, not a marriage proposal. (If you're reading this FAQ before reading the actual article, congratulations on your commitment to doing things backwards. Scroll up. The good stuff is up there.) The trick is to say something that could be interpreted as flirty or friendly, then let the context do the work. If he's into you, he'll read it as flirty. If he's not, you have plausible deniability. It's the texting equivalent of a poker face.

### What's the "10-second text" that makes him smile for hours?

The callback. Reference something specific from a previous conversation that nobody else would know. "Just walked past that pizza place you said changed your life. The line was insane. Maybe you were onto something." That text takes 10 seconds to write and it tells him: I listen, I remember, you matter. Most women underestimate how rarely men feel truly seen. A callback text fixes that.

### How often should you text a guy you like?

Match his energy, then add 10%. If he texts you twice a day, three is fine. If he texts you once every two days, daily texts will feel like a siege. You're going for Nora Ephron rom-com, not Glenn Close in Fatal Attraction. The research backs this up: Luo & Tuney found that positive texts only boost satisfaction when they feel organic and unsolicited. The moment texting becomes an obligation it stops being attractive. You want him to see your name and feel excited, not exhausted.

### What texts do guys actually like receiving?

In my experience (and I say this as a guy who has received his fair share of both incredible and horrifying texts), guys respond best to texts that are: specific (not generic), low-pressure (not demanding a response), slightly bold (a little forward goes a long way), and funny (humor disarms everything). The worst text a guy can receive is one that feels like homework. "How was your day?" every single day at 6 PM is not a conversation. It's a timesheet. Give him variety. Surprise him. Be the text he screenshot-sends to his group chat with the caption "bro look at this girl."
