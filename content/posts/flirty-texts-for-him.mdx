<TLDR>

### The Best Flirty Text Is Specific and True

> You were very attractive explaining something you clearly care about. I would like a second demonstration over drinks.

</TLDR>

## For a New Match

- “Your profile made the Like easy. Which question should I ask first?”
- “You seem interesting, and I am choosing directness over a mysterious emoji.”
- “Your answer about tiny museums was excellent. Want to compare favorites?”
- “I was going to play it cool, but your third photo disrupted the plan.”
- “You have my attention. What are you looking forward to this week?”

## After a Good Date

- “I had a genuinely good time. I would like to see you again.”
- “You looked very good tonight, in case my face was too subtle.”
- “Still laughing about the story you told. When is part two?”
- “Home safe and already considering our next plan.”
- “The conversation was my favorite part, and the food was strong competition.”

Stating interest is more useful than waiting a prescribed number of hours to appear unavailable.

## Playful Texts

- “Important question: how much of your confidence is earned?”
- “I have a date idea. Your role is to be charming and arrive on time.”
- “You are becoming a very enjoyable distraction.”
- “I am willing to admit your recommendation was correct. Do not misuse this power.”
- “Choose our low-stakes rivalry before I choose for us.”

Teasing should target a shared joke or harmless preference, not an insecurity.

## Direct Texts

- “I like you.”
- “I want to kiss you the next time I see you. How does that sound?”
- “I am attracted to you and enjoying getting to know you.”
- “Want to come with me to the exhibit Saturday?”
- “I would rather see you than continue this by text all week.”

Directness includes room for the other person's answer. A desire is not a demand.

## Long-Distance Texts

- “Different time zones, same person on my mind.”
- “I miss the ordinary version of being near you.”
- “Three sleeps until I can flirt in person.”
- “Tell me one small detail from your day when you have time.”
- “I saved a story because I wanted your reaction live.”

## Established Relationship Texts

- “I still get excited when I know I am seeing you later.”
- “You handled today with a lot of care. That is very attractive.”
- “I have plans for us tonight. Interested?”
- “Thank you for making ordinary life feel like ours.”
- “I miss your face and several other things.”

Attraction is not only appearance. Appreciation, competence, kindness, humor, and shared history can all be genuinely flirty.

## Escalate With Consent

Before sending explicit text or images, ask:

> I want to make this conversation more suggestive. Are you in that mood too?

Enthusiastic agreement matters. Silence, a vague reaction, or past flirting is not blanket permission for every future message.

## Do Not Text to Manipulate

Avoid:

- artificial delays designed to create anxiety;
- jealousy bait;
- insults labeled as banter;
- repeated follow-ups after no reply;
- pretending to share an interest;
- testing him with a message whose “correct” answer is hidden.

Flirting should make the connection easier to understand, not harder.

## FAQ

### Do men prefer direct texts?

People vary. Direct, respectful language reduces guessing, but no preference applies to all men.

### How often should I flirt by text?

Match the relationship's rhythm. Quality and responsiveness matter more than a quota.

### What if he replies slowly?

A delay alone does not prove interest or disinterest. Look at the broader pattern and ask about communication preferences.

### Can I send a sexual text first?

Ask whether that tone is welcome before making the message explicit.
