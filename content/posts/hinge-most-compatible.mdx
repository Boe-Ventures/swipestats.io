## What Is "Most Compatible" on Hinge? (The Purple Tile You Keep Ignoring)

Every day, Hinge puts a purple tile at the top of your Discover feed. One profile. One suggestion. One shot. That's your Most Compatible.

How does Hinge determine Most Compatible? Short version: Nobel Prize math combined with AI that's been watching your every tap, skip, and 2 a.m. doom-scroll since you downloaded the app.

Hinge claims you're **8x more likely to go on a date** with your Most Compatible than with any random profile in your feed. Eight times. That's not a marginal improvement. That's the difference between "maybe someday" and "Friday night."

The catch? It expires in 24 hours. Don't act on it and it vanishes like your self-esteem after a week of no matches. There's no archive, no second chances. Hinge is basically running a dating flash sale and you keep sleeping through it.

## How Does Hinge Determine Most Compatible? (Nobel Prize Math Meets Your DMs)

The backbone of Most Compatible is the **Gale-Shapley stable matching algorithm**. Two economists invented it in 1962. It won a Nobel Prize in 2012. And now it's deciding whether you go on a date this weekend.

Here's why this matters: unlike [Tinder's old ELO system](/blog/tinder-algorithm) that ranked everyone on a single attractiveness ladder (congrats, you're a 6), Gale-Shapley finds **mutual fits**. It's not asking "who's the hottest?" It's asking "who actually wants each other?"

The algorithm cycles through proposals. Person A proposes to their top choice. Person B either accepts or rejects based on their own preferences. The rejected go to their next choice. This repeats until every pair is stable. No one can improve by switching. It's brutally efficient. Like a bouncer who knows exactly who belongs together at the party.

On top of Gale-Shapley, Hinge layers **collaborative filtering**. Same tech Netflix uses to recommend shows. "Users who liked Profile A also liked Profile B." Except instead of binge-watching Tiger King, you're binge-swiping on people who will never text you back.

For the full deep dive on how the [Hinge algorithm](/blog/hinge-algorithm) works under the hood, I wrote a separate breakdown. But Most Compatible is the algorithm's crown jewel. It's where all the math converges into one daily suggestion.

## The Three Signals Hinge's AI Is Watching

The algorithm doesn't just pull names out of a hat. It's tracking three categories of signals, and yes, it's judging you harder than your friend who's "brutally honest" after two glasses of wine.

### Profile Signals (Your Photos Are Being Graded)

Everything visible on your profile feeds the machine:

- **Photos.** All six slots (you ARE using all six, right?). Hinge's AI analyzes composition, faces, activities. If your lead photo is a blurry group shot from 2019, the algorithm knows you're not serious.
- **Prompts.** Your three prompt answers tell the AI what kind of person you're trying to attract. Generic prompts ("I love to laugh") attract generic matches. Funny, specific prompts attract people with actual personalities.
- **Basics.** Age, location, job title, education. The AI uses these for initial filtering before the heavier math kicks in.
- **Dealbreakers and preferences.** Distance, height, religion, politics, kids. These are hard filters. If someone's outside your dealbreakers, they'll never show up as Most Compatible. Period.

### Intent Signals (Yes, It Knows You Mass-Like)

This is where most people screw themselves. The algorithm watches HOW you use the app, not just that you use it.

- **Who you like and who you skip.** Hinge builds a preference model from your behavior. Like a bunch of brunettes? More brunettes incoming. Skip everyone over 30? Noted.
- **Whether you send comments with likes.** Sending a comment gets you [roughly 40% more matches](/blog/best-hinge-openers). The algorithm treats commented likes as higher-intent signals. It's the difference between waving at someone and actually walking over to talk.
- **Speed of swiping.** Blow through 50 profiles in 3 minutes and the algorithm flags you as a mass-liker. Your visibility tanks. Spend time on profiles and the AI assumes you're being thoughtful (even if you're just reading their prompts out of boredom).
- **Roses and Standouts.** Using roses and engaging with [Standouts](/blog/hinge-standouts) sends strong preference signals. It tells the algorithm "I really want THIS type of person."

### Outcome Signals (Did You Actually Go On a Date?)

This is Hinge's secret weapon and what separates it from every other dating app. Hinge tracks what happens AFTER the match.

- **Like-to-match ratio.** If people you like aren't liking you back, the algorithm adjusts your suggestions. Harsh but fair.
- **Do conversations actually happen?** Matching and then never messaging teaches the algorithm that something's off. Either you're not interested or you're too scared to talk. Both are data points.
- **The "We Met" feature.** Hinge asks both people if they actually met up and how it went. This is the real gold. Real-world dating outcomes feed directly back into the algorithm. If your dates keep reporting positive experiences, your Most Compatible suggestions get better. If they report bad ones, well. You get what you deserve.

I'll be honest. When I first learned Hinge was using post-date feedback to train its algorithm, I was equal parts impressed and terrified. It's like your dating history has a Yelp page you can't see.

## Is Most Compatible on Hinge Shown to Both Users? (The Mutual Question)

Yes. Most Compatible is designed to be **mutual**. When you see someone as your Most Compatible, they see you as theirs too.

But there's a timing wrinkle. You won't necessarily see each other on the same day. It depends on when each person opens the app, their activity patterns, and their filter settings. You might get someone as your Most Compatible on Tuesday while they got you on Thursday.

The 24-hour expiry makes this matter. If you don't act on your Most Compatible within a day, it's gone. No takebacks. No "let me think about it." Hinge treats hesitation the same way the algorithm treats mass-liking. As a signal that you're not serious.

And if your [Most Compatible disappears](/blog/hinge-match-disappeared) mid-day? They probably deleted their account, paused their profile, or changed their settings so you no longer fit their filters. It's not a glitch. It's just life.

## What Your Most Compatible Says About You (The Uncomfortable Mirror)

Here's the part nobody wants to hear. Your Most Compatible is a diagnostic tool.

If your daily Most Compatible suggestions are consistently people you're not attracted to, or people who seem "below your level" (yes, I know how that sounds, but we're all thinking it), that is not the algorithm being broken. That is the algorithm telling you how it perceives your profile.

Most Compatible pairs people of similar "desirability" based on the signals above. If the algorithm thinks you're a catch, it pairs you with other catches. If it thinks your profile is mid, you get mid suggestions. It's a mirror, and mirrors don't lie.

This is actually useful information if you're not too fragile to act on it.

**What to do if your Most Compatible sucks:**

- **Upgrade your photos.** Get a friend with a decent camera. Or invest in a dating photographer. Your bathroom selfie from 2021 is costing you dates.
- **Rewrite your [prompts](/blog/best-hinge-prompts).** Be specific, be funny, be yourself. "I love tacos" is not a personality.
- **Stop mass-liking.** Every time you blanket-like 30 profiles, the algorithm drops your perceived value. Be choosy. Like people you'd actually want to date.
- **Engage meaningfully.** Send comments. Reply to messages. Use the app like a person, not a bot.

Think of it this way: if you walked into a bar and the bartender kept introducing you to people you weren't interested in, you wouldn't blame the bartender. You'd ask "what vibe am I giving off?"

## Does Hinge Use AI? What Changed in 2025-2026

Short answer: yes. Longer answer: Hinge went from "using AI" to "being an AI company that happens to make a dating app."

Here's the timeline:

- **January 2025:** **AI prompt feedback** started giving users suggestions on how to improve their prompt answers before posting.
- **March 2025:** An **AI-powered comment filter** began screening incoming messages for quality and appropriateness.
- **November 2025:** Hinge rolled out a **deep learning algorithm update** that replaced their previous matching system. The result? Double-digit increases in match rates across the platform. Not a small tweak. A full engine swap.
- **December 2025:** **AI Convo Starters** launched. The AI scans the other person's profile and suggests three icebreaker messages. It's like having a witty friend whispering in your ear, except the friend is a language model and it never gets tired.
- **December 2025:** Hinge founder Justin McLeod left to start Overtone, an AI-first dating app. When the guy who built Hinge thinks AI dating needs its own company, pay attention.

Match Group (Hinge's parent company) invested **$20-30 million in AI development** for 2025 alone. That's not "experimenting with AI." That's betting the company on it.

What does this mean for your Most Compatible? The suggestions you're getting today are significantly smarter than what you got even six months ago. The algorithm has more data, better models, and a deeper understanding of what makes two people actually want to meet.

Whether that's comforting or dystopian depends on your relationship with technology. Personally, I'd rather have a Nobel Prize algorithm pick my dates than my drunk friends. But that's just me.

## How to Get Better Most Compatible Matches (Fix Your Feed)

You can't hack Most Compatible. But you can give the algorithm better data to work with. Think of it like filing your taxes. Garbage in, garbage out.

1. **Complete your profile. All of it.** Six photos. Three prompts. Every basic field filled in. An incomplete profile tells the algorithm you're not committed. The algorithm responds by not committing to showing you to good matches. Fair is fair.

2. **Stop mass-liking.** I've said it three times now and I'll say it again because most of you won't listen. [Being selective](/blog/how-to-use-hinge) with your likes is the single biggest lever you have. The algorithm watches your like-to-skip ratio. If you like 90% of profiles, Hinge assumes you have no standards and treats your likes as low-value.

3. **Send comments with your likes.** Sending a comment with a like instead of a bare heart gets you roughly **40% more matches**. It also tells the algorithm you're a high-intent user, which improves your visibility and your Most Compatible quality.

4. **Use the app daily but don't doom-scroll.** 30-60 minutes of focused usage beats three hours of mindless swiping. Open the app, review your Most Compatible, send a few thoughtful likes, respond to your messages, close the app. That's it. The algorithm rewards consistency over marathon sessions.

5. **Actually reply to messages.** Matching and ghosting teaches the algorithm that your matches aren't working. Reply to people. Have conversations. Even short ones. The data feeds back into your suggestions.

6. **Use "We Met" honestly.** After you've been chatting with someone, Hinge asks if you met up and how it went. Answer honestly. This is the most powerful feedback loop in the entire app. It literally trains the AI on what a good match looks like for you.

7. **Write a [bio](/blog/hinge-bio) that doesn't make people cringe.** Your prompts and bio are the difference between someone sending a like with a comment and someone skipping past you faster than you skip your Most Compatible. Put effort in.

## FAQ

### How does Hinge Most Compatible work?

Hinge uses the Gale-Shapley stable matching algorithm (the one that won a Nobel Prize) combined with machine learning. It analyzes your profile, behavior, preferences, and post-match outcomes to find one person per day who's the best mutual fit for you. Both users see each other as Most Compatible.

### Is Hinge Most Compatible based on attractiveness?

Not directly. There's no single "attractiveness score." Most Compatible is based on mutual compatibility signals: your preferences, behavior patterns, dealbreakers, and how similar users have interacted. That said, if you only like conventionally attractive profiles, the algorithm learns that preference and factors it in.

### When does Hinge Most Compatible reset?

You get a new Most Compatible every day, typically when you first open the app. The previous one expires after 24 hours. There's no way to refresh it early or get a second one (unless you're on Hinge X, which offers additional premium features).

### Why did my Most Compatible disappear on Hinge?

Three likely reasons: the 24-hour window expired, the other person deleted or paused their account, or they changed their preferences so you no longer match their filters. It's not a glitch. It's just the system working as designed.

### Is Hinge Most Compatible mutual?

Yes. When you see someone as your Most Compatible, they also see you as theirs. The timing might differ (you might see them on Monday, they see you on Wednesday), but the pairing itself is mutual.
