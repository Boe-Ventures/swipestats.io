<TLDR>

### Most Compatible in 30 Seconds

- Most Compatible is a recommendation that may appear at the top of Discover.
- Hinge says it tries to provide one new suggestion per day when available.
- The current help page names mutual Dealbreakers, recent activity, and shared Like patterns as inputs.
- The suggestion expires after 24 hours.
- It is a prediction, not proof of attraction, compatibility, or a future date.

</TLDR>

## What Is Hinge Most Compatible?

Most Compatible is a profile Hinge predicts you are likely to connect with. When available, it appears at the top of the Discover feed and expires after 24 hours.

The label does not mean the other person has already liked you, agreed to a date, or passed a private compatibility test. You still decide whether to send a Like, and the other person still decides whether to match.

## How Does Hinge Choose It?

Hinge's current help page names three broad inputs:

- **mutual Dealbreakers** — the non-negotiable preference filters each person has set;
- **recent activity** — whether profiles are currently participating on the app; and
- **shared Like patterns** — patterns in whom you and other people tend to like.

Hinge also says its recommendations use information supplied directly by members, activity generated while using the service, and information from other users. It does not publish the full model, exact weight of each signal, or a score users can inspect.

That means claims about a secret attractiveness rank, swipe-speed penalty, or guaranteed behavior boost go beyond the documented facts.

## Is It Based on Gale-Shapley?

Hinge described the original Most Compatible launch as using a form of the Gale-Shapley matching approach. That history explains why older coverage calls it “Nobel Prize math.”

It does not reveal the exact current production system. Algorithms evolve, and Hinge's present help page describes the user-facing inputs rather than promising that one published formula controls every recommendation. Treat Gale-Shapley as product history, not a complete reverse-engineering guide.

## Is Most Compatible Mutual?

Do not assume the label proves that both people see the same tile at the same moment. Hinge's current public help page describes a recommendation shown to you, but does not promise reciprocal display in its user instructions.

What matters operationally is simpler: if you are interested, send a thoughtful Like before the recommendation expires. The normal matching rules still apply.

## What If the Recommendation Feels Wrong?

One suggestion is a noisy signal, not a diagnosis of your profile or dating life. Check:

- whether age, distance, dating intentions, and other preferences are current;
- which preferences are marked as Dealbreakers;
- whether location is set correctly;
- whether your own Like behavior reflects what you actually want.

Then keep evaluating profiles on their contents. A label should not override an incompatibility you can see, and a profile without the label is not a worse match by definition.

## Most Compatible vs. Standouts

| Feature | Hinge's description | Action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Most Compatible | A recommended profile based on Dealbreakers, activity, and Like patterns | Send an ordinary Like from Discover |
| Standouts | Profiles receiving attention that meet your preferences | Send a Rose from the Standouts feed |

Neither surface guarantees a response. Standouts reflects attention and uses Roses; Most Compatible is a single recommendation inside Discover.

## How to Use the Recommendation Well

1. Read the full profile, not just the label.
2. Confirm important intentions and Dealbreakers.
3. Like the specific photo or prompt that genuinely interested you.
4. Add a short comment when there is a natural question.
5. Skip it when the profile is not a fit.

There is no reason to send a Like merely because the tile expires. Scarcity is not compatibility.

## FAQ

### How often do you get Most Compatible?

Hinge says it tries to send one new recommendation per day, when available.

### How long does it last?

The current suggestion expires after 24 hours.

### Does Most Compatible mean they already liked me?

No. The recommendation still uses the ordinary Like and match process.

### Can I find an expired recommendation again?

Hinge does not document a Most Compatible archive. The profile could appear elsewhere later, but there is no promise that it will.
