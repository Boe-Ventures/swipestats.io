## Is Hinge Actually Good? (Cut the Crap, Give Me the Answer)

Yes. If you want a relationship, Hinge is good. If you want a serious relationship, Hinge is probably the [best dating app](/blog/best-dating-apps) available right now. There, I said it. You can close this tab now if that's all you needed.

Still here? Good. Let's talk about why.

**35% of married couples who met on dating apps used Hinge**, according to The Knot's 2023 study. Tinder? 25%. Bumble? 20%. That's not a small gap. That's Hinge lapping the competition at its own game.

Hinge pulled in **$691 million in revenue in 2025**, up 26% year over year. It's the only Match Group app that's actually growing. Tinder is hemorrhaging users. Bumble lost 16% of its paying subscribers. Hinge is the cockroach that survived the nuclear winter of dating app fatigue. (I mean that as a compliment. Mostly.)

**89% of Hinge users say they're looking for serious relationships.** Compare that to Tinder, where a LendEDU poll found 44% of users admitted they're swiping purely for confidence boosting. Almost half of Tinder is just people fishing for validation. On Hinge, at least most people are pretending to want something real.

But here's the thing. "Is Hinge a good dating app" is the wrong question. The right question is: good for what? If you're looking for hookups, Hinge will bore you to tears. Go to Tinder. Nobody's judging you. (Okay, we're judging you a little.)

## The Numbers Behind the Hype (Because Your Opinion Doesn't Count)

I love opinions. Everybody has one. They're also worthless without data. So let's look at what the numbers actually say about Hinge.

- **32 million users globally**, with **1.9 million paying subscribers.** Not Tinder-sized, but Hinge doesn't need to be. Quality over quantity. Something your Tinder swiping strategy could learn from.
- Revenue went from **$8 million in 2018 to $691 million in 2025.** That's not growth. That's a SpaceX launch.
- **90% of users say their first dates went well.** 72% wanted a second date. This comes from Hinge's own "We Met" feedback data, so take it with a grain of salt, but even discounting for corporate optimism those numbers are impressive.
- **Gender ratio: 60/40 male to female.** Sounds skewed? Compare it to Tinder's 78/22 sausage fest. If you're a guy on Tinder, you're competing with roughly four other dudes for every woman. On Hinge, it's closer to 1.5. Your odds just got a lot better. Math doesn't care about your feelings.
- **Core age demographic: 25-34** makes up 31.5% of users. If you're in that range and living in a city, Hinge is basically built for you.

Now for the ugly part. Trustpilot gives Hinge a **1.1 out of 5 stars.** The BBB customer rating sits at **1.09 out of 5.** That's not a typo. Hinge's customer service is so bad it makes your cable company look like the Ritz-Carlton. If you get wrongly banned (and it happens), you're basically screaming into the void.

At SwipeStats, we've analyzed data from [over 7,000 dating profiles](/upload) across Tinder and Hinge. What we consistently see is that Hinge users who put effort into their profiles get dramatically better results than those who phone it in. Shocking revelation, I know.

## Hinge vs Tinder vs Bumble (Let the Data Fight It Out)

Enough with the solo evaluation. Let's put these three in a cage and see who walks out. Here's a [Tinder vs Hinge](/blog/tinder-vs-hinge) vs Bumble breakdown with actual data, not vibes.

| | **Hinge** | **Tinder** | **Bumble** |
|---|---|---|---|
| **User Intent** | 89% serious | 44% use for confidence boosting | 55% serious |
| **Gender Ratio** | 60/40 M/F | 78/22 M/F | 46/54 M/F |
| **Revenue Trend** | +26% YoY | Declining | -10% |
| **Free Daily Likes** | 10 | ~50 | Unlimited |
| **Profile Depth** | High (6 photos + 3 prompts) | Low | Medium |
| **First Message** | Either party | Either party | Women first |
| **Marriage Share** | 35% | 25% | 20% |

A few things jump out here.

Tinder is becoming a self-esteem app, not a dating app. A study found that **71% of college Tinder users have never met a single match in person.** Seventy-one percent. That's not a dating platform. That's a dopamine slot machine with better photos.

Bumble's "women message first" gimmick sounded revolutionary in 2015. In 2026, it's resulted in an avalanche of "hey" messages and a platform that's losing paying users at an alarming rate. The novelty wore off faster than your New Year's resolution to hit the gym.

Hinge is the one growing because the product actually works. It forces effort through prompts and limited likes. It penalizes lazy swiping through its algorithm. And it collects post-date feedback to improve matches over time. It's the dating app equivalent of that friend who tells you to put down the phone and go talk to her already.

## Who Hinge Works For (And Who It'll Eat Alive)

Let me save you some time and heartbreak.

### Hinge is great for:

- **25-35 year olds** who are past the "let's see what happens" phase and actually want to find someone
- **Relationship seekers** who are tired of matching with people who ghost after two messages
- **Women**, since the 60/40 ratio means you're not drowning in a sea of desperate dudes (just wading in a puddle of them)
- **City dwellers** where the user pool is big enough to sustain your increasingly specific dealbreakers
- **People who actually fill out their profiles.** Three thoughtful prompts and six good photos will put you in the top 20% of users. The bar is underground and people still trip over it.

### Hinge is NOT great for:

- **Hookup seekers.** Go to Tinder. Hinge doesn't want you and neither do the people on it. (Fine, some of them do, but they won't admit it.)
- **Rural area residents.** If your town has one traffic light, your Hinge pool is going to be about as deep as a parking lot puddle.
- **People over 50.** Only 12% of users are 45+. You'll have options, but not many. Silver Singles exists for a reason.
- **Lazy profile builders.** This is the big one. The [Hinge algorithm](/blog/hinge-algorithm) is unforgiving. Unlike Tinder, you can't right-swipe 500 profiles a day and pray for a miracle. Hinge shows your profile to fewer people if it detects you're low-effort. Think of Hinge like a job interview and Tinder like a career fair. At the career fair, you hand your resume to everyone with a pulse. At the interview, you better have prepared.

## What Hinge Gets Right (That Other Apps Fumble)

Credit where credit is due. Hinge nails several things that its competitors keep screwing up.

**Prompt-based profiles.** No more blank bios or "ask me anything" copouts. You pick three prompts and answer them, which means even the most conversationally bankrupt person has something to comment on. It's training wheels for human interaction. We need them. Looking at you, guy whose entire Tinder bio is his height and a fish emoji.

**Free messaging with likes.** On Tinder, messaging someone before matching costs money. On Hinge, you can send a comment with every like for free. You can actually say something specific about someone's profile before they decide to engage. Wild concept, right?

**Free location changes.** Tinder and Bumble both charge for this through premium subscriptions. Hinge just lets you do it. Moving to a new city? Check out the dating pool before you arrive. No paywall required.

**"Designed to be deleted" isn't just marketing BS.** The "We Met" feedback loop actually exists. After you exchange numbers with someone, Hinge asks if you went on a date and how it went. That data feeds back into the algorithm. It's the only dating app that genuinely benefits from you leaving.

**10 likes per day sounds limiting but it's actually genius.** It forces you to be selective. It prevents the "right-swipe-on-everyone" strategy that turns Tinder into a wasteland. When you only have 10 shots, you aim them at profiles you actually care about. And that leads to better matches.

## What Hinge Gets Wrong (And Yeah, It's Not Minor)

I'm not here to write a press release for Hinge. The app has real problems, and pretending otherwise would make this blog about as useful as a screen door on a submarine.

**Pricing will make your wallet weep.** Hinge+ runs about $30/month. HingeX is roughly $50/month. For a dating app. That's a gym membership. That's three months of a streaming service. And if you're over 30, dynamic pricing might charge you even more, because apparently aging is a premium feature now. (Check our full breakdown on [Hinge subscription](/blog/hinge-subscription) tiers.)

**Customer service is a dumpster fire.** I mentioned the 1.09/5 BBB rating. That's not a rounding error. If you get banned, wrongly flagged, or experience a technical issue, you are essentially on your own. Hinge support responds with the urgency of a DMV employee on a Friday afternoon.

**Roses and Standouts are a paywall for attractive people.** The Standouts section showcases the most popular profiles. But you can only "like" them with Roses, which you get one of per week for free. Want more? Pay up. It's dating's class system in app form, and it's about as subtle as a brick through a window.

**The algorithm is a black box.** You don't know why you're seeing who you're seeing. You don't know why your match rate dropped. You don't know if you've been soft-penalized for something. It just happens. If you suspect a [Hinge shadowban](/blog/hinge-shadowban), good luck confirming it.

**Smaller user pool in less populated areas.** This is the reality for any app that isn't Tinder. If you live somewhere with fewer than 100,000 people, your options will be limited. And you'll start seeing the same profiles recycled faster than a sitcom on basic cable.

## Is Hinge Free? (And What Does "Free" Actually Get You?)

Yes, the basic version of Hinge is free and genuinely usable. Unlike some apps (looking at you, eHarmony, charging people money just to read their own messages), Hinge gives free users enough to actually find a date.

Here's what each tier gets you:

| | **Free** | **Hinge+ (~$30/mo)** | **HingeX (~$50/mo)** |
|---|---|---|---|
| Daily Likes | 10 | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| See Who Liked You | No | Yes | Yes |
| Advanced Filters | Basic | Yes | Yes |
| Message with Likes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Location Changes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Priority Placement | No | No | Yes |
| Enhanced Recs | No | No | Yes |

The free tier is honestly solid. 10 likes per day, basic filters, messaging with your likes, and free location changes. You can absolutely find a relationship without paying a dime. Millions of people do.

But if you want to see who already liked you (instead of guessing from the blurred thumbnails like some kind of dating app Rorschach test), you'll need Hinge+. And if you want the algorithm to actually prioritize showing your profile to people, HingeX is the move. [Is Hinge Plus worth it?](/blog/is-hinge-plus-worth-it) Depends on how much your time is worth to you.

One thing to know: Hinge uses dynamic pricing. Users over 30 may pay more. Users in bigger cities may pay more. It's the dating app version of surge pricing. Romantic.

## Your Burning Hinge Questions, Answered

### Does Hinge actually work?

Yes. The data is pretty clear on this. 35% marriage share among app-matched couples. 90% positive first date ratings from "We Met" feedback. Revenue growing while competitors shrink. It works. The question is whether YOU work on it. A half-assed Hinge profile will get half-assed results. Or, more accurately, no results.

### Is Hinge better than Tinder?

For relationships? Absolutely. It's not even close. For hookups or casual swiping? No. Tinder still has the user base and the culture for that. Read our full Hinge vs Tinder comparison if you want the detailed breakdown. The short version: Hinge is a restaurant. Tinder is a vending machine. Both serve food. One is considerably more satisfying.

### Is Hinge better than Bumble?

In 2026? Yes. Bumble's "women message first" feature hasn't aged well, the company is losing paying users, and the [Hinge vs Bumble](/blog/hinge-vs-bumble) gap keeps widening. Bumble still has a slightly better gender ratio (46/54), so if you're a guy purely playing the numbers game, it's worth having both installed.

### Is Hinge good for guys?

Better than Tinder, worse than Bumble on raw gender ratio. But Hinge rewards effort more than any other app. If you write good prompts, choose strong photos, and send thoughtful comments with your likes, you'll outperform 80% of the guys phoning it in with "hey." Our guide on how Hinge works for guys goes deeper on strategy.

### Is Hinge good for people over 40 or 50?

It exists for you, but the pickings are slim. Only 12% of users are 45+. You'll find some matches, especially in major cities, but if you're in a smaller market the pool dries up fast. You might want to supplement with apps that skew older.

### Is Hinge free to message?

Yes. You can send a message (comment) with every like, completely free. No paywall. No premium requirement. This is one of Hinge's biggest advantages over Tinder, where messaging before matching is a paid feature. Use it. A thoughtful comment on someone's prompt is worth ten generic right-swipes.
