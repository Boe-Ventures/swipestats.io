## When Do Hinge Roses Reset? (The Answer Is Sunday. You're Welcome.)

Hinge roses reset every Sunday. One free rose per week. Every user gets it. Free users, Hinge+ subscribers, HingeX members. Doesn't matter. One rose, every Sunday, use it or lose it.

Think of roses as Hinge's version of a Super Like. You send one to a profile and it rockets you to the top of their Likes You queue. It's the dating app equivalent of cutting to the front of the line at a club, except the bouncer is an algorithm and the club is full of people judging your prompts.

Here's what you need to know about how often you get roses on Hinge:

- **Free users:** 1 rose per week, resets Sunday
- **Hinge+ ($29.99/mo):** 1 free rose per week, plus additional roses included
- **HingeX ($49.99/mo):** 1 free rose per week, plus more additional roses
- **Purchased roses:** Never expire. They sit in your inventory like that gym membership you keep "meaning to use"

No rollover. If you sit on your free rose all week and let Sunday hit without sending it, it's gone. Poof. Like your confidence after checking your Likes You tab and seeing zero.

## What Time on Sunday Do Roses Reset? (Nobody Actually Knows)

Here's where things get annoying. Hinge doesn't publish the exact reset time. They just say "Sunday." Thanks, Hinge. Super helpful.

Based on user reports and some aggressive Reddit detective work, the consensus is that roses reset around **4 AM local time on Sunday**. That lines up with when [Hinge likes reset](/blog/when-do-hinge-likes-reset) too.

But the timezone situation creates real confusion. Some users report getting their rose Saturday night. Others don't see it until Sunday afternoon. Reddit debates this with the intensity of people arguing about whether a hot dog is a sandwich.

My practical advice: check Sunday morning. If your rose is there, great. If it's not, check again in a few hours. Don't lose sleep over it. You're losing enough sleep swiping at 2 AM.

## How Roses Actually Work (And Why Hinge Only Gives You One)

Roses do two things. First, they move you straight to the top of someone's Likes You queue. Second, they're the **only way** to interact with Standouts profiles.

That second part is where Hinge's monetization gets clever. Bordering on evil, actually.

Hinge shows you roughly **70 Standouts per week**. These are the profiles the [Hinge algorithm](/blog/hinge-algorithm) has decided are the most attractive and compatible with you. Seventy profiles. And they give you one free rose to interact with them.

One. Out of seventy. That's a 1.4% hit rate. Hinge is essentially showing you a candy store, handing you a single nickel, and pointing at the $5 chocolate bars. Then putting a "Buy More Roses" button right where your thumb naturally rests.

But here's the thing. Roses actually work.

Hinge's own data shows roses are **2x more effective** than regular likes. The CEO, Justin McLeod, confirmed this in a Fortune interview. And if you add a thoughtful comment to your rose? You're **3x more likely** to get a response compared to a naked like.

That last stat matters. A rose without a comment is just a louder version of saying nothing. You spent the currency. You got their attention. Then you stood there silently like a mannequin at a speed dating event. Write something. Something specific to their profile. Something that shows you have a pulse and a personality.

## When to Send Your Rose (And When to Keep It in Your Pocket)

Not every profile deserves your rose. I know that sounds harsh, but you get one per week (unless you're buying extras). Be strategic about it.

### Send it when:

- You have a genuine "hell yes" reaction to the profile. Not a "they're cute I guess." An actual visceral response.
- You have a personalized comment ready. Something about their prompts. Something specific. "Nice smile :)" doesn't count.
- It's Saturday night and your rose is about to expire. Better to send it than waste it.
- The person is a Standout you can't reach any other way.

### Keep it when:

- The profile gives you nothing to comment on. Three photos, no prompts, no personality. Don't reward low effort with your highest-value currency.
- You're sending a Hail Mary to someone who probably gets 200 likes a day. Be realistic.
- Your own profile needs work. Sending a rose from a half-assed profile is like showing up to a job interview in sweatpants. Fix your [Hinge bio](/blog/hinge-bio) first.
- You can only think of a generic comment. "You seem cool" is not a comment. It's a diagnosis of brain death.

**Best send time:** Sunday or Monday evening, **7 to 10 PM**. That's peak Hinge activity. **64% of Hinge users are male and 36% are female**, which means competition is stiff (as always). Timing your rose during high-activity hours maximizes the chance she actually sees it before it gets buried.

And remember what we've seen across [over 7,000 profiles](/upload): **selective swipers get 2.7x higher match rates**. The same principle applies to roses. Being picky isn't a bug. It's the whole strategy.

## Hinge Rose Jail: Yes, It's a Real Thing

You've probably seen people complaining about "Hinge rose jail" online. It actually refers to two different problems that people mash together.

**Problem 1: Your feed went stale.** The algorithm ran out of quality profiles to show you, so you're seeing the same faces on repeat or getting nothing good. This isn't technically about roses, but people call it "rose jail" because they feel stuck.

**Problem 2: The Standouts paywall.** Hinge shows you 70 incredible profiles per week in Standouts but only gives you one free rose to interact with them. The rest cost money. That's the jail. The walls are made of paywalls.

CEO Justin McLeod has been surprisingly transparent about this. He's said "We don't really have an attractiveness score" when asked about the algorithm, which... sure, Justin. The algorithm uses Gale-Shapley matching, which is literally a Nobel Prize-winning system for ranking preferences. But okay. No attractiveness score. Just "compatibility."

He's also defended the one-rose limit: "If everyone had unlimited Roses, then that Rose wouldn't mean anything anymore." And honestly? He's not wrong. Scarcity is what makes the rose mean something. If everyone got 50 free roses a week, it'd just be another like button.

### The Fresh Start Hack

If your feed feels genuinely dead, there's a nuclear option. Go to **Settings > Delete Account > select "Prefer not to say"** when asked why. Hinge will offer you a **Fresh Start** that resets your algorithm without nuking your matches.

This [resets your algorithm](/blog/how-to-reset-hinge) and gives you a new pool of profiles while preserving your existing matches and conversations. Your discovery feed starts fresh but nothing else gets nuked. Use it as a last resort, not a weekly ritual.

## FAQ

### Do roses expire on Hinge?

Free roses: yes. Use it or lose it each week. Sunday to Sunday. If you don't send it, it resets and you get a fresh one. Purchased roses: never expire. They'll sit in your account until you use them or Hinge gets acquired by Meta (knock on wood).

### Is sending a rose on Hinge weird?

No. Sending a rose with zero comment? That's weird. It's like sliding someone a $20 bill at a bar and then walking away without saying a word. The rose gets their attention. The comment is where you actually make an impression. Skip the comment and you've wasted your one weekly shot.

### How often do Standouts refresh?

Daily. There's a visible countdown timer in the Standouts section showing exactly when the next batch arrives. You'll see roughly **70 Standouts per week**, refreshed in daily batches.

### Do roses carry over if I don't use them?

No. Your free weekly rose does not roll over. It resets every Sunday whether you used it or not. If you want roses that stick around, you have to buy them.

### Can you send a rose to anyone or just Standouts?

Both. Standouts **require** a rose. That's the only way to interact with them. But you can also send a rose to anyone in your regular Discover feed as an optional power move. Think of it like the difference between a mandatory toll road and choosing to tip your barista. One is required. The other just shows you care.

### How many free roses do you get on Hinge?

One. Per week. For all users regardless of subscription tier. Hinge+ and HingeX get additional roses as part of their subscription, but the base free rose is the same for everyone. The average guy on Hinge gets about **3 to 5 likes per week**, so making that one rose count matters.
